# Child Marriage

## Definition
Child marriage is the marriage of a person below the legally prescribed minimum age: 21 years for males and 18 years for females under the Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2006. It is prohibited as a social evil linked to denial of education, health risks (especially to girls), and exploitation.

## Current Law

### Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2006 (PCMA)
- § 2(a) — "Child" means a male below 21 years and a female below 18 years
- § 3 — Child marriage voidable (not void): the contracting child may repudiate the marriage within 2 years of attaining majority (i.e., male before 23, female before 20); court shall grant divorce on such petition and make ancillary orders for maintenance and return of valuables
- § 5 — Marriage solemnised in contravention of any injunction under PCMA is void
- § 9 — Adult male (≥18 years) who contracts a child marriage: simple imprisonment up to 2 years or fine up to ₹1 lakh or both
- § 10 — Person performing, conducting, directing, or abetting a child marriage (e.g., priest, parent, guardian): simple imprisonment up to 2 years or fine up to ₹1 lakh or both; parent or guardian who permits or negligently fails to prevent — same punishment
- § 12 — Void marriages: a child marriage is void (not merely voidable) where the child is taken or enticed away from lawful guardian, or compelled by force or fraud, or is sold / trafficked for marriage, or is married to facilitate trafficking
- § 13 — Child Marriage Prohibition Officers (CMPO): appointed by State Governments; power to prevent marriages, enter premises, apply for injunctions
- § 14 — Courts may issue injunctions to prevent imminent child marriages; violation of injunction is punishable

### Proposed PCMA Amendment Bill 2021
- Proposes raising the minimum age of marriage for females from 18 to 21 years (equating it with males), bringing India in line with a Jaya Jaitly Committee recommendation
- Bill referred to Parliamentary Standing Committee; as of 2026 not yet enacted; therefore current minimum age for females remains 18

### Interaction with Personal Laws
- Several High Courts (Rajasthan, Gujarat, Allahabad) have held that Muslim personal law governs the age of marriage for Muslim parties and that PCMA may not override personal law; this question has not been definitively settled by the Supreme Court
- Independent Thought v Union of India (SC 2017) addressed an adjacent issue: the court read down Exception 2 to IPC § 375 (now BNS § 63 Exception 2) — which previously excluded marital rape of a wife aged 15-18 from the definition of rape — holding that sexual intercourse with a wife below 18 is rape regardless of marriage. This ruling specifically addressed the rape-within-marriage issue and did not hold that the underlying child marriage is void
- HAMA § 5(iii) and HMA § 5(iii): marriage of a girl below 18 or boy below 21 is a voidable marriage under HMA; grounds for divorce or annulment

### IPC / BNS Provisions
- BNS § 63 (formerly IPC § 375) — rape; Exception 2 as modified post-Independent Thought: sexual intercourse with own wife below 18 is not excluded from rape
- PCSO Act 2012 — sexual offences against a child (below 18) are prosecuted under POCSO even if parties are married; marriage does not provide immunity

## Key Provisions

### Voidable vs Void Child Marriages
| Category | When | Who Can Void It |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| Voidable (§ 3) | Standard child marriage not involving force/fraud/trafficking | The child alone, within 2 years of majority |
| Void (§ 12) | Child trafficked, sold, forced, or enticed away | Treated as void ab initio; no petition needed |
| Injunction (§§ 13-14) | Imminent marriage about to be solemnised | CMPO or any person on behalf of child |

### Offences and Penalties under PCMA 2006
| Offence | Section | Penalty |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Adult male (≥18) marrying a child | § 9 | 2 years SI or ₹1 lakh fine or both |
| Conducting / facilitating child marriage | § 10 | 2 years SI or ₹1 lakh fine or both |
| Permitting child marriage (parent / guardian) | § 10 | 2 years SI or ₹1 lakh fine or both |
| Violating injunction under § 13 | § 15 | 2 years RI or ₹1 lakh fine or both |

All offences under PCMA are cognisable (police may arrest without warrant) and non-bailable.

### Ancillary Relief on Voidance
When a child marriage is voidable and the child petitions under § 3:
- Maintenance for the female petitioner from her former husband
- Residence order if needed
- Return of gifts, ornaments, and money given at marriage (§ 4)
- If the female was pregnant at the time of decree, provisions for the child's legitimacy and maintenance
