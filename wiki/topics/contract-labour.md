# Contract Labour

## Definition
Contract labour refers to workmen supplied by a contractor to a principal employer; the Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970 (CLRA) regulates this arrangement by requiring registration and licensing, imposing welfare obligations, and empowering governments to prohibit contract labour in perennial or core activities.

## Current Law

### Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970

- § 2(e) — "contractor": person who undertakes to produce a given result through contract labour, or supplies contract labour for any work; includes sub-contractor
- § 2(g) — "principal employer": owner or occupier of an establishment, or head of a government department, where contract labour is deployed
- § 7 — registration of establishments: every principal employer of an establishment employing ≥20 contract workers must be registered with the registering officer; penalty for non-registration
- § 12 — licensing of contractors: every contractor employing ≥20 workmen must obtain a licence; licence specifies number of workmen, nature of work, and conditions
- § 10 — prohibition of contract labour: appropriate government (central/state) may, after consulting the Central/State Advisory Board, prohibit employment of contract labour in any process/operation/other work in any establishment; factors considered — perennial nature, done by regular workmen in similar establishments, sufficient to employ a workman full-time
- § 16 — canteen: where ≥100 contract workers employed and work likely to continue ≥6 months, contractor must provide canteen
- § 17 — rest rooms: where contract workers required to halt at night, rest rooms with prescribed facilities
- § 18 — other welfare facilities: sufficient supply of wholesome drinking water, latrines/urinals, washing facilities, first-aid
- § 19 — creches: where ≥20 women contract workers employed, contractor to provide crèche
- § 21 — responsibility for payment of wages: contractor primarily liable; if contractor fails, principal employer must pay wages and recover from contractor; principal employer to nominate a representative to be present during wage disbursement
- CLRA (Central) Rules 1971: detailed procedure for registration, licensing, welfare obligations, inspection

## Key Provisions

### Registration and Licensing Requirements

| Requirement | Who | Threshold | Authority |
|-------------|-----|-----------|-----------|
| Registration (§ 7) | Principal employer | ≥ 20 contract workers | Registering Officer |
| Licence (§ 12) | Contractor | ≥ 20 workmen deployed | Licensing Officer |
| Renewal | Contractor | Annually or as specified | Licensing Officer |

### Welfare Obligations

| Facility | Who is Liable | Trigger |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| Canteen (§ 16) | Contractor (PE if contractor fails) | ≥ 100 workers + work continuing ≥ 6 months |
| Rest rooms (§ 17) | Contractor | Workers required to halt at night |
| Drinking water, latrines, washing (§ 18) | Contractor | All contract labour engagements |
| Crèche (§ 19) | Contractor | ≥ 20 women contract workers |
| Wages (§ 21) | Contractor primarily; PE secondarily | All engagements |

### Prohibition of Contract Labour (§ 10)

- Central/state government may issue notifications prohibiting contract labour in specific operations
- Relevant factors: perennial nature of work, whether similar work done by regular workmen elsewhere, whether work is incidental or necessary to the main work
- Key Supreme Court ruling — **Steel Authority of India Ltd v National Union Waterfront Workers (2001)**: abolition of contract labour under § 10 does not automatically lead to absorption of contract workmen into principal employer's regular workforce; absorption requires a specific provision in the notification or a settlement/award

### Principal Employer's Vicarious Liability

- If contractor fails to pay wages within prescribed period, principal employer must pay wages and is entitled to recover from contractor (§ 21(4))
- If contractor fails to provide welfare amenities, principal employer is required to provide them (§§ 20, 21)
- Non-registration of establishment is an offence; no contract labour can be engaged in an unregistered establishment
