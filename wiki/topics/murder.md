## Definition

Murder is the unlawful killing of a person with the intention to cause death, or with knowledge that the act is so imminently dangerous that it will in all probability cause death. It is the most serious homicide offence under Indian criminal law.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 101** — Culpable homicide (lesser offence; death caused without full intent)
- **§ 103** — Murder (punishment: death or life imprisonment + fine)
- **§ 104** — Culpable homicide not amounting to murder (punishment: imprisonment up to 10 years, or life)
- **§ 105** — Death by negligence (punishment: up to 5 years + fine)

## Key Provisions

**Elements of murder (§ 103):**
1. Act done with intention of causing death, **or**
2. Act done with intention of causing bodily injury likely to cause death, **or**
3. Act done with knowledge it is imminently dangerous and will probably cause death

**Exceptions that reduce murder to culpable homicide (§ 101 exceptions):**
- Grave and sudden provocation
- Private defence (exceeded in good faith)
- Public servant acting in good faith
- Sudden fight without premeditation
- Consent of victim (age 18+, in good faith)

**Punishment:**
- Murder (§ 103): Death **or** life imprisonment, with fine
- Culpable homicide not amounting to murder (§ 104): Life imprisonment **or** up to 10 years + fine

## Transition Note

Previously governed by **Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)**:
- IPC § 299 → BNS § 101 (culpable homicide)
- IPC § 300 → BNS § 100 (definition of murder)
- IPC § 302 → BNS § 103 (punishment for murder)
- IPC § 304 → BNS § 104 (culpable homicide not amounting to murder)

Section numbers changed; substantive provisions substantially the same. BNS also introduced § 103(2) specifically for mob lynching — group killing on grounds of race, caste, community, sex, place of birth, language, or personal belief — carrying death or life imprisonment.
