## Definition

A contract is a legally enforceable agreement. Under Indian law, an agreement becomes a contract when it is made by free consent of parties competent to contract, for a lawful consideration and a lawful object. Contracts form the foundation of all commercial and many personal legal relationships in India.

## Current Law

**Indian Contract Act, 1872 (ICA)** — still in force, unrepealed.

- **§ 2** — Definitions (offer, acceptance, promise, agreement, contract, voidable contract, void agreement)
- **§ 10** — What agreements are contracts (the core provision)
- **§ 11** — Who is competent to contract (age, soundness of mind, not disqualified by law)
- **§ 13–22** — Consent (free consent, coercion, undue influence, fraud, misrepresentation, mistake)
- **§ 23** — Lawful consideration and object
- **§ 25** — Agreements without consideration are void (with exceptions)
- **§ 56** — Doctrine of frustration (agreement void if performance becomes impossible)
- **§ 73** — Compensation for breach (damages: direct loss + indirect loss flowing naturally)
- **§ 74** — Liquidated damages and penalty (reasonable compensation, not penalty)

## Key Provisions

**Essentials of a valid contract (§ 10):**
1. Offer and acceptance (agreement)
2. Free consent (§ 14: not by coercion, undue influence, fraud, misrepresentation, or mistake)
3. Competent parties (§ 11: major + sound mind + not legally disqualified)
4. Lawful consideration (something in return — money, service, forbearance)
5. Lawful object (not forbidden by law, not immoral, not against public policy)
6. Not expressly declared void

**Void vs voidable:**
- *Void agreement*: No legal effect from the beginning (e.g., agreement with minor, agreement in restraint of trade)
- *Voidable contract*: Valid until avoided by the aggrieved party (e.g., contract under coercion or fraud)

**Breach and remedies:**
- Damages (§ 73): Direct + consequential losses; duty to mitigate
- Specific performance: Available under Specific Relief Act, 1963 when damages are inadequate
- Injunction: To restrain breach of negative covenants
- Quantum meruit: Reasonable compensation for partial performance

**Frustration (§ 56):** Contract becomes void when performance is impossible due to events not foreseen at formation (destruction of subject matter, supervening illegality, death of personal service contractor). Note: economic hardship alone is not frustration.
