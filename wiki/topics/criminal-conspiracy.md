## Definition

Criminal conspiracy is an agreement between two or more persons to do, or cause to be done, an illegal act or a legal act by illegal means. Unlike most offences, the conspiracy itself — the agreement — is the offence; the intended act need not be completed.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 61(1)** — Definition: agreement between two or more persons to do/cause to be done an illegal act, or a legal act by illegal means
- **§ 61(2)** — Punishment: where the conspiracy is to commit an offence punishable with death, life imprisonment, or rigorous imprisonment ≥ 2 years — same punishment as the offence itself; in other cases, imprisonment up to 6 months + fine, or fine only
- **§ 61(3)** — Parties need not know each other or be in direct contact; each conspirator is liable for acts of co-conspirators done in furtherance of the common object

## Key Provisions

**Elements of criminal conspiracy:**
1. Agreement between two or more persons
2. Agreement to: (a) do an illegal act, OR (b) do a legal act by illegal means
3. At least one overt act in furtherance (not required for the agreement offence itself, but relevant for evidence)

**Illegal act (§ 2(y) BNS):** An act punishable by law, or which is prohibited by law. Includes civil wrongs if they are also criminally prohibited.

**Agreement is the gist:** The moment the agreement is reached, the offence is complete — no overt act needed. This distinguishes conspiracy from attempt.

**Meeting of minds required:** Mere knowledge of another's plan is not conspiracy. There must be a common design that both parties subscribe to.

**Two-person rule:** Acquittal of all co-accused does not automatically entitle the remaining accused to acquittal — courts assess each accused's involvement separately.

**Conspiracy + substantive offence:** Conspirators are typically charged both for conspiracy (§ 61) and the substantive offence (e.g., murder § 103). If the substantive offence is committed, the conspiracy charge merges for sentencing purposes.

**Abetment vs conspiracy:**

| Feature | Abetment (§ 45) | Criminal conspiracy (§ 61) |
|---------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Parties | Abettor + principal | Two or more co-conspirators |
| Agreement | Not essential | Essential |
| Completion of act | Abettor can be liable even if act not done | Conspiracy complete at agreement |
| Both parties | Need not agree | Must agree |

## Transition Note

Previously governed by **Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)**:
- IPC § 120A → BNS § 61(1) (criminal conspiracy defined)
- IPC § 120B → BNS § 61(2) (punishment for criminal conspiracy)

Substantive law unchanged. BNS consolidates into a single section (§ 61) versus IPC's two sections.
