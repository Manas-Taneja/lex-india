## Definition

Panchayati Raj is India's system of local self-government in rural areas, given constitutional status by the 73rd Amendment (1992), which inserted Part IX (Articles 243–243O) into the Constitution. It creates a three-tier structure of elected local bodies at village, intermediate, and district levels.

## Current Law

**Constitution of India, Part IX — Articles 243–243O** (inserted by 73rd Amendment, 1992)

**Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996 (PESA)**

- **Article 243** — Definitions (Gram Sabha, intermediate level, panchayat area, etc.)
- **Article 243A** — Gram Sabha: body of persons registered as voters in a village
- **Article 243B** — Constitution of Panchayats: three-tier structure (village, intermediate, district); States with population ≤ 20 lakh may not have intermediate tier
- **Article 243C** — Composition of Panchayats; seats filled by direct election
- **Article 243D** — Reservation of seats: not less than 1/3 reserved for women; reservation for SC/ST proportionate to population; States may provide reservation for OBCs
- **Article 243E** — Duration: 5-year term; fresh elections must be completed before expiry or within 6 months of dissolution
- **Article 243F** — Disqualification for membership
- **Article 243G** — Powers and functions: Legislature may endow panchayats with powers for self-governance, including subjects in Eleventh Schedule
- **Article 243H** — Taxes, duties, tolls — Legislature may authorise panchayats to levy
- **Article 243I** — Finance Commission: State Finance Commission every 5 years to review finances
- **Article 243J** — Audit of accounts
- **Article 243K** — State Election Commission: superintendence of panchayat elections
- **Article 243L** — Application to Union Territories
- **Article 243M** — Part IX does not apply to Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram, certain hill areas, and Scheduled Areas (Fifth Schedule) — unless Parliament extends it
- **Article 243N** — Continuance of existing laws
- **Article 243O** — Bar to interference in electoral matters by courts

**Eleventh Schedule (Article 243G) — 29 subjects including:**
Agriculture, land improvement, minor irrigation, animal husbandry, fisheries, rural housing, drinking water, fuel and fodder, roads, rural electrification, poverty alleviation, primary and secondary education, markets and fairs, health and sanitation, family welfare, women and child development, social welfare, libraries, cultural activities.

## PESA, 1996

Extends Part IX to Fifth Schedule (tribal) areas in 10 States (Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Himachal Pradesh, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha, Rajasthan, Telangana) with modifications protecting tribal customs:

- Gram Sabha has powers to approve development plans and control resources
- State legislation must be compatible with tribal customary law and traditions
- Prior consultation with Gram Sabha required for land acquisition in Scheduled Areas
- Gram Sabha controls minor forest produce, minor water bodies, minor minerals

## Key Provisions

**Three-tier structure:**

| Tier | Name (common) | Level |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Tier 1 | Gram Panchayat | Village |
| Tier 2 | Panchayat Samiti / Block Panchayat | Intermediate (block/taluka) |
| Tier 3 | Zila Parishad | District |

**Mandatory reservations:**
- ≥ 1/3 seats for women (many States have raised this to 50%)
- SC/ST seats proportionate to their population in the area
- Offices of Chairpersons also reserved in proportion

**State Election Commission (Article 243K):**
- Independent body; Superintendence, direction, control of electoral rolls and conduct of all panchayat elections
- State Election Commissioner removable only like a High Court judge

**State Finance Commission (Article 243I):**
- Constituted every 5 years
- Reviews financial position; recommends principles for tax sharing between State and panchayats
