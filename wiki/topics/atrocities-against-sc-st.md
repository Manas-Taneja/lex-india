## Definition

Atrocities against Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes are offences committed by non-SC/ST persons against members of SC/ST communities on the basis of their caste identity. The Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 (PoA Act), as amended in 2015, creates a special category of aggravated offences and mandates Special Courts and fast-track trials.

## Current Law

**SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 (as amended 2015)**

- **§ 3(1)** — Offences: enumerated list of atrocities — includes forcing to drink/eat noxious substances; dumping excreta on premises; parading naked; dispossessing from land; interfering with right to vote; forcing bonded labour; sexual exploitation; preventing access to water bodies, roads, public places; defiling places of worship
- **§ 3(2)** — Aggravated offences by public servants: additional punishment for public servants facilitating offences
- **§ 3(2)(v)** — Committing specified IPC/BNS offences (murder, rape, kidnapping etc.) against an SC/ST person with knowledge of their identity: minimum 10 years, up to life imprisonment
- **§ 4** — Neglect of duties by public servant: imprisonment 6 months–1 year
- **§ 8** — Presumption: if accused is not a member of SC/ST and the victim is, and the offence was committed in a place where SC/ST persons are in a minority — the court shall presume the offence was committed on the ground of caste
- **§ 14** — Special Courts: dedicated courts in each district for trial
- **§ 15A** — Rights of victims and witnesses: protection from intimidation, legal aid, travel expenses, witness protection
- **§ 17** — Preventive measures by District Magistrate
- **§ 18** — No anticipatory bail: no person accused of an offence under this Act shall be granted anticipatory bail

**2015 Amendment — key additions:**
- New offences added: using social/economic/political dominance to coerce SC/ST; imposing social or economic boycott; preventing right to marry a person of one's choice; defiling SC/ST person's body after death
- Enhanced rehabilitation and compensation provisions
- Special Public Prosecutor mandatory

## Key Provisions

**No anticipatory bail (§ 18):** Supreme Court in Subhash Kashinath Mahajan v State of Maharashtra (2018) had added procedural conditions (preliminary inquiry before FIR, approval before arrest). Parliament nullified this via 2018 amendment — § 18 and § 18A restored the strict no-anticipatory-bail rule.

**Presumption of caste motive (§ 8):** Where the accused is not an SC/ST member and the victim is, and the act occurs in a predominantly non-SC/ST area, caste motive is presumed. Accused must rebut.

**Mandatory minimum sentences:** Most offences under § 3 carry mandatory minimum imprisonment (6 months to life). Courts cannot impose lesser sentences.

**Compensation:** SC/ST (PoA) Rules, 1995 (as amended 2016) — mandatory compensation from State Government to victims:
- Murder of SC/ST person: ₹8.25 lakh
- Rape on SC/ST woman: ₹5 lakh (gang rape: ₹8.25 lakh)
- Other offences: ₹85,000–2.5 lakh depending on offence

**Exclusive jurisdiction:** Only Special Courts (designated Sessions Courts) have jurisdiction; no committal from lower courts needed.

**Who can file:** Any person (not necessarily the victim) can file a complaint. Protection Officers designated under the Act assist complainants.
