## Definition

Assault is the act of making a gesture or preparation that causes another person to apprehend that criminal force is about to be used against them. No actual physical contact is required — the apprehension itself constitutes the offence. Criminal force (actually touching or moving a person without consent) is a separate but related offence.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 129** — Assault: intentional gesture/preparation causing apprehension of criminal force
- **§ 130** — Criminal force: knowingly using force on a person without consent to cause fear, hurt, or wrongful restraint
- **§ 131** — Punishment for assault or criminal force (not covered by more specific sections): imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to ₹1,000, or both
- **§ 132** — Assault or criminal force to deter a public servant: up to 2 years, or fine, or both
- **§ 133** — Assault with intent to commit other offences: up to 2 years, or fine, or both
- **§ 134** — Assaulting or using criminal force with intent to dishonour: up to 2 years, or fine, or both
- **§ 74** — Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty: up to 5 years + fine
- **§ 75** — Sexual harassment: up to 3 years (first offence), up to 5 years (subsequent offence) + fine

## Key Provisions

**Elements of assault (§ 129):**
1. A gesture or preparation by the accused
2. Done intentionally or knowingly
3. The other person reasonably apprehends that criminal force is about to be used

**Elements of criminal force (§ 130):**
1. Use of force on another person
2. Without that person's consent
3. Intentionally — to cause fear, hurt, wrongful restraint, or commit an offence

**Words alone insufficient:** Mere words do not constitute assault. However, words combined with a gesture that creates apprehension can amount to assault.

**Hurt vs assault:** Hurt (§ 114) requires actual bodily pain, disease, or infirmity — assault requires only apprehension. If force is actually applied and hurt results, the charge is hurt (§ 115–118), not mere assault.

**Grievous hurt (§ 116):** Emasculation, permanent loss of sight/hearing, disfigurement of face, fracture or dislocation of bone, dangerous hurt. Punishment: up to 7 years + fine.
