# Property Registration

## Definition
Registration is the process by which documents relating to immovable property are formally recorded with a government officer, conferring notice of the transaction to the world and giving the document legal enforceability.

## Current Law

### Registration Act 1908
- § 17 — compulsory registration: documents listed must be registered or they have no effect
- § 18 — optional registration: documents not under § 17 may be voluntarily registered
- § 19 — documents executed outside India may be registered in India within prescribed time
- § 23 — time for presentation: within 4 months of execution; Registrar may extend by further 4 months on payment of penalty (up to 10× fee)
- § 28 — place of registration: document relating to immovable property must be presented at office in whose district the property is situated
- § 32 — who may present: executant, claimant, representative, or authorised agent with power of attorney
- § 34–35 — enquiry by registering officer before accepting admission of execution
- § 49 — effect of non-registration: unregistered document cannot affect immovable property, create any right therein, or be admitted as evidence of a transaction required to be registered
- § 31 — exception: an unregistered document may be admitted as evidence of a collateral fact or for any purpose other than creating, assigning, or extinguishing an interest in immovable property

## Key Provisions

### Documents Requiring Compulsory Registration (§ 17)

| Document | Condition |
|----------|-----------|
| Instruments of gift of immovable property | Always |
| Non-testamentary instruments purporting to create/assign/limit/extinguish any right in immovable property | Value ≥ ₹100 |
| Leases of immovable property | Term > 1 year, or reserving yearly rent |
| Non-testamentary instruments acknowledging receipt/payment of any consideration for creation/etc. of such right | Value ≥ ₹100 |
| Decrees or orders of court affecting immovable property | As prescribed |

### Section 17(2) Exceptions (No Registration Needed)
- Wills (testamentary instruments)
- Grants by government
- Instruments of partition made by revenue officer
- Order granting a loan under Land Improvement Loans Act
- Powers of attorney authorising specific acts not relating to immovable property

### Hierarchy of Registration Authorities

| Authority | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Sub-Registrar | Receives and registers documents at taluk/district level |
| Registrar | Hears appeals against Sub-Registrar; can extend presentation time |
| Inspector General of Registration | Superintends all registration offices in state; policy and appellate |

### Effect of Non-Registration (§ 49)
- Cannot affect the immovable property described therein
- Cannot confer any power or create any right or liability in respect of such property
- Cannot be received as evidence in any suit for specific performance
- Cannot be admitted as evidence of any transaction affecting immovable property

### DORIS (Document Registration Information System)
State-level computerisation initiative linking sub-registrar offices; enables online appointment booking, e-registration in some states, digital encumbrance certificate (EC) search, and stamp duty calculation via ready reckoner rates. Several states (Maharashtra — iGR, Karnataka — Kaveri, Telangana — IGRS) have fully online workflows.
