# Gratuity

## Definition
Gratuity is a statutory lump-sum payment made by an employer to an employee as a retirement or terminal benefit in recognition of past service, governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 (PGA), and payable on superannuation, retirement, resignation, death, or disablement after a minimum qualifying period of continuous service.

## Current Law

### Payment of Gratuity Act 1972

- § 1(3) — applicability: every factory, mine, oilfield, plantation, port, railway company, and every shop/establishment with ≥10 employees (once applicable, remains so even if workforce falls below 10)
- § 2(e) — "employee": any person employed on wages whether directly or through contractor; includes part-time workers but excludes apprentices
- § 4(1) — gratuity payable on: (a) superannuation; (b) retirement or resignation after continuous service ≥5 years; (c) death; (d) disablement due to accident or disease (no 5-year requirement for death/disablement)
- § 4(2) — formula: 15 days' wages for each completed year of service (or part thereof in excess of 6 months)
  - For seasonal establishments: 7 days' wages per season
  - "Wages" = last drawn basic + DA (excludes HRA, OTA, bonus, commission)
  - 15 days' wages = (Monthly wages × 15) / 26 (26 working days per month for monthly-rated employees)
- § 4(3) — maximum ceiling: ₹20 lakh (enhanced by Payment of Gratuity (Amendment) Act 2018; previously ₹10 lakh); central government may notify higher ceiling
- § 4(5) — more favourable terms: employer may pay gratuity more than statutory minimum; employee entitled to whichever is more beneficial
- § 4(6) — forfeiture: gratuity may be forfeited wholly or partially if employment terminated for: (a) wilful omission or negligence causing damage/destruction to employer's property; or (b) riotous/disorderly conduct or any act of violence; or (c) moral turpitude offences
- § 4AA — maternity leave treated as continuous service: period of maternity leave availed under the Maternity Benefit Act 1961 shall be treated as continuous service for the purpose of gratuity
- § 6 — nomination: employee must nominate a family member to receive gratuity in event of death; nomination filed with employer; can be modified
- § 7 — determination and recovery: employer must determine and pay gratuity within 30 days of becoming payable; interest at rate specified by central government on delayed payment
- § 7(3) — application: employee or nominee may apply to employer; if employer fails to pay, apply to Controlling Authority
- § 7(7) — appeal: appeal to appellate authority within 60 days of Controlling Authority's order
- § 8 — recovery: Controlling Authority may issue recovery certificate; recoverable as arrears of land revenue
- § 9 — penalties: failure to pay gratuity — imprisonment 6 months to 2 years; making false statements — imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fine up to ₹10,000
- § 14 — overriding effect: Act overrides any agreement, award, or contract providing less favourable terms; more favourable terms preserved

## Key Provisions

### Gratuity Calculation

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Minimum continuous service | 5 years (waived for death/disablement) |
| Rate | 15 days' wages per completed year |
| Part year rounding | > 6 months counts as full year |
| Daily wage derivation | Monthly wage ÷ 26 × 15 |
| Maximum ceiling | ₹20 lakh (as of 2018) |
| Seasonal establishments | 7 days' wages per season |

### Forfeiture Grounds (§ 4(6))

| Ground | Extent of Forfeiture |
|--------|---------------------|
| Wilful omission/negligence causing loss | Proportionate to loss caused |
| Damage/destruction of employer's property | Up to the value of damage |
| Riotous conduct, violence | Whole gratuity |
| Offence involving moral turpitude | Whole gratuity |

### Key Timelines

| Event | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Payment after becoming due | Within 30 days |
| Interest on delayed payment | Applicable after 30 days |
| Application to Controlling Authority | If not paid within 30 days |
| Appeal to Appellate Authority | Within 60 days of CA order |

### Continuous Service (§ 2A)

- Continuous service includes: authorised leave, accident/illness leave, lay-off, strike (not illegal), maternity leave (§ 4AA)
- Service interrupted by termination followed by re-employment may be counted if terms so provide
- Probation period counts as service if employee is ultimately confirmed
