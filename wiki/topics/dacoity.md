## Definition

Dacoity is the commission of robbery by five or more persons acting conjointly. It is the most serious property-related offence in Indian criminal law, combining violent robbery with gang criminality. Robbery itself is aggravated theft or extortion accompanied by the use or threat of force.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 309** — Robbery: theft or extortion with use/threat of immediate force or causing/attempting to cause death or wrongful restraint to commit theft or extortion
- **§ 310** — Dacoity: robbery committed by five or more persons conjointly (or attempting such robbery)
- **§ 311** — Punishment for robbery: up to 10 years rigorous imprisonment + fine; up to 14 years if on a highway between sunset and sunrise
- **§ 312** — Punishment for dacoity: rigorous imprisonment for life, or up to 10 years + fine
- **§ 313** — Dacoity with murder: death, or life imprisonment, or rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years + fine
- **§ 314** — Robbery or dacoity with attempt to cause death or grievous hurt: rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years + fine (robbery); life imprisonment (dacoity)
- **§ 315** — Attempting to commit robbery or dacoity when armed: rigorous imprisonment up to 7 years (robbery); up to 10 years (dacoity)
- **§ 317** — Making preparation to commit dacoity: up to 10 years + fine
- **§ 318(1)** — Belonging to a gang of dacoits: rigorous imprisonment for life, or up to 10 years + fine

## Key Provisions

**Chain: Theft → Robbery → Dacoity:**

| Offence | Key element | Section | Punishment |
|---------|------------|---------|-----------|
| Theft | Dishonest taking without consent | § 303 | Up to 3 years |
| Extortion | Putting in fear to deliver property | § 308 | Up to 3 years |
| Robbery | Theft/extortion + immediate force/fear | § 309 | Up to 10 years |
| Dacoity | Robbery by 5 or more persons | § 310 | Life or 10 years |

**Five-person rule (§ 310):** All five need not actually commit robbery — they must be present and acting conjointly. A person who stays outside as a lookout but is part of the gang can be charged.

**Preparation for dacoity (§ 317):** Rare in Indian law — preparation is ordinarily not an offence. Dacoity is serious enough that even preparation is punishable (up to 10 years).

**Gang membership (§ 318(1)):** Being a member of a dacoity gang is itself an offence even if no specific robbery is committed at the time.
