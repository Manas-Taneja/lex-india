## Definition

Consumer rights in India are governed by the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (CPA 2019), which replaced the Consumer Protection Act, 1986. The 2019 Act established the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA), created product liability provisions, strengthened e-commerce regulation, and substantially increased pecuniary limits for consumer commissions. A "consumer" is any person who buys goods or hires services for consideration, not for commercial resale.

## Current Law

**Consumer Protection Act, 2019**

- **§ 2(7)** — Consumer: person who buys goods or hires services for consideration; includes user with consent of buyer; excludes persons who buy for resale or commercial purpose
- **§ 2(9)** — Consumer rights: right to safety; right to be informed; right to choose; right to be heard; right to seek redressal; right to consumer education
- **§ 2(11)** — Deficiency: any fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in quality, nature, or manner of performance in service
- **§ 2(34)** — Product liability: manufacturer or seller or service provider liable for injury caused by defective product or deficient service
- **§ 2(47)** — Unfair trade practice: false representation, misleading advertisement, bait and switch, high-pressure sales, deceptive pricing, unsolicited goods with demand for payment
- **§ 15** — Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA): promotes consumer rights; investigates complaints; can issue recall orders, refund orders, cease-and-desist; impose penalty up to ₹10 lakh for misleading advertisements
- **§ 21** — Misleading advertisements: CCPA may prohibit and impose fines on manufacturers and endorsers; endorser liable (₹10 lakh first offence; ₹50 lakh subsequent offences)
- **§ 34** — District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: pecuniary jurisdiction ≤ ₹50 lakh
- **§ 47** — State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: ₹50 lakh–₹2 crore
- **§ 58** — National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC): > ₹2 crore
- **§ 35** — Filing complaint: complaint may be filed at place where complainant resides or works (changed from 2019 — pro-consumer shift from 1986 Act's defendant's place rule)
- **§ 36** — Admissibility of complaint: commission checks admissibility on first hearing; frivolous complaints may be dismissed with costs
- **§ 38** — Mediation: commission may refer matter to mediation at any stage with consent of parties
- **§ 39** — Reliefs: refund, replacement, removal of defect, compensation for loss/injury, punitive damages, payment of costs, discontinuation of unfair/restrictive practice
- **§ 69** — Limitation: complaint must be filed within 2 years from date of cause of action; delay may be condoned for sufficient cause
- **§§ 82-87** — Product liability: manufacturer, seller, or service provider liable for injury from defective product; includes design defect, manufacturing defect, inadequate warning; strict liability for some categories

## Key Provisions

**Commission pecuniary limits (2019 Act vs 1986 Act):**

| Forum | 1986 Act | 2019 Act |
|-------|----------|----------|
| District Commission | Up to ₹20 lakh | Up to ₹50 lakh |
| State Commission | ₹20 lakh–₹1 crore | ₹50 lakh–₹2 crore |
| National Commission (NCDRC) | Above ₹1 crore | Above ₹2 crore |

**Who is a consumer — key exclusions:**
- Person who buys goods for commercial purpose is not a consumer (e.g., a business buying machinery for a factory)
- Exception: person who buys goods for self-employment to earn livelihood — IS a consumer (e.g., auto-rickshaw driver buying vehicle for personal livelihood)

**E-commerce obligations (Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020):**
- Sellers must display all mandatory information: country of origin, expiry date, total price
- No fake reviews; no preferential treatment to own entities on marketplace
- Grievance officer must be appointed; complaints resolved within 1 month
- Flash sales that prevent fair access are prohibited

**Complaint process:**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Filing | Written complaint with facts, relief sought, supporting documents |
| Admissibility | Commission checks within 21 days |
| Mediation | Optional — referred with consent |
| Notice to opposite party | Response within 30 days |
| Hearing | Evidence, cross-examination |
| Order | Within 3 months (2 months where no laboratory testing needed) |
| Execution | Like a civil court decree |

**Product liability (§§ 82-87):** A product liability action lies for death, bodily injury, or property damage caused by a defective product against the manufacturer, seller, or service provider. Defect categories: manufacturing defect, design defect, deviation from manufacturing specifications, inadequate instructions/warnings.

Defences available to manufacturer: product was not defective at time of delivery; plaintiff modified the product; plaintiff knew the danger and proceeded.

**Arbitration clause in consumer contracts:** Arbitration clause in a standard form consumer contract does not oust consumer commission jurisdiction. Consumer may elect to go to consumer commission even if there is an arbitration agreement (National Seeds Corp v M. Madhusudhan Reddy, 2012 — SC).

**Unfair contract terms (§ 46):** NCDRC may declare unfair clauses in standard form contracts void — one-sided, unreasonable terms in consumer contracts.

**Appeals:**
- From District Commission → State Commission (within 45 days of order)
- From State Commission → NCDRC (within 30 days)
- From NCDRC → Supreme Court (within 30 days)
