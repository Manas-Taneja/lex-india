# Mental Health Law

## Definition
The Mental Healthcare Act 2017 (MHA 2017) replaced the Mental Health Act 1987 and establishes a rights-based framework for the care, treatment, and protection of persons with mental illness, aligning Indian law with the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).

## Current Law

### Mental Healthcare Act 2017
- § 3 — Right to access mental healthcare and treatment from government-run and government-funded services
- § 5 — Right to confidentiality: information about mental illness not to be disclosed without consent except as required by law
- § 7 — Advance directive: person may make written directive specifying how they wish to be treated or not treated during mental illness; must be registered with the concerned authority
- § 18 — Right to free mental health treatment at all government mental health establishments
- § 20 — Right to legal aid at every stage of proceedings before the Mental Health Review Board
- § 21 — No seclusion: no person with mental illness shall be kept in seclusion under any circumstances
- § 23 — Right to live in the community: cannot be segregated from society against their will
- § 24 — No forced treatment except in emergency situations; preference for supported decision making over substituted decision making (CRPD compliance)
- § 29 — Insurance parity: every insurer shall make provision for medical insurance for treatment of mental illness on same basis as physical illness
- § 100 — Duties of police: police to take person with apparent mental illness to nearest government mental health establishment; not to be kept in lock-up
- § 115 — Attempt to suicide: person who attempts suicide under severe stress presumed to have mental illness; shall not be punished; government to rehabilitate and provide care

### Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023
- § 226 — Attempt to commit suicide punishable only if it obstructs or attempts to obstruct a public servant from performing duty; suicide per se is no longer an offence (replacing IPC § 309)

## Key Provisions

### Rights of Persons with Mental Illness
| Right | Section | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Access to healthcare | § 3 | Government-run services at affordable cost |
| Confidentiality | § 5 | No disclosure without consent |
| Advance directive | § 7 | Written instructions for treatment preferences |
| Free treatment | § 18 | All govt mental health establishments |
| Legal aid | § 20 | At every stage of MHRB proceedings |
| No seclusion | § 21 | Absolute prohibition |
| Community living | § 23 | Cannot be institutionalised against will |
| Insurance parity | § 29 | Same coverage as physical illness |

### Regulatory Bodies
| Body | Sections | Function |
|------|---------|----------|
| Central Mental Health Authority (CMHA) | §§ 33–45 | National-level regulation, policy, registers |
| State Mental Health Authority (SMHA) | §§ 46–64 | State-level licensing and oversight |
| Mental Health Review Board (MHRB) | §§ 73–77 | Review involuntary admissions, advance directives |

### Mental Health Establishments
- §§ 65–72 — Registration and regulation; no establishment to operate without registration with SMHA
- § 66 — Standards to be maintained: physical environment, staffing, treatment protocols
- § 79 — Inspection of establishments by SMHA or designated authority
- §§ 81–85 — Penalties for operating unregistered establishments

### Admission Categories
| Type | Basis | Review |
|------|-------|--------|
| Independent (voluntary) | Own consent; capacity intact | Self-discharge permitted |
| Supported admission | Incapacity; supported by nominated representative | MHRB review within 7 days |
| Involuntary admission | Incapacity; high risk; no willing representative | MHRB review within 7 days; 30-day limit |
