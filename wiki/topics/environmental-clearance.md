# Environmental Clearance

## Definition
Environmental Clearance (EC) is a mandatory prior approval required before starting any project or activity listed under the EIA Notification 2006, granted by MoEFCC (Category A) or the State-level SEIAA (Category B) after an appraisal process that includes public consultation.

## Current Law

### Environment (Protection) Act 1986
- § 3 — Central Government power to take all measures for protecting and improving environmental quality
- § 5 — Central Govt may issue binding directions to any person, officer, or authority (including closure/prohibition orders)
- § 7 — Prohibition on persons carrying on industry or operation causing discharge of environmental pollutants in excess of prescribed standards
- § 15 — Penalty for contravention: imprisonment up to 5 years + fine up to ₹1 lakh; continuing violation attracts additional fine of ₹5,000 per day

### EIA Notification 2006 (under EPA § 3)
- Category A projects — appraised at central level by MoEFCC with Expert Appraisal Committee (EAC)
- Category B projects — appraised at state level by SEIAA; sub-divided into B1 (EIA required) and B2 (no EIA required)
- 4-stage process: Screening → Scoping → Public Hearing → Appraisal
- Public Hearing — minimum 30 days' notice; conducted by SPCB; mandatory for Category A and B1 projects
- EC validity: 5 years for new projects; 10 years for river valley/mining projects; extendable once

### Forest Conservation Act 1980
- § 2 — No state government may divert forest land for non-forest use without prior Central Govt approval
- Stage I clearance (in-principle approval) → Stage II clearance (final approval after conditions met)
- Compensatory Afforestation Fund Act 2016: non-net present value (NPV) and CA funds deposited with CAMPA

### Coastal Regulation Zone Notification 2019
- Classifies coastal stretches into CRZ-I (ecologically sensitive), CRZ-II (urban), CRZ-III (rural), CRZ-IV (islands)
- No development permitted in No Development Zone (NDZ); CRZ-I requires special clearance

### National Green Tribunal Act 2010
- §§ 14–18 — NGT has jurisdiction over substantial questions relating to environment including enforcement of any legal right
- § 20 — NGT may award compensation and relief
- § 22 — Orders of NGT enforceable as decree of civil court
- § 23 — Appeals from NGT orders lie to the Supreme Court

## Key Provisions

### EIA 4-Stage Process
| Stage | What Happens | Who Decides |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Screening | Categorise as A, B1, or B2 | MoEFCC / SEIAA |
| Scoping | Define Terms of Reference (ToR) for EIA study | EAC / SEAC |
| Public Hearing | Present EIA report; receive objections (≥30 days notice) | SPCB |
| Appraisal | Review EIA + public hearing minutes; grant/reject EC | EAC / SEAC → MoEFCC / SEIAA |

### Post-EC Obligations
| Obligation | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Half-yearly compliance reports | Submitted to MoEFCC/SEIAA and SPCB |
| Environment Management Plan (EMP) | Must be implemented as per EC conditions |
| Corporate Environment Responsibility | Mandatory for certain industries |
| Violation | Revocation of EC + prosecution under § 15 EPA |
