# Maternity Benefit

## Definition
The Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (MBA) protects the employment of women during maternity and entitles them to fully paid leave, nursing breaks, crèche facilities, and protection from dismissal, with the 2017 amendment significantly expanding these rights.

## Current Law

### Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (as amended 2017)

- § 2 — applicability: every establishment (factory, mine, plantation, shop, or establishment under Shops & Establishments Act) employing ≥10 persons
- § 3(n) — "woman": a female employee whether employed directly or through contractor
- § 4 — prohibition of work during certain periods: employer shall not knowingly employ, and woman shall not work, during 6 weeks immediately following delivery/miscarriage
- § 5(1) — maternity benefit: paid leave at average daily wage rate
  - 26 weeks for the first 2 children (≥ 8 weeks must be taken before expected delivery)
  - 12 weeks for the 3rd child and beyond
  - 12 weeks for women who adopt a child below 3 months of age
  - 12 weeks for commissioning mothers (surrogacy)
- § 5A — work from home: employer may allow work from home after the leave period if the nature of work permits; by mutual agreement
- § 6 — notice of claim: woman must give written notice to employer before availing leave
- § 9 — leave for miscarriage: 6 weeks paid leave following miscarriage/medical termination
- § 9A — leave for tubectomy: 2 weeks paid leave following tubectomy
- § 10 — leave for illness: additional leave up to 1 month for illness arising out of pregnancy, delivery, or premature birth
- § 11 — nursing breaks: 2 nursing breaks of prescribed duration per day until child is 15 months old (in addition to rest interval)
- § 11A — crèche facility: employer with ≥ 50 employees must provide crèche facility; woman employee entitled to 4 daily visits to the crèche
- § 12 — dismissal during absence prohibited: employer shall not discharge or dismiss a woman during maternity absence; any such dismissal is void
- § 13 — no deduction from wages: absence for maternity shall not be treated as break in service
- § 21 — penalties: imprisonment up to 1 year and/or fine up to ₹5,000 for contravention

## Key Provisions

### Leave Entitlement Summary

| Category | Duration | Pre-delivery |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| First or second child | 26 weeks | ≥ 8 weeks before delivery |
| Third child onwards | 12 weeks | ≥ 6 weeks before delivery |
| Adoption (child < 3 months) | 12 weeks | From date of adoption |
| Commissioning mother (surrogacy) | 12 weeks | From date of birth |
| Miscarriage / MTP | 6 weeks | N/A |
| Tubectomy | 2 weeks | N/A |
| Illness related to pregnancy | Up to 1 month (additional) | N/A |

### Employer Obligations

| Obligation | Threshold | Provision |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| Paid maternity leave | ≥ 10 employees | § 5 |
| Crèche facility | ≥ 50 employees | § 11A |
| Nursing breaks (×2/day) | All covered establishments | § 11 |
| Work from home option | All covered establishments | § 5A |
| Non-dismissal protection | All covered establishments | § 12 |

### Protection Against Dismissal
- Any discharge or dismissal during maternity leave is void
- Employer who dismisses a woman knowing she is pregnant is liable to pay maternity benefit in full in addition to penalty
- Woman may resign without forfeiting maternity benefit if dismissal is unlawful
