## Definition

Insurance law in India is governed primarily by the Insurance Act, 1938 (as substantially amended), the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India Act, 1999 (IRDAI Act), and sector-specific statutes for motor (Motor Vehicles Act, 1988) and life/health insurance. The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) is the apex regulator. Insurance contracts are contracts of utmost good faith (uberrimae fidei) — both parties must disclose all material facts.

## Current Law

**Insurance Act, 1938**

- **§ 2(7A)** — Health insurance business: insurance against sickness, bodily injury, death (as ancillary), disability
- **§ 3** — Registration of insurers: no person shall carry on insurance business without certificate of registration from IRDAI; minimum paid-up equity capital ₹100 crore (life/general); ₹200 crore (reinsurance)
- **§ 6** — Capital requirements: life — ₹100 crore; general — ₹100 crore; health — ₹100 crore; reinsurance — ₹200 crore
- **§ 27-27G** — Investment of assets: insurers must invest in specified assets (government securities, approved securities); not less than 25% in Central Government securities (life); solvency margin requirements
- **§ 38** — Assignment and transfer of life insurance policies: assignment valid if in prescribed form; notice to insurer; insurer may question assignee's insurable interest
- **§ 39** — Nomination of beneficiary: life insurance policyholder may nominate person to receive policy proceeds on death; nominee is a trustee (not absolute beneficiary) where legal heirs exist; except if nominee is spouse/child/parent — they receive as beneficial nominee
- **§ 41** — Prohibition of rebates: no rebate of premium permitted; offering rebate = criminal offence
- **§ 45** — Policy not to be called in question after 3 years: insurer cannot contest life insurance policy on any ground after 3 years from date of commencement/revival — except fraud; no time limit for fraud-based repudiation
- **§ 64UM** — Surveyors: claims above ₹50,000 in general insurance must be surveyed by licensed surveyor
- **§ 64VB** — Premium to be paid in advance: risk attaches only after premium paid; insurer not on risk if premium not received

**IRDAI Act, 1999**

- **§ 14** — IRDAI powers: register insurers; regulate investment; specify products and premium; conduct inspection; adjudicate disputes between insurer/intermediary; promote and regulate insurance business

**Key IRDAI Regulations:**

- **IRDAI (Protection of Policyholders' Interests) Regulations, 2017:** prospectus/proposal form requirements; free-look period (15 days from receipt of policy document — 30 days for distance marketing); claim settlement within 30 days; insurer must provide reasons for repudiation
- **IRDAI (Health Insurance) Regulations, 2016:** standard products (Arogya Sanjeevani); lifetime renewability; portability of health insurance
- **IRDAI Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS):** centralised complaint registration for policyholders

**Motor Vehicles Act, 1988**

- **§ 146** — Compulsory third-party insurance: no motor vehicle shall be driven without a valid policy of insurance covering third-party liability (personal injury/death)
- **§ 147** — Requirements of insurance policy: covers liability — death/bodily injury to any person (unlimited); property damage (₹7.5 lakh for private, ₹7.5 lakh for goods vehicles); no need to cover damage to own vehicle
- **§ 165** — Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal: State Government establishes MACTs to adjudicate compensation claims
- **§ 166** — Claims petition: by victim/dependants within 6 months of accident (no limitation post-2019 Amendment — but promptly advised)

## Key Provisions

**Principles of insurance law:**

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Utmost good faith (uberrimae fidei) | Both insured and insurer must disclose all material facts; non-disclosure → voidable policy |
| Insurable interest | Insured must have financial stake in the subject matter; prevents wagering |
| Indemnity | General insurance: insured restored to pre-loss position; not more than actual loss (life insurance: not indemnity — fixed sum) |
| Subrogation | On indemnifying insured, insurer steps into insured's shoes to recover from third party |
| Contribution | Where multiple policies cover same risk, insurers contribute proportionally |
| Proximate cause | Loss must be proximately (directly) caused by insured peril |

**Life vs general insurance:**

| Feature | Life Insurance | General Insurance |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| Principle | Not indemnity — benefit contract | Indemnity |
| Insurable interest | Required at inception only | Required at inception + at time of loss |
| Assignment | Permitted (§ 38) | Permitted in marine; restricted otherwise |
| Contestability | Policy incontestable after 3 years (§ 45) | No similar provision |
| Nomination | § 39 — beneficial nomination | N/A |

**Claim settlement timeline (IRDAI regulations):**
- Intimation of claim: immediately after event
- Survey (for general): surveyor appointed within 72 hours; report within 30 days of last document
- Claim settlement: within 30 days of receiving all documents; 45 days if investigation required
- Interest on delayed payment: bank rate + 2% per annum from date of claim if not settled within time

**Insurance Ombudsman:** Established under Insurance Ombudsman Rules, 2017. Jurisdiction up to ₹50 lakh for individual policyholders. Adjudicates complaints about claim repudiation, delay in settlement, disputes on premium. Decision is binding on insurer; complainant may accept or reject award.

**Free-look period:** 15 days from receipt of policy (30 days for policies sold through distance marketing/online). Policyholder may return policy; insurer refunds premium after deducting proportional risk premium and medical examination costs.

**§ 45 — three-year rule for life insurance:**
- Within 3 years: insurer may contest policy on any ground including misrepresentation, non-disclosure
- After 3 years: insurer can contest only on ground of fraud; burden of proof on insurer
