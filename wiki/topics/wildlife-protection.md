# Wildlife Protection

## Definition
The Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 (WPA) provides a comprehensive framework for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants by prohibiting hunting, regulating trade, and establishing a network of protected areas including sanctuaries, national parks, and tiger reserves across India.

## Current Law

### Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 (as amended 2022)
- § 2(16) — Defines "wild animal" to include animals specified in Schedules I to IV; 2022 amendment replaced 6 schedules with 4 (Schedule I: strictly protected; Schedule II: regulated; Schedule III: plants; Schedule IV: vermin species)
- § 9 — Hunting of any wild animal specified in Schedules I–IV prohibited; "hunting" includes poisoning, trapping, snaring, capturing
- § 16 — Hunting of Schedule I animals: imprisonment not less than 3 years (extendable to 7 years) + fine not less than ₹10,000 (second or subsequent offence: imprisonment 3–7 years + fine ≥ ₹25,000)
- § 18 — State Govt may, by notification, declare any area a Wildlife Sanctuary for protection of wild animals
- § 26A — Core area/critical wildlife habitat of a sanctuary — no human activity except by specific permit
- § 35 — State/Central Govt may declare any area a National Park; no grazing or collection of forest produce permitted
- § 38V — Central Govt may, on recommendation of the Tiger Conservation Authority, notify a Tiger Reserve; Project Tiger administered thereunder
- § 38W — Critical Tiger Habitat (CTH) within a Tiger Reserve must be kept inviolate; no diversion without opinion of Scientific Committee and affirmation by expert Committee
- §§ 48A–48C — Establishment and management of Conservation Reserves and Community Reserves
- § 49B — Prohibition on trade in any scheduled animal, animal article, trophy, or meat
- §§ 51–58 — Penalties; 2022 amendment significantly enhanced minimum fines

### CITES Implementation
- India is a signatory to the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES)
- Schedule I of WPA includes species in CITES Appendix I (prohibited trade); Schedule II includes Appendix II species (regulated trade)
- Wildlife Crime Control Bureau (WCCB) — established under § 38(Z) of WPA; coordinates enforcement, intelligence, and prosecution on wildlife crime
- Directorate of Wildlife Preservation — apex body under MoEFCC for policy and administration

## Key Provisions

### Protected Area Hierarchy
| Category | Legal Basis | Key Restriction |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Wildlife Sanctuary | § 18 WPA | No hunting; regulated human activity |
| National Park | § 35 WPA | No grazing, collection, or human settlement |
| Tiger Reserve | § 38V WPA | Buffer + core zone; CTH inviolate |
| Conservation Reserve | § 36A WPA | Community-managed; limited settlement |
| Community Reserve | § 36C WPA | Local community management; no land acquisition |

### Penalty Tiers (post-2022 amendment)
| Offence | Minimum Imprisonment | Minimum Fine |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| Hunting Schedule I animal | 3 years | ₹10,000 |
| Second or subsequent Schedule I offence | 3 years | ₹25,000 |
| Trade in scheduled animal/article | 3 years | ₹10,000 |
| Violation of protected area rules | 3 years | ₹25,000 |
| Ivory/rhino horn possession | 3 years | ₹10,000 |

### 2022 Amendment — Key Changes
| Change | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Schedules reduced from VI to IV | Simplified protection categories |
| IUCN Red List species included | Near-threatened and above automatically protected |
| Enhanced penalties | Minimum fines raised across all offences |
| TRAFFIC India collaboration | Strengthened trade monitoring |
| Invasive species regulation | New powers to control invasive plants/animals |
