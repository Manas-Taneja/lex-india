## Definition

Terror offences in India are primarily governed by the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 (UAPA), as amended in 2004, 2008, 2012, and 2019. UAPA criminalises membership of unlawful associations and terrorist organisations, terrorist acts, funding of terrorism, and recruitment. The 2019 amendment allows designation of individuals (not just organisations) as terrorists.

## Current Law

**Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 (UAPA)**

- **§ 2(o)** — Terrorist act: an act committed with intent to threaten the unity, integrity, security, or sovereignty of India or to strike terror, by using bombs, explosives, firearms, or hazardous substances, or by disrupting essential services or critical infrastructure
- **§ 10** — Membership of unlawful association: imprisonment up to 2 years + fine; active membership or bearing arms — up to 7 years
- **§ 15** — Terrorist acts: death, mutilation, kidnapping, damage to property, disruption of essential services — punishment: death or life imprisonment (if death caused); 5 years to life (otherwise)
- **§ 16** — Punishment for terrorist acts
- **§ 17** — Raising funds for terrorist acts: life imprisonment + fine
- **§ 18** — Conspiracy, attempt, abetment of terrorist acts: same as the act itself
- **§ 19** — Recruiting for terrorist organisations: life imprisonment + fine
- **§ 20** — Being a member of a terrorist gang or organisation: life imprisonment + fine
- **§ 35** — Designation of terrorist organisations (First Schedule): Central Government can notify any organisation as a terrorist organisation
- **§ 35A** — Designation of individuals as terrorists *(inserted by 2019 amendment)*: Central Government can designate any individual as a terrorist if satisfied they are involved in terrorism
- **§ 43D(5)** — Bail restriction: bail shall not be granted if the court, on a perusal of the case diary or chargesheet, is of prima facie opinion that the accusation is true

**National Investigation Agency (NIA) Act, 2008:**
- Creates the NIA as the national counter-terrorism police force
- NIA has jurisdiction over UAPA, explosives, nuclear/radiological offences, hijacking, maritime piracy, cyberterrorism
- NIA courts (Special Courts) for trial

## Key Provisions

**Section 43D(5) — Bail bar:** The most stringent bail provision in Indian law. Court need only find the accusation "prima facie true" based on the case diary — actual evidence is not tested at this stage. Courts have noted this makes bail virtually impossible under UAPA.

**Individual designation (§ 35A, 2019):** Central Government can place an individual on the terrorist list without trial. Challenged as violating Article 21; constitutional validity upheld by various High Courts, pending final Supreme Court determination.

**Unlawful activity vs terrorist act distinction:**
| | Unlawful activity (§ 10) | Terrorist act (§ 15) |
|-|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Examples | Separatist propaganda, anti-India slogans | Bombing, assassination, hostage-taking |
| Intent | Enough to question sovereignty/integrity | Must intend to strike terror or threaten sovereignty |
| Punishment | Up to 7 years | Life imprisonment or death |

**Chargesheet deadline:** UAPA allows extended police custody (up to 30 days) and detention without chargesheet up to 180 days (compared to 90 days under ordinary law), with court approval.

**UAPA + BNS:** Terror cases are also charged under BNS (murder, conspiracy, etc.). UAPA's special provisions override ordinary criminal procedure under BNSS in case of conflict.

**Schedule (First Schedule):** Lists banned organisations — includes various separatist groups, religious extremist organisations, and internationally designated terrorist groups. Central Government reviews the list periodically.
