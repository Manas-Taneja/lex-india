# Land Acquisition

## Definition
Land acquisition is the compulsory acquisition of private land by the state for a public purpose, in exchange for compensation. The Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act 2013 (RFCTLARR / LARR Act) replaced the colonial Land Acquisition Act 1894 and introduced mandatory consent, social impact assessment, and enhanced compensation.

## Current Law

### RFCTLARR Act 2013
- § 2(e) — "public purpose" defined exhaustively: strategic/national security, infrastructure, industrial corridors, mining, government projects, planned development, rural infrastructure, housing for poor
- § 4 — Social Impact Assessment (SIA) mandatory for all acquisitions (except urgency clause); conducted within 6 months
- § 5 — public hearing on SIA report before Expert Group appraisal
- § 7 — notice to Collector after government decision to acquire
- § 11 — preliminary notification published in Official Gazette and two local newspapers; affected families listed
- § 15 — no change in ownership/use after preliminary notification
- § 19 — declaration of acquisition by appropriate government after SIA and consent requirements met
- § 23 — Collector takes possession only after full compensation and R&R entitlements are paid/deposited
- § 24 — lapse of old (1894 Act) acquisitions: if compensation not paid OR possession not taken for 5 years, acquisition lapses
- § 40 — urgency clause: only for national defence, national security, or natural calamity; SIA and consent waived; limited to minimum land required

## Key Provisions

### Consent Requirements

| Project Type | Consent Required |
|-------------|------------------|
| Private companies | 80% of affected families |
| Public-Private Partnership (PPP) | 70% of affected families |
| Government infrastructure | No consent required |
| Urgency (§ 40) | No consent required |

### Compensation Formula (§§ 26–30)

| Component | Rural | Urban |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Market value multiplier | 2× (double) | 1× |
| Solatium | 100% of market value | 100% of market value |
| Annual interest on delayed payment | 12% p.a. | 12% p.a. |

Market value = highest of: (a) circle rate / ready reckoner, (b) average sale price of similar land in vicinity over 3 years, (c) consented amount in private negotiation.

### Rehabilitation and Resettlement (§§ 31–46)

R&R package applies when acquisition displaces families. Entitlements include:
- Subsistence allowance (₹3,000/month for 12 months)
- Housing (house of specified area or ₹1.5 lakh for rural / ₹1 lakh for urban)
- Employment or ₹5 lakh one-time payment or annuity of ₹2,000/month
- Land-for-land if government land available (irrigated land preferred)
- Resettlement site with civic amenities when > 100 families displaced

### Key Procedural Timeline

| Stage | Action |
|-------|--------|
| SIA commissioned | Starts on § 4 notification |
| SIA completion | Within 6 months |
| Expert Group appraisal | Within 2 months of SIA |
| Preliminary notification (§ 11) | After government approval |
| Objections period | 60 days from § 11 |
| Declaration (§ 19) | Within 12 months of § 11 |
| Award (§ 29) | Within 12 months of § 19 |
| Possession | Only after payment/deposit of full compensation + R&R |

### Key Case Law
- **Pune Municipal Corporation v Harakchand Misirimal Solanki (SC 2014)** — Held that under § 24(2) of the 2013 Act, acquisition under 1894 Act lapses if compensation not paid and possession not taken; clarified the conjunctive vs disjunctive reading (overruled in part by Constitution Bench in Indore Development Authority v Manoharlal, SC 2020, which held § 24(2) requires both conditions to be unfulfilled simultaneously)
- **Indore Development Authority v Manoharlal (SC 2020, 5-judge bench)** — § 24(2) lapse requires BOTH: compensation not deposited in court AND possession not taken; overruled the Pune Municipal Corp line
