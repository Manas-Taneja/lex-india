## Definition

Child sexual abuse offences in India are primarily governed by the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO Act), which defines and punishes sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography involving children (persons under 18 years). POCSO is a gender-neutral law — it protects all children regardless of sex.

## Current Law

**Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO Act)**

- **§ 2(d)** — Child: any person below the age of 18 years
- **§ 3** — Penetrative sexual assault: penetration of penis/object/part of body into vagina, mouth, urethra, or anus of a child, or compelling the child to do so to another person
- **§ 4** — Punishment for penetrative sexual assault: rigorous imprisonment 10–20 years + fine; life imprisonment for aggravated cases (§ 5–6)
- **§ 5** — Aggravated penetrative sexual assault: by police officer, armed forces, public servant, management/staff of an educational/medical institution, relative, gang, person in a position of trust or authority; or on a child below 16 years; causing grievous hurt, pregnancy, mental illness
- **§ 6** — Punishment for aggravated penetrative sexual assault: rigorous imprisonment 20 years to life + fine; death if victim is below 16 years (2019 amendment)
- **§ 7** — Sexual assault: touching with sexual intent (non-penetrative)
- **§ 8** — Punishment for sexual assault: imprisonment 3–5 years + fine
- **§ 9** — Aggravated sexual assault (same aggravating circumstances as § 5)
- **§ 10** — Punishment for aggravated sexual assault: 5–7 years + fine
- **§ 11** — Sexual harassment of a child: making sexual remarks, showing pornographic material, stalking, enticing, etc.
- **§ 12** — Punishment for sexual harassment: imprisonment up to 3 years + fine
- **§ 13–15** — Child pornography: storage for personal use — up to 3 years; transmission/distribution — 5–7 years; using a child for pornographic purposes — up to 5 years; second or subsequent conviction — 7 years
- **§ 19** — Mandatory reporting: any person who has knowledge or apprehension that a child has been or is likely to be subjected to an offence must report to Special Juvenile Police Unit or local police — failure to report is punishable with up to 6 months + fine
- **§ 23** — Prohibition on disclosure of child's identity in media
- **§ 28–38** — Special courts: dedicated courts for trial; child-friendly procedures, in-camera proceedings, screen/curtain, support person
- **§ 29** — Presumption of guilt: where penetrative sexual assault is alleged, the court shall presume the accused guilty unless the accused proves otherwise

## Key Provisions

**Age of consent:** 18 years for all purposes under POCSO. Consensual sexual activity between persons where one or both are below 18 is technically an offence under POCSO — courts have raised concerns about this in "Romeo and Juliet" cases; no blanket exception exists.

**Gender-neutral victim:** POCSO protects boys, girls, and transgender children equally. Unlike rape (BNS § 63 — only woman as victim), POCSO covers all children.

**Mandatory reporting (§ 19):** Unique — even a parent, teacher, or doctor who suspects abuse must report. Non-reporting is itself a criminal offence.

**Presumption of guilt (§ 29):** Reversal of ordinary criminal law principle. Once penetrative sexual assault is alleged, the accused must prove innocence — prosecution only needs to show the act occurred.

**Death penalty (post-2019 amendment):** For aggravated penetrative sexual assault on a child below 16 years (§ 6), death penalty is available. This was inserted following public outrage over child rape cases.

**Overlap with BNS:** POCSO is a special law and overrides the general provisions of BNS for child victims in sexual offences. Where BNS and POCSO both apply, POCSO's stricter provisions prevail.
