# Loan Recovery

## Definition
Loan recovery law governs the mechanisms by which banks and financial institutions can recover outstanding dues from defaulting borrowers. India has a multi-track system — secured creditors may enforce security out of court under SARFAESI; larger unsecured or complex debts go to specialised Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs); and insolvent corporate debtors are addressed through the IBC before the NCLT.

## Current Law

### SARFAESI Act 2002 — Secured Asset Enforcement

- § 2(f) — "non-performing asset" (NPA): an asset classified as such in accordance with RBI directions; generally a loan where interest/principal is overdue for more than 90 days
- § 2(o) — "secured creditor": bank, financial institution, or Asset Reconstruction Company (ARC) holding security interest
- § 13(2) — 60-day demand notice: secured creditor must serve written notice on borrower and guarantor(s) requiring discharge of the entire secured debt within 60 days
- § 13(3A) — borrower may make a representation or raise objections within 15 days; secured creditor must communicate reasons for non-acceptance within 15 days
- § 13(4) — on expiry of 60-day notice period (if default continues): creditor may (a) take possession of secured assets, (b) take over management of business, (c) appoint any person as manager, (d) require debtors of the borrower to pay directly to the creditor
- § 14 — secured creditor may approach the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate (CMM) or District Magistrate (DM) for assistance in taking physical possession of secured assets; CMM/DM must pass order within 60 days (extendable by 60 days for reasons recorded)
- § 17 — borrower or guarantor may apply to DRT within 45 days of possession or action under § 13(4); DRT may restore possession if action was not in accordance with the Act
- § 18 — appeal from DRT order to DRAT within 30 days; pre-deposit of 50% of debt required (may be reduced but not below 25% by DRAT)
- § 13(8) — if NPA proceeds insufficient to cover debt, balance may be recovered as per contract (unsecured)

### Recovery of Debts and Bankruptcy Act 1993 (RDB Act)

- Establishes Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs) and Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunals (DRATs)
- DRT jurisdiction: banks and financial institutions for recovery of debts ≥ ₹20 lakh
- § 19 — application for recovery of debt filed before DRT; Presiding Officer may issue Recovery Certificate
- § 22 — powers of DRT: examine witnesses, call for documents, issue interim orders (attachment, injunction), appoint receiver
- § 26A — DRT-I, DRT-II etc. in major cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata, and others — 39 DRTs nationwide)
- Recovery Officer executes Recovery Certificate; can attach and sell property of the debtor
- Appeal to DRAT: deposit 75% of the amount of debt determined by DRT (may be waived partially by DRAT)

### IBC 2016 — Corporate Insolvency Resolution

- § 3(12) — "default": non-payment of debt (minimum threshold ₹1 crore) when it has become due and payable
- § 7 — financial creditor (bank/FI) may file application for CIRP before NCLT on occurrence of default
- § 14 — moratorium: once CIRP admitted, moratorium prevents filing/continuation of suits, enforcement of security interests, recovery proceedings, and transfer of assets for the duration of CIRP (180 days + 90 day extension + 330-day outer limit)
- § 31 — resolution plan approved by Committee of Creditors (CoC) by 66% vote and sanctioned by NCLT is binding on all creditors including dissenting creditors
- § 33 — if no resolution plan approved within time limit, NCLT orders liquidation
- Waterfall under § 52 / § 53: secured creditors paid in priority during liquidation (operational creditors, unsecured creditors, equity shareholders follow)

### Alternate Recovery Mechanisms

- Lok Adalat: Banking cases up to ₹20 lakh (pre-litigation or during proceedings) can be referred; settlement under Legal Services Authorities Act 1987; award is decree of civil court, non-appealable
- Cheque dishonour under NI Act § 138: criminal prosecution for dishonoured cheques (imprisonment up to 2 years + fine up to twice the cheque amount); 30-day legal notice mandatory before filing complaint; limitation 1 month from cause of action
- Revenue Recovery Act (state-level): state governments can recover dues as arrears of land revenue on behalf of banks in some states
- One Time Settlement (OTS): RBI guidelines allow banks to settle NPA accounts below book value; must follow board-approved policy; no haircut below prescribed floors for wilful defaulters

### Willful Defaulters

- RBI Master Circular on Wilful Defaulters: a borrower is a wilful defaulter if (a) capable of paying but not doing so, (b) diverted funds, (c) siphoned funds, or (d) disposed of assets
- Consequences: no fresh facilities from any bank; promoters/directors debarred from accessing institutional finance for 5 years; names published on RBI/CIBIL lists; criminal action under § 420 IPC / § 316 BNS

## Key Provisions

### Recovery Track Selection

| Scenario | Forum | Governing Law |
|---|---|---|
| Secured NPA ≥ ₹1 lakh | Out-of-court enforcement | SARFAESI § 13 |
| Debt ≥ ₹20 lakh (secured or unsecured) | DRT | RDB Act 1993 |
| Debt < ₹20 lakh; cheque bounce | Lok Adalat / Civil Court | Legal Services Act / NI Act |
| Corporate default ≥ ₹1 crore | NCLT (CIRP) | IBC 2016 |
| Cheque dishonour | Criminal court (Magistrate) | NI Act § 138 |

### SARFAESI Timeline

| Step | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Account classified NPA | 90 days overdue (RBI norms) |
| § 13(2) demand notice served | Day 0 |
| Borrower reply / objection | Within 15 days of notice |
| Creditor response to objection | Within 15 days of objection |
| Expiry of notice period | Day 60 |
| Creditor takes possession / CMM/DM order | Post Day 60 |
| Borrower challenge before DRT | Within 45 days of possession |
| DRT appeal to DRAT | Within 30 days of DRT order |

### Priority of Payment in IBC Liquidation (§ 53)

| Priority | Creditor Category |
|---|---|
| 1 | Insolvency resolution and liquidation costs |
| 2 | Workmen dues (24 months) + secured creditors |
| 3 | Other employee dues (12 months) |
| 4 | Government dues (2 years) |
| 5 | Unsecured financial creditors |
| 6 | Government dues beyond priority period; remaining debts |
| 7 | Preference shareholders |
| 8 | Equity shareholders |
