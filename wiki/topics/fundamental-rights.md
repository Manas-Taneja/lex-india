## Definition

Fundamental Rights are a set of basic rights guaranteed to all citizens (and in some cases all persons) of India by Part III of the Constitution of India (Articles 12–35). They are enforceable directly against the State — any law or executive action violating them can be struck down by courts. They represent the core of individual liberty against state power.

## Current Law

**Constitution of India, Part III — Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)**

| Article | Right |
|---------|-------|
| 14 | Equality before law |
| 15 | Prohibition of discrimination (religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth) |
| 16 | Equality of opportunity in public employment |
| 17 | Abolition of untouchability |
| 18 | Abolition of titles |
| 19 | Six freedoms (speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession) |
| 20 | Protection in respect of conviction for offences (no ex post facto, no double jeopardy, no self-incrimination) |
| 21 | Protection of life and personal liberty |
| 21A | Right to education (6–14 years) |
| 22 | Protection against arbitrary arrest and detention |
| 23 | Prohibition of traffic in human beings and forced labour |
| 24 | Prohibition of child labour (under 14 in factories/mines/hazardous employment) |
| 25–28 | Freedom of religion |
| 29–30 | Cultural and educational rights of minorities |
| 32 | Right to constitutional remedies (Dr. Ambedkar: "heart and soul of the Constitution") |

## Key Provisions

**Article 21 (most expansively interpreted):** "No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law." The Supreme Court has read into this: right to privacy (K.S. Puttaswamy, 2017), right to livelihood, right to health, right to a clean environment, right to speedy trial, right to legal aid, right to dignity.

**Article 19 freedoms** are available only to citizens and subject to reasonable restrictions (Articles 19(2)–19(6)): public order, decency/morality, sovereignty/integrity, defamation, incitement to offence, relations with foreign states, professional qualifications, etc.

**Article 14 (equality):** Permits classification but forbids arbitrary classification. Test: intelligible differentia + rational nexus with the object of the law.

**Article 32:** Gives every person the right to move the Supreme Court directly for enforcement of fundamental rights. Writs available: habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, certiorari, quo warranto.

**State (Article 12):** Fundamental rights apply against the "State" — defined broadly to include Parliament, state legislatures, all governments (central/state), local authorities, and other authorities under government control (instrumentalities of the State).

**Suspension:** Article 19 rights can be suspended during a national emergency (Article 352). Article 20 and 21 cannot be suspended even during emergency (44th Amendment, 1978).

**Amendment:** Fundamental rights can be amended by Parliament under Article 368, but cannot be abrogated or abridged so as to damage the "basic structure" of the Constitution (Kesavananda Bharati, 1973).
