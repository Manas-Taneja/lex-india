## Definition

An Income Tax Return (ITR) is the form filed by a taxpayer with the Income Tax Department declaring total income earned during a financial year, deductions claimed, taxes paid, and taxes owed or refundable. Filing an ITR is mandatory for persons whose income exceeds the basic exemption limit, and voluntary for others seeking a refund or maintaining financial records.

## Current Law

**Income Tax Act, 1961** — primary legislation. Rules under Income Tax Rules, 1962. Administered by Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) under Ministry of Finance.

- **§ 139** — Obligation to file return of income
- **§ 139A** — Permanent Account Number (PAN)
- **§ 139B** — Tax Return Preparer scheme
- **§ 140** — Return by whom to be signed/verified
- **§ 143** — Assessment procedure (scrutiny assessment)
- **§ 234F** — Fee for late filing of return

## Key Provisions

**Who must file (§ 139(1)):**
- Every individual/HUF/firm/company whose income exceeds the basic exemption limit
- Basic exemption limits (FY 2024-25, Old Regime): ₹2.5 lakh (general), ₹3 lakh (senior citizen 60-80), ₹5 lakh (super senior 80+)
- Basic exemption (New Regime, § 115BAC): ₹3 lakh for all
- Mandatory regardless of income: companies, firms, persons claiming treaty benefits, those with foreign assets/income, those with signing authority in foreign accounts

**Due dates (§ 139(1)):**
- Individuals/HUFs (non-audit): 31 July of the assessment year
- Businesses requiring audit: 31 October
- Transfer pricing cases: 30 November

**Late filing (§ 139(4)):** Belated return can be filed up to 31 December of the assessment year. Fee under § 234F:
- ₹5,000 if filed after due date but before 31 December
- ₹1,000 if total income does not exceed ₹5 lakh

**Revised return (§ 139(5)):** Can be filed to correct mistakes, up to 31 December of the assessment year (or completion of assessment, whichever is earlier).

**ITR forms (notified by CBDT annually):**
- ITR-1 (Sahaj): Salaried individuals, income up to ₹50 lakh, one house property
- ITR-2: Individuals/HUFs with capital gains, foreign income/assets, more than one house
- ITR-3: Individuals/HUFs with business/professional income
- ITR-4 (Sugam): Presumptive income (§ 44AD/44ADA/44AE) up to ₹50 lakh
- ITR-5/6/7: Firms, companies, trusts

**New vs Old Tax Regime (§ 115BAC):** From FY 2023-24, the new regime is the default. Taxpayers can opt for the old regime (with deductions like 80C, HRA, LTA) by filing a declaration. New regime offers lower slab rates but most deductions are unavailable.

**Advance tax (§ 207-219):** Taxpayers with estimated tax liability above ₹10,000 must pay advance tax in four instalments (June 15, September 15, December 15, March 15). Interest under §§ 234B/234C for shortfall.
