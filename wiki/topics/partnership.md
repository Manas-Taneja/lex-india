## Definition

A partnership is a relation between persons who have agreed to share the profits of a business carried on by all or any of them acting for all. In India, general partnerships are governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932 (IPA). Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs) — a hybrid between partnership and company — are governed by the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008. Unlike a company, a general partnership firm has no separate legal personality; partners are personally and jointly liable for firm debts.

## Current Law

**Indian Partnership Act, 1932**

- **§ 4** — Definition of partnership: relation between persons who agree to share profits of a business; carried on by all or any acting for all; minimum 2 partners; maximum 50 (for banking: 10)
- **§ 6** — Determining existence of partnership: sharing of profits is evidence but not conclusive; receipt of share in property, debt payment by installments, servant's wages linked to profits, widow's annuity — none of these alone constitutes partnership
- **§ 8** — Partnership at will: where no fixed term — either partner may dissolve by notice in writing
- **§ 9** — General duties: render true accounts, give full information, act in good faith
- **§ 11** — Implied authority of partner: in the ordinary course of business, every partner is agent of the firm and can bind the firm
- **§ 19** — Restriction on implied authority: act outside ordinary business, opening new accounts, admitting liability in suit, compromising — requires express authority
- **§ 25** — Liability of partners: each partner jointly liable with all other partners and the firm for all acts done while a partner
- **§ 26** — Liability for wrongful acts: partners jointly and severally liable for loss caused by wrongful act or omission of a partner acting in ordinary course of business
- **§ 30** — Minors in partnership: minor cannot be a partner but may be admitted to benefits of partnership with consent of all partners; minor's share is liable for firm acts; on attaining majority, minor must elect within 6 months to become partner or not
- **§ 32** — Expulsion of partner: only if the power exists in the partnership deed and is exercised bona fide in the interest of the firm
- **§ 37** — Rights of outgoing partner: share of profits attributable to use of their share since retirement or interest at 6% per annum
- **§ 39-44** — Dissolution: by agreement, by notice (partnership at will), on death/insolvency/retirement of partner, by court order (misconduct, persistent breach, just and equitable)
- **§ 45** — Liability after dissolution: partners remain liable for acts done before dissolution notice is received by third party
- **§ 58-65** — Registration of firm: registration with Registrar of Firms optional but unregistered firm cannot sue third parties or co-partners to enforce contract rights

**Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008**

- **§ 3** — LLP is a body corporate — separate legal personality; perpetual succession
- **§ 8** — Partners not personally liable for wrongful acts of other partners (unlike general partnership)
- **§ 13** — LLP agreement governs rights and duties of partners
- **§ 27** — LLP must file annual return and Statement of Account and Solvency
- **§ 30** — Criminal liability: partner/designated partner liable for fraud
- **§ 34-35** — Winding up: voluntary or by tribunal; similar to company winding up

## Key Provisions

**Types of partnership:**

| Type | Feature | Governed by |
|------|---------|-------------|
| General partnership | Unlimited personal liability; no separate legal identity | IPA 1932 |
| LLP | Separate legal entity; partners' liability limited to agreed contribution | LLP Act 2008 |
| Limited partnership | Not recognised separately in India — only LLP and general partnership |  |

**Registration — effect of non-registration:**

An unregistered firm **cannot**:
- Sue third parties to enforce a right arising from contract
- Sue a co-partner to enforce a right from contract
- Claim set-off exceeding ₹100 in a suit by a third party

Third parties can still sue an unregistered firm. Registration does not affect liability to third parties.

**Implied vs actual authority:**

| Authority | Scope | Example |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| Implied (§ 11) | Ordinary course of business | Buying/selling goods, receiving payments |
| Restricted (§ 19) | Requires express authority | Opening new bank account, compromising suits |
| Partnership deed provision | Can expand or restrict § 11 defaults | Wholesale only, no credit beyond ₹X |

**Dissolution by court (§ 44):** Court may dissolve on:
- Partner of unsound mind
- Partner permanently incapable of performing duties
- Partner guilty of conduct likely to prejudicially affect business
- Partner willfully/persistently breaching partnership agreement
- Business can only be carried on at a loss
- Just and equitable grounds

**Goodwill on dissolution (§ 55):** On dissolution, goodwill is part of firm assets; sold to highest bidder. Outgoing/remaining partners may agree not to compete — such restriction is enforceable if reasonable in time and area.

**LLP vs company:**

| Feature | LLP | Private Company |
|---------|-----|----------------|
| Separate legal entity | Yes | Yes |
| Partners/members liability | Limited to contribution | Limited to share capital |
| Audit required | Only if turnover > ₹40L or contribution > ₹25L | Mandatory always |
| Directors/Board | No — designated partners | Board of Directors |
| Foreign investment | Restricted sectors — RBI permission needed | FDI policy applies |
