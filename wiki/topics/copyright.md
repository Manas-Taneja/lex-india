# Copyright

## Definition
Copyright is the exclusive legal right granted to the creator of an original work to control its reproduction, distribution, adaptation, and public communication. In India it is governed by the Copyright Act 1957, substantially amended in 2012 to align with the WIPO Internet Treaties and strengthen performers' rights.

## Current Law

### Copyright Act 1957 (as amended 2012)

- § 13 — works in which copyright subsists: original literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works; cinematograph films; sound recordings
- § 2(d) — definition of "author" (includes composer, photographer, producer of sound recording, etc.)
- § 14 — exclusive rights of the copyright owner (reproduce, issue copies, perform, communicate to public, adapt, translate)
- § 17 — first owner of copyright is the author; employer owns copyright in works made in the course of employment
- § 22 — term for literary, dramatic, musical, artistic works: life of author + 60 years
- § 26 — term for cinematograph films: 60 years from year of publication
- § 27 — term for sound recordings: 60 years from year of publication
- § 57 — moral rights: right of attribution and right of integrity; subsist even after assignment; cannot be waived or transferred
- §§ 30–34 — assignment: must be in writing signed by assignor; must specify works, rights assigned, and royalties; term and territorial extent must be stated
- § 38A — performers' rights: performers have exclusive rights to broadcast, record, and communicate their performances; term 50 years
- § 52 — fair dealing exceptions: criticism or review, reporting current events, research or private study, education, judicial proceedings
- § 63 — criminal liability for wilful infringement: minimum 6 months imprisonment and fine (up to 2 lakh for first offence); enhanced penalties for repeat offenders
- § 65A — protection of technological protection measures (DRM): circumvention prohibited; exceptions for interoperability and security research
- § 65B — rights management information: removal or alteration of RMI is an offence

### Copyright Board / IPAB / High Court

- Copyright Board handled licence disputes and compulsory licence applications under §§ 31–31D
- Intellectual Property Appellate Board (IPAB) took over appellate functions; IPAB abolished by the Tribunals Reforms Act 2021
- Post-abolition, appeals and rectifications lie directly before the High Court

## Key Provisions

### Duration of Copyright

| Category | Term |
|---|---|
| Literary / dramatic / musical / artistic | Life of author + 60 years |
| Anonymous or pseudonymous works | 60 years from publication |
| Cinematograph films | 60 years from publication |
| Sound recordings | 60 years from publication |
| Government works | 60 years from publication |
| Posthumous works | 60 years from publication |

### Fair Dealing Exceptions (§ 52)

| Purpose | Conditions |
|---|---|
| Research or private study | Must be non-commercial |
| Criticism or review | Source must be acknowledged |
| Reporting current events | Reasonable extent |
| Education (teaching / examination) | Not for sale |
| Judicial or legislative proceedings | Permitted without restriction |

### Assignment Requirements (§§ 30–34)

- Must be in writing and signed by the assignor or authorised agent
- Must identify the specific works and rights being assigned
- Must specify the term and territorial extent (defaults to 5 years / India if unspecified)
- Royalty terms must be stated; if not, Copyright Board (now HC) can determine fair royalty
- Unilateral reversion: if assignee fails to exercise rights within 1 year, assignment may be terminated

## International Framework

- India is a member of the Berne Convention (joined 1928): protection automatic on creation, no registration required
- TRIPS Agreement (WTO 1994): minimum standards including 50-year term (India provides 60 years)
- Universal Copyright Convention (UCC)
- Rome Convention: performers' and producers' rights
- India has not ratified WIPO Copyright Treaty (WCT) or WIPO Performances and Phonograms Treaty (WPPT) but 2012 amendments domestically implement their key provisions
