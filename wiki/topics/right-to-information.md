## Definition

The Right to Information Act, 2005 (RTI Act) gives every citizen the right to access information held by public authorities. Information includes records, documents, memos, emails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data material held in any electronic form. The Act applies to all public authorities — Union Government, State Governments, local bodies, and substantially funded NGOs.

## Current Law

**Right to Information Act, 2005**

- **§ 2(f)** — Information: any material in any form including records, documents, memos, emails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data material held in any electronic form
- **§ 2(h)** — Public authority: any authority/body/institution of self-government established or constituted by the Constitution, Parliament, State Legislature, notification, or substantially funded by government
- **§ 2(j)** — Right to information: includes inspection of work, documents, records; taking notes, extracts, or certified copies; taking certified samples; obtaining information in form of diskettes, floppies, tapes, video cassettes
- **§ 4** — Obligations of public authorities: proactive disclosure — every public authority shall publish within 120 days: particulars of organisation, functions, powers, duties, officers, rules, regulations, policies, budget, execution plans
- **§ 6** — Request for information: written/electronic application to Public Information Officer (PIO) in English/Hindi/official language of area; no reason for seeking information required
- **§ 7** — Disposal of request: information to be provided within 30 days of receipt (48 hours where life or liberty of person is involved); fee for information (₹10 application fee; ₹2/page for copy)
- **§ 8** — Exemptions: information affecting sovereignty/security/strategic interests; information received in confidence from foreign government; information undermining investigation; Cabinet papers and deliberations; trade secrets and intellectual property where disclosure harms competitive position; personal information with no public activity or interest
- **§ 9** — Rejection of request: PIO may reject if the request is in respect of information held by a third party in a fiduciary capacity, or is exempt under § 8
- **§ 10** — Severability: if exempt information can be severed from non-exempt information, the non-exempt part must be provided
- **§ 11** — Third party information: PIO must notify third party before disclosing information supplied in confidence by that party; third party may object within 10 days
- **§ 12-14** — Central Information Commission (CIC): Chief Information Commissioner + up to 10 Information Commissioners; appointed by President on recommendation of committee chaired by PM
- **§ 15-16** — State Information Commissions: mirror structure at state level
- **§ 18** — Powers of CIC/SIC: powers of civil court; receive and inquire into complaints against PIOs; impose penalties
- **§ 19** — Appeals: First appeal to officer senior to PIO within 30 days of rejection/non-response; Second appeal to Information Commission within 90 days of first appeal order
- **§ 20** — Penalties: CIC/SIC may impose penalty of ₹250/day (maximum ₹25,000) on PIO for unjustified delay or denial; penalty from PIO's own salary; recommendation for departmental action for malafide denial

## Key Provisions

**Who can file RTI:**
- Any citizen of India (not companies/bodies, only individuals)
- No reason or justification required
- Application in writing/electronic in English, Hindi, or local official language

**Timeline:**

| Stage | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| Response by PIO | 30 days |
| Response — life/liberty matters | 48 hours |
| First appeal order | 30 days (extendable to 45 days) |
| Second appeal (Information Commission) | 90 days from first appeal order |

**Exemptions under § 8 — key ones:**

| Exemption | Condition |
|-----------|-----------|
| Security, sovereignty, intelligence | Absolute |
| Cabinet deliberations | Until decision is taken; thereafter — facts and reasons disclosed |
| Personal information | If no public activity or interest; or would cause unwarranted invasion of privacy |
| Trade secrets | Only if disclosure would harm competitive position |
| Investigations | Until investigation complete |
| Third-party confidential information | Disclosure only after notice and 10-day window for objection |

**Public interest override (§ 8(2)):** Notwithstanding any of the exemptions, information may be disclosed if the public interest in disclosure outweighs the harm to protected interests — applies to all exemptions except sovereignty and security of State.

**Deemed refusal:** If PIO fails to respond within 30 days (or 48 hours for life/liberty), it is treated as rejection — first appeal lies immediately.

**Proactive disclosure (§ 4):** Public authority must suo motu publish 17 categories of information on its website, including budget, plans, expenditure, names and designations of PIOs. RTI filings are reduced where § 4 disclosure is robust.

**RTI and judiciary:** Chief Justice of India's office was held to be a public authority (CPIO Supreme Court v Subhash Chandra Agarwal, 2019 — Constitution Bench). Information about assets of judges, appointment process, and collegium recommendations is subject to RTI with appropriate redactions.

**RTI and private entities:** RTI does not apply to private companies/individuals unless they are substantially funded by government. NGOs receiving significant government grants are covered.

**2019 Amendment — CIC tenure change:** The RTI (Amendment) Act, 2019 removed the fixed 5-year terms and fixed salaries for the CIC and Information Commissioners — terms and salaries now prescribed by Central Government. Critics argue this reduces independence.
