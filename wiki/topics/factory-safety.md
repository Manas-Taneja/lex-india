# Factory Safety

## Definition
The Factories Act 1948 regulates health, safety, and welfare conditions in factories, setting mandatory standards for working hours, leave entitlements, machinery safety, and worker amenities to protect the physical well-being of industrial workers.

## Current Law

### Factories Act 1948

- § 2(m) — "factory": premises where ≥10 workers are employed with the aid of power, or ≥20 workers without power, on any day in the preceding 12 months
- § 6 — approval and licensing: factory must be approved by Chief Inspector before construction/extension; annual licence required
- § 7 — notice: occupier must send written notice to Chief Inspector before occupying or using factory

### Chapter III — Health (§§ 11–20)

- § 11 — cleanliness: factory kept clean; walls/ceilings washed/painted as prescribed
- § 13 — ventilation and temperature: adequate ventilation; temperature comfortable for workers
- § 14 — dust and fume control: effective measures to prevent inhalation
- § 15 — artificial humidification: standards for humidity in textile factories
- § 16 — overcrowding: minimum 14.2 m³ space per worker; 4.2 m³ for older factories
- § 18 — drinking water: adequate supply of wholesome drinking water at suitable points
- § 19 — latrines and urinals: sufficient number; separate for male and female workers

### Chapter IV — Safety (§§ 21–41)

- § 21 — fencing of machinery: all dangerous machinery must be securely fenced
- § 22 — work on or near machinery in motion: only trained/certified adult male workers
- § 28 — hoists and lifts: examination every 6 months; safe working load marked
- § 31 — pressure vessels: examined and tested as prescribed; safety valves required
- § 38 — precautions against fire: fire exits, fire-fighting equipment, fire drills
- § 40A — safety officers: factories with ≥1,000 workers must appoint safety officers

### Chapter V — Welfare (§§ 42–50)

- § 42 — washing facilities: adequate and suitable; separate for male and female
- § 44 — sitting arrangements: suitable facilities for occasional sitting
- § 45 — first-aid: at least 1 first-aid box per 150 workers; ambulance room for ≥500 workers
- § 46 — canteen: compulsory canteen for factories with ≥250 workers
- § 48 — crèches: where ≥30 women workers employed; must provide crèche with prescribed amenities
- § 49 — welfare officers: factories with ≥500 workers must appoint welfare officers

### Chapter VI — Working Hours (§§ 51–66)

- § 51 — weekly hours: maximum 48 hours per week
- § 54 — daily hours: maximum 9 hours per day
- § 55 — intervals for rest: at least 30 minutes after 5 hours of work
- § 56 — spread over: total period (including intervals) shall not exceed 10.5 hours
- § 59 — overtime: hours beyond 9/day or 48/week; overtime wages = 2× ordinary rate
- § 64 — exemption: state government may exempt; even so, maximum OT ≤ 50 hours per quarter (§ 65(3))
- § 66 — restriction on women: women shall not work before 6 AM or after 7 PM (state may vary)

### Chapter VIII — Annual Leave with Wages (§ 79)

- § 79 — leave entitlement: 1 day of paid leave for every 20 days of work in the previous calendar year (adult); 1 day per 15 days for workers under 15 years
- Carry forward: up to 30 days of unutilised leave may be carried forward
- Leave encashment on discharge/dismissal/quit/superannuation

## Key Provisions

### Working Hours Summary

| Parameter | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Maximum weekly hours | 48 hours |
| Maximum daily hours | 9 hours |
| Spread over (including breaks) | 10.5 hours |
| Maximum overtime per quarter | 50 hours |
| Overtime rate | 2× ordinary wages |
| Rest interval | ≥ 30 min after 5 hours continuous work |

### Welfare Facility Thresholds

| Facility | Worker Threshold |
|----------|-----------------|
| Canteen | ≥ 250 workers |
| Crèche | ≥ 30 women workers |
| Welfare officer | ≥ 500 workers |
| Safety officer | ≥ 1,000 workers |
| Ambulance room | ≥ 500 workers |
