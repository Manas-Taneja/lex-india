# Capital Gains Tax

## Definition
Capital gains tax is levied under the Income Tax Act 1961 on profits arising from the transfer of a capital asset. The tax rate and holding period classification depend on the nature and type of the asset, with distinct treatment for financial assets, immovable property, and business assets.

## Current Law

### Income Tax Act 1961 (§§ 45–55A)

- § 2(14) — **capital asset**: any property of any kind held by an assessee; excludes stock-in-trade, consumable stores, personal effects (jewellery is included), rural agricultural land
- § 2(42A) — **short-term capital asset** (STCA): held for ≤36 months; ≤24 months for immovable property and unlisted shares (w.e.f. AY 2018-19); ≤12 months for listed equity shares, equity-oriented mutual funds, and specified securities
- § 45 — charging section: capital gains are chargeable as income of the year in which the transfer takes place
- § 47 — transactions not regarded as transfer: gift, will, inheritance (§ 47(iii)); hence no capital gains on the donor/deceased at the time of gift/inheritance
- § 49 — **cost of previous owner**: where asset acquired by gift or inheritance, cost to the previous owner (who had paid for it) is adopted as the cost of acquisition for the current holder
- § 50 — **depreciable assets**: if asset is part of a block of assets, no LTCG/STCG distinction; entire deemed gain is short-term capital gain regardless of holding period
- § 50B — **slump sale**: transfer of a whole undertaking for a lump sum without assigning values to individual assets; deemed LTCG/STCG based on holding period of undertaking; net worth is the cost of acquisition
- § 50C — **full value of consideration for immovable property**: if stamp duty value (circle rate) exceeds sale consideration, stamp duty value is deemed to be the full value of consideration; applies to seller
- § 55(2)(b) — **fair market value as on 1 April 2001**: for assets acquired before 1 April 2001, assessee may opt to substitute the FMV on that date as the cost of acquisition (grandfathering)

### Tax Rates

- § 111A — **STCG on listed equity/equity MFs**: 15% (flat); applicable where STT has been paid on transfer; surcharge and cess extra; w.e.f. Budget 2024, rate increased to 20% (applicable from 23 July 2024)
- § 112A — **LTCG on listed equity/equity MFs**: 10% (flat) on gains exceeding ₹1,00,000 in a year; no indexation benefit; applicable to transfers after 31 January 2018; grandfathering: cost deemed to be higher of actual cost or FMV as on 31 January 2018; w.e.f. Budget 2024, rate increased to 12.5%, exemption threshold raised to ₹1.25L
- § 112 — **LTCG on other assets** (property, unlisted shares, debt MFs, gold, etc.): 20% with indexation benefit (Cost Inflation Index applied to cost of acquisition and improvement); **post-Budget 2024 (w.e.f. 23 July 2024)**: rate reduced to 12.5% without indexation for assets other than listed securities; taxpayers may choose the more favourable option for property acquired before 23 July 2024 under transitional provisions

## Key Provisions

### Holding Period Classification
| Asset Type | Short-Term (STCA) | Long-Term (LTCA) |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Listed equity shares / equity MFs | ≤12 months | >12 months |
| Immovable property / unlisted shares | ≤24 months | >24 months |
| Other capital assets (gold, bonds, etc.) | ≤36 months | >36 months |
| Debt mutual funds (post Apr 2023) | Always short-term regardless of holding |

### Capital Gains Exemptions
| Section | Exemption | Condition |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| § 54 | Reinvestment in residential house | Seller must be individual/HUF; LTCG from residential property; invest in 1 new house within 1 year before / 2 years after (or construct within 3 years) |
| § 54B | Agricultural land | Individual/HUF; LTCG/STCG from agricultural land; reinvest in agricultural land within 2 years |
| § 54EC | Investment in specified bonds | LTCG from any long-term asset; invest in REC/NHAI bonds within 6 months; max ₹50L; lock-in 5 years |
| § 54EE | Investment in notified funds | LTCG from any asset; max ₹50L; invest in notified fund (e.g., start-up fund) within 6 months |
| § 54F | Residential house (other assets) | Individual/HUF; LTCG from any asset other than residential house; net consideration must be invested in residential house; not available if more than 1 house already owned |

### Computation Notes
- **Indexation**: Cost Inflation Index (CII) published annually by CBDT; adjusted cost = actual cost × (CII of year of sale ÷ CII of year of acquisition); base year is 2001-02 (CII = 100)
- **Cost of improvement**: expenditure incurred on structural improvements (not repairs) is added to cost; also eligible for indexation
- **Set-off**: STCL can be set off against STCG or LTCG; LTCL can only be set off against LTCG; unabsorbed capital losses carried forward for 8 assessment years (return must be filed on time)
