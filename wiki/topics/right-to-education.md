# Right to Education

## Definition
The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009 (RTE Act) operationalises Article 21A of the Constitution, which was inserted by the 86th Constitutional Amendment in 2002, making free and compulsory education a fundamental right for every child aged 6 to 14 years.

## Current Law

### Constitution of India
- Article 21A — Free and compulsory education for all children aged 6–14 as a fundamental right (inserted by 86th Amendment Act 2002)

### Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009
- § 3 — Every child of age 6–14 years has the right to free and compulsory elementary education in a neighbourhood school
- § 8 — Duties of the appropriate government: ensure availability of school, trained teachers, maintenance of standards
- § 12(1)(c) — Private unaided non-minority schools must admit at least 25% of their class intake from weaker sections and disadvantaged groups; government to reimburse
- § 16 — No detention policy: no child shall be held back (detained) or expelled from school until completion of elementary education (amended 2019: classes 5 and 8 may hold examinations; students may be held back once if they fail, after being given remedial instruction and a re-examination opportunity)
- § 17 — Prohibition of physical punishment and mental harassment: any person who contravenes this provision shall be liable to disciplinary action under applicable service rules
- § 18 — No school to be established or function without obtaining a certificate of recognition from the prescribed authority
- § 19 + Schedule — Infrastructure and staffing norms that a school must fulfil within three years of the Act's commencement
- § 23 — Teachers to possess minimum qualifications as laid down by academic authority (Teacher Eligibility Test — TET); no unqualified person to be appointed as teacher after commencement of this Act
- § 28 — No teacher shall engage in private tuition or private teaching activity
- § 29 — Curriculum and evaluation procedure to be laid down by academic authority; to conform to constitutional values and be based on all-round development of the child, learning through activities, discovery, exploration
- § 31 — National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) to monitor children's right to education; State Commissions for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR) at state level

## Key Provisions

### Infrastructure Norms (§ 19 + Schedule)
| Parameter | Primary (Classes I–V) | Upper Primary (Classes VI–VIII) |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------------------------|
| Pupil–Teacher Ratio (PTR) | 30:1 | 35:1 |
| Working days (minimum) | 200 days | 220 days |
| Working hours (minimum) | 800 hours/year | 1000 hours/year |
| All-weather school building | Required | Required |
| Separate toilet for girls | Required | Required |
| Safe drinking water | Required | Required |
| Playground | Required | Required |

### 25% Reservation Mechanism (§ 12(1)(c))
| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Applicable schools | Private unaided non-minority schools |
| Quota | 25% of seats in Class I (or pre-school entry class) |
| Beneficiaries | Children from weaker sections and disadvantaged groups |
| Reimbursement | Government pays school: lower of per-child expenditure or actual school fee per child |
| Exemption | Minority institutions (§ 1(5)), unaided schools receiving no government aid? subject to SC ruling |

### Amendments — No Detention Policy (2019)
| Aspect | Pre-2019 | Post-2019 Amendment |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| Classes I–IV | No detention | No detention |
| Classes V and VIII | No detention | Examination held; one chance for re-exam after remedial instruction; may be detained once on persistent failure |
| Class IX onwards | Not covered by RTE Act | Not covered |

## Landmark Cases
- **Society for Unaided Private Schools of Rajasthan v Union of India (SC 2012):** Upheld constitutional validity of § 12(1)(c); held that the 25% reservation applies to unaided non-minority schools; minority schools exempted under Article 30.
- **Ashok Kumar Thakur v Union of India (SC 2008):** While on OBC reservations, the Court affirmed that Article 21A read with Article 45 (now Article 21A) imposes a positive obligation on the State.
