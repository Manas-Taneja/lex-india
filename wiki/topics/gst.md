# Goods and Services Tax (GST)

## Definition
GST is a unified, destination-based indirect tax on the supply of goods and services, replacing a patchwork of central and state levies. India follows a dual GST model where both the Centre (CGST) and States (SGST/UTGST) levy tax concurrently on intra-state supplies, while IGST applies to inter-state supplies.

## Current Law

### Constitution (101st Amendment) Act 2016
- Inserted Art. 246A — concurrent power to legislate on GST for Centre and States
- Inserted Art. 279A — constitutes the GST Council (Union Finance Minister as Chairperson)
- Abolished CST and subsumed central excise, service tax, VAT, entry tax

### Central Goods and Services Tax Act 2017
- § 9 — levy of CGST; § 9(3) reverse charge on notified supplies; § 9(4) reverse charge on purchases from unregistered persons (notified categories)
- § 10 — composition scheme: turnover up to ₹1.5 Cr; restricted to intra-state, no ITC
- § 16 — eligibility conditions for input tax credit (ITC): tax invoice, receipt of goods/services, tax paid by supplier, return filed
- § 17 — blocked credits: motor vehicles (with exceptions), food/beverages, club memberships, works contract for immovable property
- § 22 — mandatory registration: aggregate turnover exceeds ₹20L (₹10L for special category states)
- §§ 51-52 — TDS (1%) by notified government entities; TCS (up to 1%) by e-commerce operators
- § 73 — demand for non-fraud cases: show-cause + 3-year limitation
- § 74 — demand for fraud/suppression cases: 5-year limitation; penalty up to 100% of tax

### IGST Act 2017
- § 5 — levy of IGST on inter-state supply; administered by Centre but apportioned to consuming state
- § 13 — place of supply rules for services

## Key Provisions

### GST Rate Schedule
| Rate | Examples |
|------|---------|
| 0% (exempt) | Fresh produce, milk, unbranded food grains, healthcare, education |
| 5% | Packaged food, footwear ≤₹1000, transport services |
| 12% | Processed food, computers, business class air travel |
| 18% | Most services, electronics, capital goods |
| 28% | Luxury goods, automobiles, tobacco, aerated drinks |

### GST Returns
| Return | Purpose | Frequency |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| GSTR-1 | Outward supplies (sales) | Monthly / Quarterly (QRMP) |
| GSTR-3B | Summary return + tax payment | Monthly |
| GSTR-9 | Annual return | Annually |
| GSTR-9C | Reconciliation statement | Annually (if turnover > ₹5 Cr) |

## Compliance Infrastructure
- **GSTN** — Goods and Services Tax Network: IT backbone for registration, returns, payments
- **E-invoicing** — mandatory for B2B supplies above notified turnover threshold; IRN generated via IRP
- **E-way bill** — required for movement of goods exceeding ₹50,000 in value; generated on GSTN portal

## Dispute Resolution
Appeals proceed: Adjudicating Authority → Appellate Authority (Commissioner-level) → GST Appellate Tribunal (GSTAT, operational 2024) → High Court → Supreme Court.
