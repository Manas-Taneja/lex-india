# Child Labour

## Definition
Indian law completely prohibits the employment of children below 14 years in any occupation or process, and restricts adolescents (14–18 years) from hazardous occupations and processes, with the Child and Adolescent Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act 1986 as amended in 2016 forming the core framework.

## Current Law

### Child and Adolescent Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act 1986 (CLPRA), as amended 2016

- § 2(ii) — "child": person who has not completed 14 years of age
- § 2(ia) — "adolescent": person who has completed 14 years but not completed 18 years
- § 3 — prohibition of employment of children: no child shall be employed or permitted to work in any establishment or any occupation
  - Exception (§ 3(2)): child may work in family/household enterprise not involving hazardous processes; child artist in audio-visual entertainment (not live performance during school hours; max 5 hrs/day; no night shifts)
- § 3A — prohibition of employment of adolescents in hazardous occupations/processes: no adolescent shall be employed in any occupation or process listed in the Schedule (includes mines, explosives, hazardous chemicals, construction)
- § 4 — Schedule of hazardous occupations/processes for adolescents: central government may add to Schedule
- § 14 — penalties:
  - First offence: imprisonment 6 months to 2 years, fine ₹20,000–₹50,000
  - Subsequent offence: imprisonment 1–3 years
  - Parent/guardian who allows child to work: fine up to ₹10,000 (unless coercion proved)
- § 14A — district magistrate as nodal authority for enforcement and rescue
- § 14C — child welfare committees to be involved for rescued children
- § 17 — inspectors: appointment and powers of inspection
- CLPR Rules 2017: procedure for inspection, rescue, rehabilitation, repatriation

### Interaction with RTE Act 2009

- Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009: every child 6–14 years entitled to free and compulsory education
- Child employed in violation of CLPRA is simultaneously denied the right to education under RTE
- RTE § 21: School Management Committees must identify out-of-school children including child labourers
- Convergence: rescued child labourers to be mainstreamed into schools; National Child Labour Project (NCLP) provides bridge education

## Key Provisions

### Age-Based Prohibition Matrix

| Age Group | Employment Status |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Below 14 years (child) | Complete prohibition in all occupations and processes |
| 14–18 years (adolescent) | Permitted in non-hazardous work; prohibited in Schedule occupations/processes |
| 18 years and above | Full adult labour laws apply |

### Permitted Exceptions for Children (under § 3(2))

| Exception | Conditions |
|-----------|-----------|
| Family/household enterprise | Not involving hazardous process; not during school hours |
| Entertainment industry (audio-visual) | Not during school hours; max 5 hours per day; no night work; no hazard |

### Penalty Structure

| Offence | Imprisonment | Fine |
|---------|-------------|------|
| First offence (employer) | 6 months – 2 years | ₹20,000 – ₹50,000 |
| Subsequent offence | 1 – 3 years | As above |
| Parent/guardian (permitting) | None | Up to ₹10,000 |

### Hazardous Occupations/Processes (Schedule — selected)

- Mines (underground and surface)
- Inflammable substance/explosive manufacture
- Hazardous chemical processes
- Construction and demolition
- Manufacture of beedis, carpets, matchsticks, fireworks
- Printing presses (treadle-operated machinery)
- Slaughterhouses
