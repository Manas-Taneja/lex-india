## Definition

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) regulates the real estate sector to protect homebuyers and promote transparency and accountability. RERA requires mandatory registration of real estate projects and agents, imposes obligations on promoters to disclose project details, mandates deposit of 70% of collections into a dedicated escrow account, and establishes Real Estate Regulatory Authorities (RERAs) in each State. Homebuyers can file complaints before RERA authority and appeal to Real Estate Appellate Tribunal (REAT).

## Current Law

**Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016**

- **§ 2(zk)** — Promoter: person who constructs/causes to be constructed apartment/building/real estate project for sale; includes developer, builder, housing board
- **§ 2(zn)** — Real estate project: development of buildings, apartments, plots for sale; includes development of infrastructure and amenities
- **§ 3** — Registration of real estate project: promoter must register project with RERA authority before advertising, marketing, booking, selling, or offering for sale; exemption for projects below 500 sq.m. or 8 apartments (single phase)
- **§ 4** — Application for registration: promoter must submit: approvals obtained; commencement certificate; layout plans; number of apartments; declared timeline for completion; proforma of allotment letter, agreement for sale, conveyance deed
- **§ 5** — Grant or rejection of registration: RERA must decide within 30 days; deemed granted if no order within 30 days
- **§ 6** — Extension of registration: on application with reasons; extension of up to 1 year for force majeure; promoter bears burden of proof
- **§ 7** — Revocation: where promoter breaches obligations; violates Act/Rules/Regulations; engages in fraudulent conduct
- **§ 9** — Real estate agent registration: agents must register with RERA; cannot facilitate sale of unregistered project
- **§ 11** — Functions and duties of promoter: disclose project details on RERA website; update quarterly; make available all approvals; provide authenticated copy of all approvals to allottee
- **§ 13** — No deposit or advance before agreement for sale: promoter cannot take more than 10% of unit cost as advance/application fee before executing registered agreement for sale
- **§ 14** — Adherence to approved plans: promoter cannot make material alterations without 2/3 consent of allottees; must complete project as per approved plans and specifications
- **§ 16** — Separate account (escrow): 70% of amounts realised for each project must be deposited in a dedicated bank account; used only for that project's land cost and construction cost; withdrawal after certification by engineer/architect/CA
- **§ 17** — Transfer of title: promoter must execute registered conveyance deed in favour of allottee within 3 months of issuance of occupancy certificate or possession, whichever is earlier
- **§ 18** — Return of amount and compensation: if promoter fails to complete or hand over possession by agreed date, allottee may withdraw and receive full refund + interest; or continue and receive interest for delay period
- **§ 19** — Rights and duties of allottees: entitled to stage-wise construction schedule; entitled to seek possession; obliged to make payments on time; take possession within 2 months of occupancy certificate
- **§ 31** — Filing of complaints: allottee or any aggrieved person may file complaint with RERA authority; promoter may also file against allottee
- **§ 40** — Recovery of interest/penalty/compensation: amounts ordered by RERA authority recoverable as arrears of land revenue
- **§ 43** — Adjudicating Officer: RERA authority appoints adjudicating officer to determine compensation
- **§ 44** — Appeal to REAT (Real Estate Appellate Tribunal): within 60 days of RERA order; deposit 30% of penalty as condition for hearing appeal
- **§ 59** — Penalties on promoter: failure to register project — penalty up to 10% of project cost; continued default — imprisonment up to 3 years or further fine up to 10% of project cost
- **§ 61** — Penalties on real estate agent: up to ₹10,000/day for failure to register; maximum ₹5 lakh
- **§ 63** — Penalty for non-compliance with RERA authority order: up to 5% of estimated project cost per day

## Key Provisions

**What must be registered:**
- Real estate projects above 500 sq.m. OR 8 apartments (if smaller — exempt)
- Each phase treated as separate project for registration
- Ongoing projects (not having received completion/occupancy certificate on commencement of Act) must also register

**70% escrow rule (§ 16):**
- 70% of realised amounts must stay in project-specific account
- Withdrawal requires certification by: Civil Engineer (construction completion %), Architect (project status), Chartered Accountant (cost audit)
- Prevents diversion of homebuyer funds to other projects — the central cause of real estate fraud pre-RERA

**Allottee rights on delay (§ 18):**

| Allottee choice | Remedy |
|-----------------|--------|
| Withdraw from project | Full refund + interest (RBI rate + 2%) from date of payment till refund |
| Continue | Interest at RBI rate + 2% per month for each month of delay in possession |

**RERA and IBC — concurrent jurisdiction:**
- Homebuyers are financial creditors under IBC (2018 amendment) — can initiate CIRP against promoter
- RERA disputes and IBC proceedings can run simultaneously on different matters
- IBC moratorium does not automatically stay RERA proceedings for refund/compensation

**Key state RERA authorities:**
- MahaRERA (Maharashtra), RERA UP, HRERA (Haryana), Karnataka RERA — active enforcement
- Some states (West Bengal, J&K pre-reorganisation) had diluted implementations

**Promoter's obligation on completion:**
- Obtain occupancy/completion certificate from local authority
- Form and register residents welfare association (Residents' Welfare Association / Apartment Owners' Association) within 3 months
- Transfer common areas, amenities, land to RWA/AOA

**Related cases:**
- **Forum for People's Collective Efforts v State of West Bengal (2021 SC):** State cannot enact law diluting RERA — central legislation prevails; West Bengal Housing Industry Regulation Act struck down
- **Imperia Structures v Anil Patni (2020 SC):** Allottees may choose between RERA remedy and Consumer Forum; not mutually exclusive
