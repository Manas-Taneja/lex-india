# Adoption

## Definition
Adoption is the legal process by which a person takes another person's child as their own, creating the same rights and obligations as a biological parent-child relationship. India has two parallel adoption regimes: a personal law route (HAMA, for Hindus only) and a secular statutory route (JJ Act, applicable to all religions and for inter-country adoption).

## Current Law

### Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act 1956 (HAMA)
- § 5 — Adoptions regulated by HAMA; any adoption not complying is void
- § 6 — Requisites of valid adoption: adoptive parent must have capacity; adoptee must be capable of being taken; adoption must be actually given and taken
- § 7 — Capacity of male Hindu to adopt: must be of sound mind; if married, wife's consent required (unless wife is of unsound mind, has ceased to be Hindu, or has renounced the world)
- § 8 — Capacity of female Hindu to adopt: only an unmarried woman, widow, divorcee, or woman whose husband is of unsound mind / has renounced world / has ceased to be Hindu
- § 10 — Who may be adopted: Hindu, unmarried, not previously adopted, below 15 years (unless custom permits older); same-sex adoption requires no prior child of that sex in the family
- § 11 — Age gap: if a male adopts a female, he must be at least 21 years older; if a female adopts a male, she must be at least 21 years older
- § 12 — Effect: adopted child is deemed child of adoptive parent for all purposes from date of adoption; ties with natural family severed (with exceptions for prohibited degrees of marriage)
- § 16 — Registered deed of adoption: presumed valid unless contrary proved
- § 17 — Prohibition of payment: no payment or reward for adoption (except for legal expenses)

### Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act 2015
- §§ 56-73 — Adoption chapter; creates CARA (Central Adoption Resource Authority) under Ministry of Women and Child Development as nodal body
- § 57 — Eligibility of Prospective Adoptive Parents (PAP): married couple (married for ≥2 years), single female (for child of any gender), single male (for male child only)
- § 58 — Procedure: registration on CARINGS (Child Adoption Resource Information and Guidance System) portal; home study report; matching; pre-adoption foster care; court order
- § 60 — Adoption order by court; child becomes legal child of adoptive parents
- § 61 — Inter-Country Adoption (ICA): India party to Hague Convention on Protection of Children and Co-operation in Respect of Intercountry Adoption 1993 (Hague Convention); CARA is Central Authority; ICA only after exhausting in-country adoption options
- § 65 — Prospective NRI / OCI / foreign PAP requirements; home study by accredited agency in country of habitual residence
- JJ Act route is secular and available to persons of all religions, including those to whom HAMA does not apply (Muslims, Christians, Parsis, Jews)

### Guardians and Wards Act 1890
- § 7 — Court (District Judge) may appoint a guardian of person and/or property of a minor if it deems it for the welfare of the minor
- § 17 — Welfare of the minor is the paramount consideration
- Guardianship ≠ adoption: a guardian appointed under GWA does not acquire parental rights; the child retains ties with natural family; the guardianship ends when the child attains majority
- Applies to children of all religions where adoption is not recognised (e.g., Muslim children cannot be adopted under personal law; GWA guardianship is the alternative)

## Key Provisions

### HAMA vs JJ Act Comparison
| Feature | HAMA | JJ Act |
|---------|------|--------|
| Applicable to | Hindus, Sikhs, Jains, Buddhists | All religions |
| Minimum age of adoptee | Any (below 15 unless custom) | Below 18 (special need / older child tracks) |
| Authority | Deed + natural parent's deed of giving | CARA / court order |
| Effect on natural family ties | Severed (§ 12) | Severed (§ 60) |
| Inter-country adoption | Not permitted | Yes, via CARA (§ 61) |
| Payment prohibition | § 17 | § 80 (₹1 lakh fine or 3 years or both) |

### CARINGS Portal Process
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | PAP registers on CARINGS portal; uploads documents |
| 2 | Home Study Report (HSR) by Specialised Adoption Agency (SAA) |
| 3 | PAP declared eligible; placed in wait-list |
| 4 | Child Profile shared; PAP accepts/declines within 48 hours |
| 5 | Pre-Adoption Foster Care (PAFC) for minimum 10 days |
| 6 | Adoption petition filed in court; order within 2 months |
| 7 | Birth certificate with adoptive parents' names issued |

### RCI Certificate
Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) certificate/clearance may be required for PAPs adopting children with special needs, ensuring the family can meet the child's rehabilitation requirements.
