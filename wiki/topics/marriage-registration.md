# Marriage Registration

## Definition
Marriage registration is the official recording of a marriage with a government authority, producing a marriage certificate. Registration does not determine the validity of a marriage — an unregistered marriage is not invalid — but the certificate is required for many civil purposes.

## Current Law

### Hindu Marriage Act 1955
- § 5 — Conditions for a valid Hindu marriage: monogamy, minimum age (groom 21, bride 18), parties not within prohibited degrees of relationship, neither spouse of unsound mind
- § 7 — Ceremonies: marriage valid if solemnised per customary rites; saptapadi (seven steps) conclusive once completed
- § 8 — Registration: State Governments may make rules for registration; entry in register is evidence of marriage but absence of entry does not invalidate it
- §§ 5-9 collectively govern conditions, void marriages (§ 11) and voidable marriages (§ 12)

### Special Marriage Act 1954
- § 4 — Conditions: neither party has a living spouse, both are of sound mind and capable of consent, male ≥21, female ≥18, parties not within degrees of prohibited relationship
- §§ 15-16 — Registration procedure: notice of intended marriage filed with Marriage Officer; 30-day public notice period during which objections may be filed (§ 7); upon expiry without valid objection, marriage is solemnised and registered
- § 17 — Marriage certificate issued by Marriage Officer; conclusive proof of marriage
- § 21A — Personal law of parties unaffected for succession unless opted out

### Muslim Marriages
- No central statute mandating registration; marriage (nikah) is a civil contract requiring offer (ijab) and acceptance (qabul) before witnesses
- Nikahnama: written contract recording terms including mahr (dower); AIMPLB model nikahnama sets standard clauses
- Multiple states (e.g., West Bengal, Assam, Kerala, Bihar) have enacted state rules for voluntary or compulsory registration of Muslim marriages
- Absence of registration does not void the nikah

### Christian Marriage Act 1872
- Governs solemnisation by Ministers of Religion, Marriage Registrars, and others
- Certificate issued after solemnisation and entry in register
- Applies across India except J&K (now J&K Reorganisation Act 2019 extends it)

### Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act 1936
- Marriage (ashirvad ceremony) officiated by an Asiravad priest; registered in register maintained under the Act

## Key Provisions

### Seema v Ashwani Kumar (SC 2006)
The Supreme Court directed all State Governments and Union Territories to frame rules for compulsory registration of marriages within three months. The court held that registration, while not determinative of validity, serves social objectives including proof of age, prevention of child marriage, and protection of women's rights.

### Registration Process Comparison
| Route | Authority | Notice Period | Certificate |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Hindu Marriage Act § 8 | Sub-Registrar (state rules) | Varies by state | Issued on registration |
| Special Marriage Act | Marriage Officer | 30 days | § 17 certificate |
| Christian Marriage Act | Registrar of Marriages | None (post-solemnisation) | Church + civil register |
| Parsi Marriage Act | Registrar appointed under Act | None | Entry in Parsi register |

### Uses of Marriage Certificate
| Purpose | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Passport (spouse endorsement / name change) | Mandatory |
| Spouse visa abroad | Mandatory |
| Joint bank account / insurance nominee | Often required |
| Inheritance / succession claims | Strong evidentiary value |
| Pension / gratuity family benefit | Required by employer rules |

## Key Points
- Registration ≠ validity: an unregistered Hindu or Muslim marriage is not void solely for want of registration
- Documents typically required: proof of age, address proof of both parties, passport-size photographs, witnesses (two for Hindu Marriage Act, three for Special Marriage Act), affidavit of marital status
- Tata v State of Rajasthan and state HC decisions have affirmed that the 30-day notice period under SMA can be challenged on privacy grounds (post-Puttaswamy 2017 debate ongoing)
