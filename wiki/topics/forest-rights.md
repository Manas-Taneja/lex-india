# Forest Rights

## Definition
The Forest Rights Act 2006 (FRA) recognises and vests forest rights in Scheduled Tribes (STs) and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (OTFDs) who have been residing in forests for generations, correcting the historical injustice of treating them as encroachers on their own ancestral lands.

## Current Law

### Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act 2006
- § 3 — Recognises 13 categories of forest rights including: individual rights over forest land up to 4 hectares; community rights over community forest resources (CFR); grazing rights; collection of Minor Forest Produce (MFP); right to in situ rehabilitation; habitat rights for Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs)
- § 4 — Procedure for recognition and vesting of forest rights; rights deemed to have vested from the date of commencement
- § 4(5) — No eviction or removal of forest dwellers pending recognition of forest rights
- § 5 — Duties of forest right holders: protect wildlife, biodiversity, catchment areas, water sources, and other ecologically sensitive areas
- § 6 — Gram Sabha is the primary authority to initiate recognition process; claims pass through Sub-Divisional Level Committee (SDLC) → District Level Committee (DLC)

### FRA and Forest Diversion (Forest Conservation Act 1980 § 2)
- Prior consent of concerned Gram Sabha mandatory before any forest land in a Scheduled Area can be diverted for non-forest use
- Absence of Gram Sabha consent renders forest diversion void — affirmed in Niyamgiri case (SC 2013: Vedanta/Orissa Mining Corp v. MoEF)
- Gram Sabha must be given full information about proposed project and its impacts before consent is sought

### Panchayats Extension to Scheduled Areas Act 1996 (PESA)
- Extends Panchayati Raj institutions to Schedule V areas (tribal areas)
- Gram Sabha has ownership over MFP; must be consulted before land acquisition or displacement in Scheduled Areas
- Intersects with FRA to reinforce Gram Sabha authority over natural resources

## Key Provisions

### 13 Categories of Forest Rights under § 3
| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Individual land rights | Cultivated land up to 4 ha in occupation before 13 Dec 2005 |
| Community forest resource rights | Protect, regenerate, conserve, manage any CFR |
| Minor Forest Produce (MFP) | Ownership, collection, use, and disposal rights |
| Grazing | Seasonal use of traditional grazing grounds |
| Conversion of pattas | Convert old pattas/leases into titles |
| Habitat rights | For PVTGs and pre-agricultural communities |
| Fishing/water bodies | In traditional seasonal resource areas |
| Intellectual property | Knowledge of biodiversity and cultural diversity |
| Right to in situ rehabilitation | Where displacement occurred without FRA compliance |

### Recognition Procedure under § 6
| Level | Authority | Role |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Village | Gram Sabha | Receive and verify claims; forward to SDLC |
| Sub-Division | Sub-Divisional Level Committee (SDLC) | Examine claims; resolve disputes; forward to DLC |
| District | District Level Committee (DLC) | Final authority to vest rights; issue title deeds |

### Key Judicial Precedents
| Case | Court | Holding |
|------|-------|---------|
| Niyamgiri (Orissa Mining Corp v. MoEF), 2013 | Supreme Court | Gram Sabha consent required before forest diversion; 12 Gram Sabhas voted against Vedanta project |
| Wildlife First v. MoEF (2019) | Supreme Court | Eviction order for rejected claimants (later stayed/modified) |
| Mamta Mahanta v. State of Assam (2018) | Gauhati HC | Gram Sabha process must be followed before any eviction |
