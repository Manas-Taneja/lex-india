# Transfer of Property

## Definition
Transfer of property is the act by which a living person conveys property, in present or in future, to one or more living persons or to himself and one or more other living persons (§ 5, TPA 1882). Transfers by will are excluded from the Act's scope.

## Current Law

### Transfer of Property Act 1882
- § 5 — defines "transfer of property": must be by a living person (not by will or operation of law)
- § 6 — property of any kind may be transferred except: spes successionis, public office, salary, right to sue, restricted interests
- § 8 — transfer vests in transferee all transferor's interest plus incidents (easements, rents, title deeds)
- § 10 — condition absolutely restraining alienation of property transferred is void
- § 14 — rule against perpetuity: interest must vest within life-in-being + 18 years
- § 15 — accumulation of income limited to life of transferor or 18 years from transfer
- § 17 — property may be made to vest on a future uncertain event (contingent interest)
- § 18 — transfer for benefit of unborn person valid; vests on birth; prior interest must be life estate
- § 41 — transfer by ostensible owner is valid against real owner if transferee acted in good faith for consideration
- § 43 — transfer by person with defective/no title: if transferor later acquires title, it enures to transferee (feeding the grant)
- § 52 — lis pendens: transfer during pending suit affecting immovable property is void against decree of court

## Key Provisions

### Sale (§§ 54–57)
- § 54 — sale = exchange of money for ownership; tangible immovable property worth > ₹100 requires registered instrument
- § 55 — duties of seller (disclose material defects, give possession, execute conveyance) and buyer (pay price, bear loss after ownership passes)
- § 56 — marshalling: if property sold to several buyers, seller's lien discharged in reverse order of sale
- § 57 — discharge of encumbrances on sale

### Mortgage (§§ 58–104)

| Type | Key Feature |
|------|-------------|
| Simple mortgage (§ 58(b)) | Personal obligation; no possession; sale through court |
| Mortgage by conditional sale (§ 58(c)) | Ostensible sale with defeasance clause |
| Usufructuary mortgage (§ 58(d)) | Mortgagee takes possession; rents towards debt |
| English mortgage (§ 58(e)) | Absolute transfer with obligation to re-transfer on repayment |
| Equitable / deposit of title deeds (§ 58(f)) | Deposit of title deeds in notified towns creates mortgage |
| Anomalous mortgage (§ 58(g)) | Any mortgage not falling in above categories |

### Lease (§§ 105–117)
- § 105 — lease = transfer of right to enjoy property for a time in exchange for rent/premium
- § 106 — lease from year to year terminable by 6 months' notice; monthly lease by 15 days' notice
- § 108 — duties of lessor (keep in tenantable repair, disclose defects) and lessee (pay rent, not commit waste, give notice of encroachment)
- § 111 — determination of lease: efflux of time, surrender, forfeiture, merger, notice, expiry of limited interest
- § 116 — holding over: acceptance of rent after expiry creates month-to-month or year-to-year tenancy

### Gift (§§ 122–137)
- § 122 — gift = transfer of existing movable/immovable property without consideration, accepted by donee
- § 123 — gift of immovable property: must be registered; gift of movable: may be by delivery
- § 124 — gift of future property is void
- § 126 — revocation of gift: only on conditions agreed at time of gift, or by mutual consent; not for ingratitude
- §§ 130–137 — actionable claims (e.g., debt, beneficial interest in movable): transferred by written instrument signed by transferor
