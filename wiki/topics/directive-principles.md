## Definition

Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) are non-justiciable guidelines in Part IV (Articles 36–51) of the Indian Constitution that direct the State in governance and law-making. They are not enforceable by any court but are fundamental to governance and it is the duty of the State to apply them in making laws.

## Current Law

**Constitution of India, Part IV — Articles 36–51**

- **Article 36** — Definition of "State" (same as Part III)
- **Article 37** — DPSPs are non-justiciable but fundamental to governance
- **Article 38** — State to secure a social order for welfare
- **Article 39** — Specific economic principles (equal pay, no concentration of wealth)
- **Article 39A** — Equal justice and free legal aid
- **Article 40** — Organisation of village panchayats
- **Article 41** — Right to work, education, public assistance
- **Article 42** — Just and humane work conditions; maternity relief
- **Article 43** — Living wage for workers
- **Article 43A** — Participation of workers in management
- **Article 44** — Uniform Civil Code
- **Article 45** — Early childhood care and education (up to age 6)
- **Article 46** — Promotion of SC/ST and weaker sections
- **Article 47** — Duty to raise nutrition/living standards; prohibition of intoxicating drinks
- **Article 48** — Organisation of agriculture; prohibition of cow slaughter
- **Article 48A** — Protection of environment, forests, wildlife
- **Article 49** — Protection of monuments of national importance
- **Article 50** — Separation of judiciary from executive
- **Article 51** — Promotion of international peace

## Key Provisions

**Classification (Granville Austin's framework):**
1. **Socialistic** — Articles 38, 39, 39A, 41, 42, 43, 43A, 47 (welfare state, economic justice)
2. **Gandhian** — Articles 40, 43, 43B, 46, 47, 48 (village self-rule, cottage industry, social uplift)
3. **Liberal-intellectual** — Articles 44, 45, 48A, 49, 50, 51 (UCC, environment, international peace)

**Justiciability:**
- DPSPs cannot be enforced in courts — no individual can sue the State for non-implementation
- However, laws implementing DPSPs are given special protection under Article 31C (inserted by 25th Amendment, 1971): such laws cannot be challenged as violating Articles 14 or 19

**DPSP vs Fundamental Rights (conflict):**
- Originally: Fundamental Rights prevailed (State of Madras v Champakam Dorairajan, 1951)
- Post-42nd Amendment (1976): DPSPs in Articles 39(b) and (c) override Articles 14 and 19
- Current position: Courts attempt harmonious construction; DPSPs inform interpretation of Fundamental Rights

## Key Cases

- **Minerva Mills v Union of India (1980)** — Struck down 42nd Amendment's attempt to make DPSPs override all FRs; harmony between FRs and DPSPs is part of basic structure
- **Unni Krishnan v State of AP (1993)** — Right to education (Article 45 DPSP) read into Article 21 as Fundamental Right
- **PUCL v Union of India (2001)** — Right to food read into Article 21 via Article 47 DPSP
