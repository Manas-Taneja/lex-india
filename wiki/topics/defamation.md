## Definition

Defamation is the making or publishing of an imputation about a person, intended to harm that person's reputation, or with knowledge or reason to believe it will harm their reputation. Indian law criminalises defamation (unlike most common law countries) while also providing a civil remedy.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 354** — Definition of defamation
- **§ 355** — Punishment for defamation: simple imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both
- **§ 356** — Printing or engraving matter known to be defamatory (same punishment)
- **§ 357** — Sale of printed or engraved substance containing defamatory matter (up to 2 years or fine or both)

## Key Provisions

**Defamation (§ 354):** Making or publishing any imputation — by words (spoken or written), signs, or visible representations — concerning any person, intending to harm, or knowing it will harm, the reputation of that person.

**Ten exceptions (§ 354 — not defamatory if):**
1. True imputation made for public good
2. Fair comment on public conduct of public servant
3. Fair comment on conduct of public meeting
4. Publication of court proceedings (substantially true)
5. Comment on merits of a case decided by a court
6. Fair criticism of literary, artistic, or other work given to public
7. Censure by a person in authority in good faith
8. Accusation made in good faith to an authorised person
9. Imputation made in good faith for protection of one's interests or those of others
10. Caution given in good faith

**Libel vs slander:** Indian law does not distinguish — both written and spoken defamation are covered.

**Criminal vs civil:** Defamation is both a criminal offence (BNS § 355) and a civil wrong (actionable in tort). Criminal complaints require proof of intention; civil suits require only proof of publication and harm.

**Company/institution defamation:** Corporations can be defamed; criminal complaint must be filed by an authorised representative.

## Transition Note

Previously governed by **Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)**:
- IPC § 499 → BNS § 354 (definition and exceptions — identical)
- IPC § 500 → BNS § 355 (punishment — identical: 2 years or fine or both)
- IPC § 501 → BNS § 356 (printing defamatory matter)
- IPC § 502 → BNS § 357 (sale of defamatory matter)

Section numbers changed; no substantive change.
