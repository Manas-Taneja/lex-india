# Employment Termination

## Definition
Employment termination under Indian labour law covers the rights and procedures governing retrenchment, layoff, closure, and dismissal of workmen, with the Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (IDA) as the primary statute providing job security protections and mandatory compensatory obligations.

## Current Law

### Industrial Disputes Act 1947

- § 2(s) — defines "workman": any person employed in an industry to do manual, unskilled, skilled, technical, operational, clerical or supervisory work, excluding managerial/supervisory staff drawing >₹10,000/month (as amended)
- § 2(oo) — defines "retrenchment": termination of service by employer for any reason other than punishment, voluntary retirement, superannuation, or non-renewal of fixed-term contract
- § 25F — conditions precedent to retrenchment: (a) 1 month's written notice or pay in lieu; (b) retrenchment compensation = 15 days' average pay for every completed year of continuous service; (c) notice to appropriate government
- § 25G — procedure for retrenchment: last-come-first-go (LCFO) rule among workmen in same category
- § 25H — re-employment of retrenched workmen: retrenched workman has preferential right to re-employment when vacancy arises
- § 25N — retrenchment in establishments with ≥100 workmen: prior permission of appropriate government required; government must decide within 60 days
- § 25O — closure of undertaking with ≥100 workmen: 90 days' prior notice to government + workmen; government permission required; compensation = 15 days' wages per year of service

## Key Provisions

### Retrenchment Compensation Formula

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Eligibility | Continuous service ≥ 1 year |
| Notice period | 1 month written notice or equivalent wages |
| Compensation rate | 15 days' average pay per completed year |
| Establishment threshold for govt permission | ≥ 100 workmen |
| Government decision timeline (§ 25N) | 60 days (deemed approved if no order) |

### Domestic Enquiry and Natural Justice

- Dismissal for misconduct requires a fair domestic enquiry
- Principles of natural justice apply: charge sheet, opportunity to be heard, impartial enquiry officer
- Management must prove misconduct; quantum of punishment must be proportionate
- Labour courts can interfere where enquiry is defective or punishment shockingly disproportionate

### Special Categories

| Category | Rule |
|----------|------|
| Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) | Employer may offer; workman's acceptance is voluntary; compensation usually higher than statutory minimum |
| Transfer of undertaking | Service continuity protected; new employer inherits obligations |
| Fixed-term employment (2018 amendment) | Fixed-term workmen entitled to same benefits as permanent workmen pro-rata; no retrenchment compensation at end of term |
| Layoff | Temporary inability to provide work; compensation = 50% basic + DA for days laid off (§ 25C); establishments ≥100 need govt permission (§ 25M) |
