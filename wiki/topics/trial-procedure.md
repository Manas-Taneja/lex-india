## Definition

Trial procedure in India governs the stages from cognizance of an offence to final judgment in criminal cases. The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS) replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (CrPC) with effect from 1 July 2024. Trials are classified as Sessions trials, Warrant trials, Summons trials, and Summary trials depending on the seriousness of the offence.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS)**

- **§ 2(1)(x)** — "Trial" includes all proceedings from cognizance to judgment
- **§ 193** — Sessions Court takes cognizance only on committal by Magistrate (except where expressly empowered)
- **§ 207-210** — Supply of documents to accused: charge-sheet, police report, FIR, statements, documents relied upon — within 14 days of appearance
- **§ 230** — Framing of charge: Magistrate considers record; if prima facie case — frames charge; if insufficient — discharges accused
- **§ 235** — Conviction on plea of guilty (Magistrate must be satisfied plea is voluntary)
- **§ 242-248** — Warrant trial (police report cases): charge framed → prosecution evidence → accused examination → defence evidence → arguments → judgment
- **§ 249-252** — Warrant trial (complaint cases): accused appearance → charge → prosecution → defence → judgment
- **§ 262-265** — Summons trial: simpler procedure; Magistrate may convict on plea; evidence of prosecution and defence
- **§ 283-290** — Summary trial: Magistrate may try summarily offences punishable up to 3 years; no detailed record of evidence; sentence cannot exceed 3 months (except under BNSS § 290 for certain offences)
- **§ 316** — Judgment: to be pronounced in open court; reasons to be given; signed and dated
- **§ 356** — Victim compensation scheme: court may direct State to pay compensation even if accused has no means
- **§ 187** — Electronic trial: proceedings may be conducted through audio-video electronic means *(new in BNSS)*
- **§ 346** — Timeline for judgment: judgment must be delivered within 45 days of conclusion of arguments *(new in BNSS)*

**Key timelines under BNSS (new statutory deadlines not in CrPC):**

| Stage | BNSS deadline |
|-------|--------------|
| Chargesheet filing | 60/90 days from arrest |
| Supply of documents to accused | 14 days of first appearance |
| Committal to Sessions | 60 days |
| Framing of charges | 60 days of first hearing |
| Judgment after arguments | 45 days |
| Entire trial (Sessions) | Target: 3 years |

## Key Provisions

**Four trial types:**

| Type | Offences | Court | Key feature |
|------|----------|-------|-------------|
| Sessions trial | Punishable by death/life/7+ years | Sessions Court | Committal required; jury-free |
| Warrant trial | Punishable by 2+ years | Magistrate | Full evidence recording |
| Summons trial | Less than 2 years | Magistrate | Simplified procedure |
| Summary trial | Up to 3 years (listed) | Magistrate | No detailed record; max 3 months sentence |

**Discharge (§ 230 BNSS):** Accused can be discharged at the charge-framing stage if the court finds the allegations groundless. This is distinct from acquittal (after full trial).

**Double jeopardy (Art 20(2) + § 300 BNSS):** Once acquitted or convicted, cannot be tried again for the same offence on same facts.

**In absentia trial (§ 356 BNSS):** If accused repeatedly evades, court may proceed with trial and attachment of property. New provision — not in CrPC.

**Electronic proceedings (§ 187 BNSS):** Summons, evidence, arguments, and even judgment delivery may be done via video conferencing. Used extensively post-COVID.

**Speedy trial (Art 21):** Supreme Court in Hussainara Khatoon (1979) held that speedy trial is a fundamental right. BNSS deadlines are a legislative response to chronic delays.

**Plea bargaining (§ 289-294 BNSS):** Available for offences not punishable by death/life, and not involving a child or woman. Accused applies; prosecutor and victim consulted; reduced sentence on mutually satisfactory disposition.
