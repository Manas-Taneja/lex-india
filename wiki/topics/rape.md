## Definition

Rape is a non-consensual sexual act committed by a man upon a woman. Under Indian law, consent must be free, voluntary, and informed — consent obtained through fear, fraud, intoxication, or false promise of marriage is not valid consent. The offence is defined exhaustively under BNS § 63.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 63** — Rape (definition and punishment: 10 years to life + fine)
- **§ 64** — Punishment for rape (rigorous imprisonment 10 years minimum, extendable to life)
- **§ 65** — Punishment where victim is under 16 (minimum 20 years, extendable to life)
- **§ 66** — Punishment where victim is under 12 (rigorous life imprisonment or death)
- **§ 70** — Gang rape (minimum 20 years, extendable to life)

## Key Provisions

**Definition of rape (§ 63):** A man commits rape if he penetrates (vagina, mouth, urethra, or anus) or inserts any object without consent, or applies mouth, or manipulates any part of the body to cause penetration.

**Seven circumstances that negate consent:**
1. Against her will
2. Without her consent
3. Consent obtained by putting her/someone she cares about in fear of death or hurt
4. Consent given under mistaken belief that the man is her husband
5. Consent given when of unsound mind or intoxicated
6. With or without consent when under 18 years of age
7. When she is unable to communicate consent

**Marital rape exception (§ 63 proviso):** Sexual intercourse by a husband with his own wife is not rape if she is 18 years or older. This exception remains in BNS (modified from IPC's 15-year threshold to 18).

**Punishment:**
- Basic (§ 64): Rigorous imprisonment 10–20 years or life + fine
- Victim under 16 (§ 65): 20 years to life + fine
- Victim under 12 (§ 66): Life imprisonment or death + fine
- Gang rape (§ 70): 20 years to life + fine (each member liable)

## Transition Note

Previously governed by **Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)**:
- IPC § 375 → BNS § 63 (definition — expanded; marital rape exception age raised from 15 to 18)
- IPC § 376 → BNS § 64 (punishment)
- IPC § 376A → BNS § 66 (causing death or persistent vegetative state)
- IPC § 376D → BNS § 70 (gang rape)

Key change: BNS expanded the definition of rape to include more forms of penetration and raised the marital rape exception age to 18.
