## Definition

Kidnapping is the taking or enticing away of a person from their lawful guardianship or from India without consent. Indian law distinguishes kidnapping from abduction: kidnapping is defined by the status of the victim (minor or person of unsound mind) and the act of taking, while abduction covers any person taken by force, compulsion, or deceit.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 137** — Kidnapping from India: taking any person beyond India's borders without consent
- **§ 137(2)** — Kidnapping from lawful guardianship: taking a minor (under 16 for males, under 18 for females) or person of unsound mind out of the keeping of their lawful guardian without consent
- **§ 138** — Abduction: compelling or inducing any person by force/intimidation/deceit to go from one place to another
- **§ 139** — Kidnapping or abducting to murder: death or life imprisonment
- **§ 140** — Kidnapping for ransom: death or life imprisonment + fine
- **§ 141** — Kidnapping to subject person to grievous hurt, slavery, or unnatural lust: up to 10 years + fine
- **§ 142** — Wrongfully concealing or keeping a kidnapped person: same punishment as kidnapping
- **§ 143** — Kidnapping or abducting a child under 10 years to steal from them: up to 7 years + fine
- **§ 144** — Enticing or taking child for begging: up to 10 years + fine
- **§ 145** — Wrongful confinement: up to 1 year + fine (up to 3 years for confinement exceeding 3 days)

## Key Provisions

**Kidnapping vs Abduction:**

| Feature | Kidnapping | Abduction |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Who can be victim | Minor / person of unsound mind | Any person |
| Means | Taking/enticing away | Force / intimidation / deceit |
| Consent of victim | Irrelevant | Irrelevant if obtained by force/deceit |
| Consent of guardian | Missing guardian's consent = offence | N/A |
| Nature | Substantive offence | Auxiliary offence (charged alongside purpose) |

**Consent of guardian matters (§ 137(2)):** If guardian consents, no kidnapping. But victim's own consent is irrelevant if they are a minor.

**Purpose determines quantum:** Kidnapping itself carries up to 7 years + fine (§ 137). The punishment escalates based on the purpose — ransom → death; murder → death; grievous hurt → 10 years.

**Parental kidnapping:** A father taking his own child may not be guilty if he is the lawful guardian, but stepparents or other relatives without guardianship can be charged.

## Transition Note

Previously governed by **Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)**:
- IPC § 359 → BNS § 137 (kinds of kidnapping)
- IPC § 360 → BNS § 137(1) (kidnapping from India)
- IPC § 361 → BNS § 137(2) (kidnapping from lawful guardianship)
- IPC § 362 → BNS § 138 (abduction)
- IPC § 363 → BNS § 137 (punishment for kidnapping: 7 years)
- IPC § 364A → BNS § 140 (kidnapping for ransom — death or life imprisonment)

Substance unchanged; BNS § 144 is a new provision specifically targeting child-begging rackets.
