## Definition

Cybercrime encompasses offences committed using computers, networks, or digital devices — including hacking, data theft, online fraud, identity theft, cyberstalking, publishing obscene material online, and ransomware attacks. In India, cybercrimes are governed primarily by the Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act) and supplemented by the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS).

## Current Law

**Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act)**

- **§ 43** — Penalty for damage to computer systems without authorisation: civil liability — compensation up to ₹1 crore
- **§ 65** — Tampering with computer source documents: imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine up to ₹2 lakh, or both
- **§ 66** — Computer-related offences (hacking, unauthorised access, damage): imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine up to ₹5 lakh, or both
- **§ 66A** — Sending offensive messages — **struck down by Supreme Court in Shreya Singhal v Union of India (2015)** as unconstitutional (violates Article 19(1)(a))
- **§ 66B** — Dishonestly receiving stolen computer resource: imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹1 lakh
- **§ 66C** — Identity theft (using electronic signature/password of another): imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹1 lakh
- **§ 66D** — Cheating by personation using computer resource: imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹1 lakh
- **§ 66E** — Violation of privacy (capturing/transmitting private images without consent): imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹2 lakh
- **§ 66F** — Cyber terrorism: denial of access to a computer resource likely to cause death/injury/damage to critical infrastructure — life imprisonment
- **§ 67** — Publishing obscene material in electronic form: imprisonment up to 3 years + fine ₹5 lakh (first offence); up to 5 years (subsequent)
- **§ 67A** — Publishing sexually explicit material: imprisonment up to 5 years + fine ₹10 lakh
- **§ 67B** — Child pornography: imprisonment up to 5 years + fine ₹10 lakh (first offence); 7 years (subsequent)
- **§ 69** — Power to intercept, monitor, or decrypt information by Central/State Government in the interest of sovereignty, security, public order
- **§ 69A** — Power to block access to information in the public interest
- **§ 72** — Breach of confidentiality and privacy by authorised officers: imprisonment up to 2 years + fine ₹1 lakh
- **§ 75** — Extra-territorial jurisdiction: IT Act applies to offences committed outside India if the computer/network is located in India

**BNS, 2023 — cyber-related provisions:**
- **§ 111** — Organised crime (includes cybercrime syndicates): life imprisonment or death
- **§ 318** — Cheating (online fraud, phishing, OTP fraud): up to 7 years + fine
- **§ 356(2)** — Criminal defamation online: same as offline defamation

## Key Provisions

**Shreya Singhal (2015):** § 66A struck down — police cannot arrest for "annoying" or "offensive" online posts. Despite the ruling, arrests under § 66A continued; Supreme Court reiterated the ban in 2021.

**Cyber fraud types and applicable sections:**

| Offence | Applicable section |
|---------|------------------|
| Phishing / OTP fraud | IT Act § 66D + BNS § 318 |
| SIM swap fraud | IT Act § 66C + 66D |
| Ransomware | IT Act § 66 + 66F |
| Non-consensual intimate images | IT Act § 66E + 67A |
| Cyberstalking | BNS § 78 (stalking) + IT Act § 67 |
| Hacking | IT Act § 66 |
| Cyber terrorism | IT Act § 66F |

**Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In):** National nodal agency for cybersecurity incidents. Organisations must report incidents within 6 hours of detection (CERT-In Directions, 2022).

**Cybercrime reporting:** Report at cybercrime.gov.in (National Cybercrime Reporting Portal) or call 1930 (cybercrime helpline) before filing an FIR.

**Jurisdiction:** Both the place where the act was committed and where its effect was felt have jurisdiction. Cross-border cybercrimes are investigated by State cybercrime units with MHA coordination.
