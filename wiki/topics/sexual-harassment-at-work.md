## Definition

Sexual harassment at the workplace is any unwelcome act or behaviour — physical contact, demand for sexual favours, sexually coloured remarks, showing pornography, or any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature. The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (POSH Act) mandates internal complaints committees, inquiry procedures, and employer obligations for all workplaces with 10 or more employees.

## Current Law

**Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (POSH Act)**

- **§ 2(n)** — Sexual harassment: unwelcome acts including physical contact, demand/request for sexual favours, sexually coloured remarks, showing pornography, any other unwelcome physical/verbal/non-verbal conduct of sexual nature
- **§ 2(o)** — Workplace: includes offices, factories, hospitals, educational institutions, sports institutes, stadiums, remote work locations, transport, and client premises where work is performed
- **§ 4** — Internal Complaints Committee (ICC): every employer with 10 or more employees must constitute an ICC; presiding officer must be a senior woman employee; at least half the members must be women; one external member from an NGO/social worker
- **§ 6** — Local Complaints Committee (LCC): District Officer constitutes LCC for establishments with fewer than 10 employees and for complaints against the employer himself
- **§ 9** — Filing of complaint: aggrieved woman must file complaint within 3 months of incident (extendable by 3 months if ICC satisfied with cause)
- **§ 11** — Inquiry: ICC must complete inquiry within 60 days; parties may be heard; conciliation possible at complainant's request (not for quid pro quo harassment)
- **§ 13** — ICC recommendations: if complaint proved — action against respondent (written apology, warning, withholding of promotion/pay, termination) + compensation to aggrieved woman
- **§ 14** — Penalty for false complaints or false evidence: action against complainant/witnesses (but no criminal liability under POSH)
- **§ 16** — Confidentiality: identity of parties and contents of complaint must not be disclosed
- **§ 19** — Employer obligations: display notice of penal consequences, organise awareness programmes, monitor timely submission of annual reports by ICC
- **§ 20** — Penalty for employer non-compliance: fine up to ₹50,000 (first offence); ₹1 lakh + cancellation of business licence (repeat offence)
- **§ 26** — Offences: cognizable under IPC/BNS

**BNS, 2023 — § 75** — Sexual harassment (criminal): imprisonment up to 3 years (first offence); up to 5 years + fine (subsequent offence)

## Key Provisions

**POSH is civil/employment law; BNS § 75 is criminal:** An aggrieved woman can file both a POSH complaint (before ICC) and an FIR (criminal complaint). The two proceedings are independent.

**Employer liability (§ 19 + 20):** An employer who fails to constitute an ICC faces fine and potential licence cancellation. Directors/officers in default are personally liable.

**Extended definition of workplace:** Applies to informal sector, domestic workers (though LCC handles this), agricultural workers. Remote work is covered.

**Vishaka Guidelines (predecessor):** POSH codified the Vishaka Guidelines issued by the Supreme Court in Vishaka v State of Rajasthan (1997). Before the 2013 Act, Vishaka applied as law.

**Quid pro quo vs hostile environment:**
- Quid pro quo: sexual favour demanded as condition of employment/advancement
- Hostile work environment: repeated unwelcome conduct that creates an intimidating or offensive workplace
Both are covered under § 2(n).

**Time limits:** 3-month filing deadline is strict but extendable. ICC inquiry must be completed within 60 days. Employer must act on recommendations within 60 days of receiving the ICC report.

**Third-party harassment:** Where harassment is by a third party (client, vendor), the employer must take steps to assist the employee and take action against the third party as far as practicable.
