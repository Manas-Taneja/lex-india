## Definition

Company incorporation is the legal process of forming a company as a separate legal entity under the Companies Act, 2013. Once incorporated, the company has its own legal identity distinct from its members — it can own property, enter contracts, sue and be sued in its own name.

## Current Law

**Companies Act, 2013** — administered by Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). Governed by rules under the Companies (Incorporation) Rules, 2014.

- **§ 3** — Formation of company (types: public, private, one person)
- **§ 4** — Memorandum of Association (MoA)
- **§ 5** — Articles of Association (AoA)
- **§ 7** — Incorporation of company (application procedure)
- **§ 9** — Effect of registration (separate legal personality)
- **§ 10A** — Commencement of business (declaration required after incorporation)
- **§ 12** — Registered office requirement

## Key Provisions

**Types of companies (§ 3):**
- *Private company*: Min 2 members, max 200; restricts share transfer; min 2 directors
- *Public company*: Min 7 members, no max; min 3 directors
- *One Person Company (OPC)*: Single member, single director; nominee required; limited liability

**Incorporation procedure (§ 7, MCA portal):**
1. Obtain DIN (Director Identification Number) for each proposed director
2. Apply for name approval (RUN — Reserve Unique Name) on MCA21 portal
3. File SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus) form:
   - MoA (Form INC-33) and AoA (Form INC-34) as e-forms
   - Declaration of compliance (INC-9)
   - Proof of registered office address
4. Certificate of Incorporation issued by Registrar of Companies (RoC)
5. File INC-20A (commencement of business declaration) within 180 days

**Memorandum of Association (§ 4):** Contains: name, state, objects, liability clause, capital clause. Objects clause defines the scope of company's activities — ultra vires acts are void.

**Minimum capital:** No minimum paid-up capital requirement for private companies (removed by 2015 amendment). OPC: no minimum capital.

**Registered office (§ 12):** Must have a registered office within 30 days of incorporation. All official communications sent here.

**Separate legal entity (§ 9):** On registration, the company becomes a body corporate with perpetual succession and a common seal, capable of holding property, contracting, and suing.

**Lifting the corporate veil:** Courts may disregard separate entity if used for fraud, tax evasion, or to evade legal obligations (judicial doctrine, not in statute).
