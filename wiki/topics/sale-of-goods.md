## Definition

The Sale of Goods Act, 1930 (SGA) governs contracts for the sale of movable goods in India. It supplements the Indian Contract Act, 1872, which continues to apply for general contract principles. A contract of sale is a contract whereby the seller transfers or agrees to transfer the property in goods to the buyer for a price. Where transfer is immediate — it is a sale; where transfer is to occur on a future event — it is an agreement to sell.

## Current Law

**Sale of Goods Act, 1930**

- **§ 4** — Sale vs agreement to sell: sale = property transferred immediately; agreement to sell = property to transfer in future; agreement to sell becomes sale when time/conditions for transfer are fulfilled
- **§ 6** — Subject matter: existing goods (owned/possessed by seller) or future goods (to be manufactured/acquired); goods perishing before contract = void; goods perishing after agreement to sell but before sale = agreement avoided
- **§ 8** — Perishing of goods: if specific goods are destroyed without seller's fault before risk passes — contract void
- **§ 12** — Conditions and warranties: condition = stipulation essential to main purpose; breach → right to treat contract as repudiated; warranty = collateral to main purpose; breach → damages only
- **§ 14** — Implied conditions: seller has right to sell; buyer to have quiet possession; goods free from encumbrance
- **§ 15** — Sale by description: implied condition that goods correspond to description
- **§ 16** — Implied condition of quality/fitness: no implied condition as to quality/fitness unless: (a) buyer relies on seller's skill and makes known purpose — implied condition of fitness; (b) goods sold by description from a dealer who deals in such goods — implied condition of merchantable quality
- **§ 17** — Sale by sample: implied condition that bulk corresponds to sample; reasonable opportunity to compare; goods free from defect rendering them unmerchantable
- **§ 19** — Property passes when intended: for specific goods — property passes when parties intend; rules in §§ 20-24 for ascertaining intention
- **§ 20** — Unconditional contract for specific goods in deliverable state: property passes when contract made
- **§ 21** — Specific goods — seller bound to weigh/measure: property passes when done and buyer has notice
- **§ 23** — Sale of unascertained goods: property passes when goods ascertained and unconditionally appropriated to the contract
- **§ 26** — Risk follows property: risk passes with property unless otherwise agreed; if delivery delayed through fault of either party — risk borne by the party at fault
- **§ 27** — Sale by person not owner (nemo dat): seller with no title cannot give better title to buyer; exceptions for mercantile agents, sale by court, estoppel, sale in market overt
- **§ 30** — Sale by buyer/seller in possession: good title to innocent third-party purchaser even if original sale has conditions
- **§ 31-44** — Delivery obligations: delivery of right quality, quantity, time; installment deliveries; delivery to carrier; c.i.f. and f.o.b. terms implied meaning
- **§ 45-54** — Unpaid seller's rights: lien on goods; stoppage in transit; right of resale; right to sue for price or damages

## Key Provisions

**Sale vs agreement to sell:**

| Aspect | Sale | Agreement to sell |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| Transfer of property | Immediate | Future/conditional |
| Risk | Passes to buyer | Remains with seller |
| Remedy on breach | Action for price (or damages) | Damages only |
| Effect of insolvency of buyer | Seller must deliver (buyer's trustee can claim) | Seller need not deliver |

**Implied terms — when they arise:**

| Term | When applies |
|------|--------------|
| Right to sell (§ 14(a)) | Always — cannot be excluded |
| Quiet possession (§ 14(b)) | Always |
| Fitness for purpose (§ 16(1)) | Buyer made purpose known + relied on seller's skill |
| Merchantable quality (§ 16(2)) | Goods sold by description by dealer in those goods |
| Correspondence to description (§ 15) | Sale by description |
| Bulk corresponds to sample (§ 17) | Sale by sample |

**Nemo dat exceptions (§ 27-30):** A person cannot give better title than they have — but exceptions:
- Sale by mercantile agent with owner's consent
- Sale under voidable contract before it is avoided
- Seller continuing in possession after sale — good title to innocent buyer
- Buyer in possession with seller's consent — can pass good title
- Estoppel by owner's conduct

**Caveat emptor (buyer beware):** General rule — buyer must examine goods; no implied warranty as to quality. Exceptions under § 16 (fitness/merchantability) and § 15 (description), and when seller fraudulently conceals defect.

**Unpaid seller's rights (§§ 45-54):**

| Right | Condition | Effect |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Lien | Goods in seller's possession; price not paid | Can retain goods until payment |
| Stoppage in transit | Buyer insolvent; goods in transit | Can retake goods from carrier |
| Right of resale | Perishable goods; or notice given | Can resell and recover loss |
| Sue for price | Property has passed; payment date passed | Claim full price |
| Sue for damages | Property not passed; buyer refuses | Difference between contract price and market price |

**SGA and Consumer Protection Act 2019:** For consumer transactions (buyer is a consumer — not for resale/commercial purpose), Consumer Protection Act 2019 gives additional remedies including deficiency of service, product liability, and access to consumer commissions. SGA continues to govern property and risk aspects.
