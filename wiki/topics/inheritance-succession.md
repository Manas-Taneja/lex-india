# Inheritance and Succession

## Definition
Succession is the transmission of property rights from a deceased person (de cujus) to their heirs or legatees, either by operation of law (intestate) or by a valid will (testamentary). India has separate succession regimes for different religious communities.

## Current Law

### Hindu Succession Act 1956 (as amended 2005)
- § 6 (substituted by 2005 Amendment) — Daughters are coparceners in a Mitakshara joint family by birth, with the same rights and liabilities as a son; applies to daughters born before or after the amendment (SC: Vineeta Sharma v Rakesh Sharma, 2020 — amendment applies retroactively irrespective of whether father was alive in 2005)
- § 8 — Intestate succession of a Hindu male: property devolves first on Class-I heirs (Schedule), then Class-II, then agnates, then cognates
- § 10 — Distribution among Class-I heirs: widow, daughters, sons take simultaneously; widow's share equal to one son's share
- § 14 — Property possessed by a Hindu female is held by her as full owner (absolute estate), not limited estate; overrides prior restrictions
- § 15 — Intestate succession of a Hindu female: property devolves on sons and daughters (including pre-deceased children's children), then husband's heirs, then mother and father, then father's heirs
- § 23 (deleted by 2005 Amendment) — Daughters' right to partition of dwelling house no longer restricted

### Mitakshara vs Dayabhaga
| School | Geography | Coparcenary |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Mitakshara | Most of India (except Bengal/Assam) | Joint family property; birth-right in coparcenary |
| Dayabhaga | Bengal and Assam | No birth-right; interest accrues only on death of ancestor |

### Indian Succession Act 1925
- Applies to Christians, Parsis, and persons married under the Special Marriage Act 1954 (§ 21A HSA excluded)
- §§ 57-191 — Testamentary succession: rules for valid wills; § 63 execution (signed by testator, attested by two witnesses who are not beneficiaries)
- § 87 — Bequest to attesting witness: void to extent of legacy, witness competent
- §§ 222-236 — Probate: court seal of authority to execute a will; mandatory in Presidency towns (Calcutta, Bombay, Madras) and notified areas for wills of Christians; not mandatory for Hindus outside those areas but grants conclusive title
- §§ 370-390 — Succession certificate: issued by civil court for debts and securities; does not establish title to immovable property but allows collecting debts

### Muslim Succession (Hanafi School)
- Governed by Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act 1937 and classical Hanafi fiqh (Hedaya)
- Quranic heirs (Sharers / Ashab al-Fara'id): fixed fractional shares in Quran (Surah 4:11-12, 176)
- Male heirs generally receive double the share of a corresponding female heir (2:1 ratio)
- Bequest (wasiyat) limited to 1/3 of net estate; cannot be made to an heir without consent of all other heirs
- Doctrine of Aul (increase): when fractional shares exceed unity, each share reduced proportionally
- Doctrine of Radd (return): residue after Quranic shares returned to Quranic heirs in proportion

## Key Provisions

### Class-I Heirs under Hindu Succession Act § 8 Schedule
| Heir | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Son | Includes son of pre-deceased son |
| Daughter | Equal share as son (post-2005) |
| Widow | Takes one share equal to each surviving son |
| Mother | One share |
| Son of pre-deceased son | Represents father's share |
| Daughter of pre-deceased son | Equal to son of pre-deceased son |
| Son / daughter of pre-deceased daughter | Represents mother's share |
| Widow of pre-deceased son | One share in son's notional share |

### Probate vs Succession Certificate vs Letters of Administration
| Instrument | Scope | Court | When Required |
|------------|-------|-------|---------------|
| Probate (§§ 222-236 ISA) | Proves will valid; executor authorised | District Judge / HC | Mandatory for Christians in Presidency towns; optional for Hindus |
| Succession Certificate (§§ 370-390 ISA) | Collects debts and securities | District Judge | Intestate estates; no immovable property title |
| Letters of Administration (§§ 234-248 ISA) | Administrator appointed for estate with or without will | District Judge / HC | No executor named; intestate estate of Christians/Parsis |

### Nominee vs Legal Heir
The Supreme Court in Sarbati Devi v Usha Devi (1984) held that a nominee under insurance/provident fund/bank accounts holds the proceeds as a trustee for the legal heirs — nomination does not override succession law. Legislative carve-outs exist (e.g., EPFO nominees under EPF Act have stronger claim; Depositories Act 1996 for demat accounts gives nominee full beneficial title).
