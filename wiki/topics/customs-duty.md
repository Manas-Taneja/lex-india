# Customs Duty

## Definition
Customs duty is a tax levied by the Central Government on goods imported into or exported from India. It is administered by the Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC) and serves both revenue and trade-policy objectives, including protection of domestic industry.

## Current Law

### Customs Act 1962
- § 11 — power to prohibit import/export of specified goods (e.g., weapons, wildlife, counterfeit currency)
- § 14 — valuation: transaction value (price actually paid or payable) as the primary basis; Customs Valuation (Determination of Value of Imported Goods) Rules 2007 prescribe fallback methods
- § 46 — importer must file a bill of entry before or on arrival of goods; types: home consumption, warehousing, transit
- § 57-73 — warehousing provisions: licensed public/private warehouses; goods may be warehoused without payment of duty; bond required
- § 79 — baggage rules: duty-free allowances for returning residents/tourists; excess goods dutiable
- § 110 — power to seize goods believed to be smuggled or liable to confiscation
- § 111 — goods liable to confiscation: misdeclaration, prohibited goods, non-declaration
- § 112 — penalty on persons knowingly concerned in importation of prohibited/misdeclared goods
- § 114AA — penalty for use of false/fraudulent documents: up to 5× the duty sought to be evaded
- § 123 — presumption of smuggling for notified goods (gold, diamonds, electronic goods, etc.): burden shifts to possessor to prove lawful import

### Customs Tariff Act 1975
- § 9A — anti-dumping duty (ADD): imposed on dumped imports (export price < normal value); investigations by Directorate General of Trade Remedies (DGTR); valid 5 years, reviewable
- § 9 — countervailing duty (CVD): offsets foreign government subsidies on imported goods
- § 8B — safeguard duty: temporary protection when surge of imports causes serious injury to domestic industry

## Key Provisions

### Import Duty Components
| Levy | Basis |
|------|-------|
| Basic Customs Duty (BCD) | Ad valorem % on transaction value; rates in Customs Tariff schedule |
| Social Welfare Surcharge (SWS) | 10% on BCD |
| IGST on imports | Applied on (transaction value + BCD + SWS); treated as input tax credit under GST |
| Integrated Customs Cess | Applicable on specified goods (e.g., health cess on medical devices) |

### Valuation Hierarchy (Customs Valuation Rules 2007)
| Method | When Applied |
|--------|-------------|
| Transaction value (Rule 3) | Primary method — price paid for goods when buyer and seller not related |
| Transaction value of identical goods (Rule 4) | If Rule 3 inapplicable |
| Transaction value of similar goods (Rule 5) | Next fallback |
| Deductive value (Rule 7) | Based on resale price in India |
| Computed value (Rule 8) | Based on cost of production |
| Residual method (Rule 9) | Flexible, reasonable means |

### Advance Ruling
Importers/exporters may seek binding advance rulings on classification, valuation, and applicability of notifications from the Authority for Advance Rulings (Customs).

## Dispute Resolution
Port-level adjudicating authority → Commissioner (Appeals) → Customs, Excise and Service Tax Appellate Tribunal (CESTAT) → High Court → Supreme Court.
