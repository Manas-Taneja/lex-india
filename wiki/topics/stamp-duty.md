# Stamp Duty

## Definition
Stamp duty is a tax levied on instruments (written documents) recording transactions. Under the Indian Stamp Act 1899, an instrument not duly stamped is inadmissible as evidence and cannot be acted upon by any public officer, though it may be admitted on payment of duty plus penalty.

## Current Law

### Indian Stamp Act 1899
- § 3 — every instrument mentioned in Schedule I chargeable with duty of indicated amount
- § 17 — instruments must be stamped before or at time of execution (not after)
- § 31 — an instrument not duly stamped may be admitted as evidence of a collateral fact (not of the transaction itself)
- § 33 — every person having official authority who receives an instrument must examine whether it is properly stamped
- § 35 — instrument not duly stamped: inadmissible in evidence for any purpose; cannot be acted upon, registered, or authenticated by public officer
- § 38 — impounding: officer before whom an insufficiently stamped instrument is produced must impound it
- § 40 — impounded instrument sent to Collector for adjudication; Collector determines proper duty and penalty
- § 47A — if Collector is of opinion that market value of property is understated (instrument value < market value), Collector may refer for adjudication of stamp duty on actual market value

## Key Provisions

### Schedule I — Central Instruments and Rates (select)

| Instrument | Duty |
|------------|------|
| Bill of Exchange (payable on demand) | 0.50 paise per ₹100 or part thereof |
| Cheque | Fixed duty (negligible; varies by state) |
| Debenture | 0.005% per year of face value |
| Power of Attorney (general) | Fixed (state rate) |
| Affidavit | Fixed (state rate) |
| Bond | 0.1% of value |

Note: States have concurrent power (Entry 63, List II, Seventh Schedule) to fix rates for instruments not in the Union List. Property transfer stamp duty is almost entirely state-governed.

### State Stamp Duty on Property Transfer

| State | Approximate Rate (% of market value) |
|-------|--------------------------------------|
| Maharashtra | 6% (5% + 1% metro cess in Mumbai) |
| Delhi | 6% (men), 4% (women), 5% (joint) |
| Karnataka | 5.6% (stamp 5% + surcharge 0.6%) |
| Uttar Pradesh | 7% |
| Tamil Nadu | 7% |
| Gujarat | 4.9% |
| West Bengal | 5–7% (varies by value) |

Rates above are indicative; states revise rates periodically. Registration fee (1–2%) is payable separately.

### Market Value / Circle Rate (Ready Reckoner)
- State governments publish area-wise minimum property values (circle rates / guidance values / ready reckoner rates)
- Stamp duty is calculated on the higher of: (a) stated consideration in instrument, or (b) circle rate value
- § 47A empowers Collector to re-assess if stated consideration is below market value
- Buyer must pay deficit duty + penalty (up to 10× deficient amount) if undervaluation detected

### E-Stamping (SHCIL)
- Stock Holding Corporation of India Ltd (SHCIL) is the Central Record Keeping Agency for e-stamps
- Authorised Collection Centres (ACCs) issue e-stamp certificates with unique certificate number
- Eliminates fake/fraudulent stamp papers (post-Telgi scam reform)
- Currently operational in most states; physical stamp papers being phased out

### Penalties and Condonation

| Situation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Unstamped / deficiently stamped | Inadmissible; impounded; duty + up to 10× penalty |
| Late stamping (before registration) | Penalty payable; Collector may condone |
| Understated market value (§ 47A) | Deficit duty + penalty on difference |
| Stamped but wrong instrument | Spoilt stamp refund / remission on application |

### Stamp Duty on Specific Transactions

| Transaction | Basis |
|-------------|-------|
| Sale deed | Market value or consideration (higher) |
| Gift deed | Market value of gifted property |
| Partition deed | Market value of separated share |
| Mortgage deed | Principal loan amount |
| Lease deed | Annual rent × prescribed multiplier |
| Power of attorney | Fixed or ad valorem (state-specific) |

### Concurrent Registration and Stamp Duty
Stamp duty and registration fee are both payable at time of registration before the Sub-Registrar. The document will not be registered unless stamp duty is fully paid. Registration fee is typically 1% of market value (subject to state cap).
