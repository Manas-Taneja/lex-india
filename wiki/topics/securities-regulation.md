## Definition

Securities regulation in India is governed by the Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992 (SEBI Act), the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 (SCRA), and the Depositories Act, 1996. SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) is the primary regulator for capital markets — stock exchanges, listed companies, intermediaries (brokers, merchant bankers, portfolio managers), mutual funds, and collective investment schemes. SEBI's mandate is to protect investors, develop the securities market, and regulate it.

## Current Law

**SEBI Act, 1992**

- **§ 11** — Powers and functions of SEBI: regulate stock exchanges, intermediaries, investment schemes; prohibit insider trading and fraudulent trade practices; call for information; conduct enquiries and audits; register and regulate intermediaries
- **§ 11A** — SEBI may prohibit issue/purchase/sale of any security in the interest of investors
- **§ 11B** — SEBI may issue directions: cease and desist; impound proceeds; prohibit dealing in securities; suspend registration of intermediary
- **§ 11C** — Investigation: SEBI may investigate any intermediary/listed company; summon documents, bank accounts, records
- **§ 12** — Registration of intermediaries: stock broker, sub-broker, share transfer agent, banker to issue, trustee, underwriter, portfolio manager, investment adviser, depository participant must be registered with SEBI
- **§ 12A** — Prohibition of manipulative and deceptive devices: market manipulation, wash trades, pump and dump, misleading statements prohibited
- **§ 15A-15L** — Penalties: failure to furnish information (₹1 lakh/day), insider trading (₹25 crore or 3× profit), market manipulation (₹25 crore or 3× profit), failure to redress investor grievances (₹1 lakh/day)
- **§ 15Z** — Appeal to SAT (Securities Appellate Tribunal): against SEBI adjudication orders
- **§ 24** — Criminal offences: imprisonment up to 10 years for violation of SEBI Act provisions

**Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956**

- **§ 2(h)** — Securities: shares, scrips, bonds, debentures, debenture stock, government securities, derivatives, units of collective investment scheme
- **§ 13** — Stock exchange recognition: Central Government/SEBI recognises stock exchanges; recognised exchange alone may conduct dealings
- **§ 16** — Contracts in securities: contracts for sale/purchase of securities in a recognised manner only; bucketshop and forward contracts outside exchange prohibited
- **§ 22-22A** — Listing conditions: company whose securities are listed must comply with listing agreement; SEBI may compel listing, suspend/delist securities

**Depositories Act, 1996**

- **§ 5** — Depository services: dematerialisation of securities; electronic transfer by book entry
- **§ 7** — Rights of depositors: same rights as holders of physical securities
- **§ 11** — NSDL and CDSL registered as depositories under this Act

**SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 (LODR)**

- Quarterly financial results within 45 days; annual results within 60 days
- Material events disclosed to stock exchange immediately
- Related party transactions approved by audit committee and shareholders
- Corporate governance: independent directors minimum 1/3 of board

**SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015**

- **Insider:** person in possession of Unpublished Price Sensitive Information (UPSI)
- **UPSI:** financial results, dividends, M&A, change in key personnel, material litigation
- Trading window: closed during UPSI possession; trades permitted only during open window
- Pre-clearance: key executives' trades require pre-clearance
- Structured Digital Database (SDD): companies must maintain log of UPSI sharing

**SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018 (ICDR)**

- IPO eligibility, pricing, disclosure requirements (DRHP), minimum subscription
- QIP, rights issue, preferential allotment — separate rules

## Key Provisions

**SEBI enforcement tools:**

| Action | Trigger | Forum |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Adjudication (penalty) | Violation of regulations | SEBI Adjudicating Officer |
| Direction (cease/desist/impound) | Urgent investor protection | SEBI Whole-Time Member |
| Debarment | Serious violation | SEBI |
| Prosecution | Criminal offence | Special Court |
| Settlement | Party offers settlement amount | SEBI Settlement Committee |

**Insider trading — key elements:**
- UPSI test: information not generally available; would materially affect price if published
- Trading prohibition: insider cannot trade in securities of company when in possession of UPSI
- Communication prohibition: cannot share UPSI except in legitimate purpose
- Defence: trades pursuant to SEBI-approved trading plan (pre-announced schedule)

**SEBI vs company law — concurrent jurisdiction:**
- SEBI governs listed companies on securities law matters
- MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) / NCLT governs corporate law matters
- Overlapping jurisdiction resolved through MOU between SEBI and MCA

**Mutual fund regulation:** SEBI (Mutual Fund) Regulations, 1996 — AMC must be registered with SEBI; investment in securities per specified limits; NAV disclosed daily; trustees ensure compliance with regulations.

**Appeals:** SEBI adjudicating orders → SAT (within 45 days) → Supreme Court (within 60 days of SAT order, on question of law only).
