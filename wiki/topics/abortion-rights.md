# Abortion Rights

## Definition
The Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act 1971 (MTP Act), as amended in 2021, governs the conditions under which a registered medical practitioner may lawfully terminate a pregnancy in India, balancing a woman's reproductive autonomy with public health safeguards.

## Current Law

### Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act 1971 (as amended 2021)
- § 3(2)(a) — Up to 20 weeks: opinion of one registered medical practitioner (RMP) sufficient
- § 3(2)(b) — 20 to 24 weeks: opinion of two RMPs required; applies only to special categories defined in MTP Rules
- § 3(2B) — Beyond 24 weeks: no upper gestational limit for substantial foetal abnormality; requires Medical Board constituted by State/UT government
- § 3(3) — Pregnancy length presumed not to exceed gestational limit if RMP forms opinion in good faith
- § 3(4) — Conditions: continuation of pregnancy would involve grave risk to life or grave injury (physical or mental) to pregnant woman; or substantial risk of serious physical or mental handicap in child
- § 5A — Confidentiality: identity of woman shall not be revealed except to persons authorised by law; violation punishable with up to one year imprisonment
- § 4 — Termination only at approved places (government hospitals or facilities certified under MTP Act)

### MTP Rules 2021 (Special Categories for 20–24 Weeks)
- Survivors of sexual assault or rape
- Minors
- Change of marital status (widowhood, divorce) during ongoing pregnancy
- Women with physical disabilities (≥40% disability per RPwD Act 2016)
- Mentally ill women
- Pregnancies with foetal malformations incompatible with life or likely to be seriously handicapped
- Women in humanitarian settings or disasters declared by government

## Key Provisions

### Gestational Limits Summary
| Gestational Age | Opinion Required | Who Can Seek |
|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Up to 20 weeks | 1 RMP | All women regardless of marital status |
| 20–24 weeks | 2 RMPs | Special categories (MTP Rules 2021) |
| Beyond 24 weeks | State/UT Medical Board | Substantial foetal abnormality only |

### 2021 Amendment: Key Changes
| Pre-2021 | Post-2021 |
|----------|-----------|
| Upper limit 20 weeks (general) | Upper limit extended to 24 weeks for special categories |
| Only "married woman" eligible under § 3(2)(b) | All women irrespective of marital status |
| No Medical Board mechanism | Medical Board for cases beyond 24 weeks |
| 12 weeks / 20 weeks two-tier | 20 weeks / 24 weeks two-tier |

## Landmark Cases
- **Suchita Srivastava v Chandigarh Administration (SC 2009):** Supreme Court held reproductive autonomy is a dimension of personal liberty under Article 21; consent of woman is paramount.
- **X v Principal Secretary, Health & Family Welfare, Govt of NCT of Delhi (SC 2022):** Unmarried women entitled to safe and legal abortion up to 24 weeks on par with married women; distinction based on marital status held unconstitutional.
