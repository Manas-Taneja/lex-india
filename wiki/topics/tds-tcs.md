# Tax Deducted at Source and Tax Collected at Source (TDS/TCS)

## Definition
TDS requires specified persons making certain payments to deduct income tax at source and remit it to the government, ensuring tax collection at the point of payment. TCS is the mirror mechanism where the seller collects tax from the buyer at the point of sale of specified goods or services.

## Current Law

### Income Tax Act 1961 — TDS Provisions (§§ 192–206AA)

- § 192 — TDS on salary: deducted by employer at average rate computed on estimated annual salary; slab rates apply; Form 16 issued to employee
- § 194A — TDS on interest (other than securities): 10%; threshold ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens) from banks
- § 194C — TDS on payments to contractors: 1% (individuals/HUF) or 2% (others); threshold ₹30,000 per contract / ₹1,00,000 aggregate per year
- § 194D — TDS on insurance commission: 5%; threshold ₹15,000
- § 194H — TDS on commission/brokerage: 5%; threshold ₹15,000
- § 194I — TDS on rent: 2% (plant/machinery) or 10% (land/building/furniture); threshold ₹2,40,000 per year
- § 194J — TDS on fees for professional or technical services: 10% (2% for technical services w.e.f. 2020); threshold ₹30,000
- § 194Q — TDS on purchase of goods (w.e.f. 1 July 2021): 0.1% where purchase from a single seller exceeds ₹50L in a year; applies to buyers with turnover > ₹10 Cr
- § 194R — TDS on benefits/perquisites (w.e.f. 1 July 2022): 10% on value of benefit/perquisite provided in course of business/profession; threshold ₹20,000
- § 203A — mandatory Tax Deduction Account Number (TAN) for all deductors; quoting TAN on challans, certificates, and returns is compulsory
- § 206AA — where deductee fails to furnish PAN, TDS at the higher of: prescribed rate, 20%, or rate in force

### Income Tax Act 1961 — TCS Provisions (§ 206C)

- § 206C(1) — TCS by seller on: scrap (1%), timber obtained under forest lease (2.5%), timber from other sources (2.5%), tendu leaves (5%), forest produce other than timber/tendu (2.5%), minerals (1%), bullion > ₹2L (1%)
- § 206C(1H) — TCS on sale of goods (w.e.f. 1 Oct 2020): 0.1% where aggregate sales to a buyer exceed ₹50L; seller's turnover > ₹10 Cr; not applicable if buyer is liable to deduct TDS under § 194Q
- § 206C(1G) — TCS on Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS): 20% (w.e.f. 1 Oct 2023) on remittances exceeding ₹7L per year; 5% for education/medical purposes financed by loan; remittances for education/medical from own funds at 5% up to ₹7L threshold

### Certificates and Relief
- § 197 — lower/nil deduction certificate: deductee may apply to Assessing Officer; AO may issue certificate permitting deduction at lower rate
- § 197A — self-declaration (Form 15G/15H): resident individuals/HUFs with income below taxable limit may submit Form 15G (general) or Form 15H (senior citizens) to deductor for nil deduction
- § 201 — consequences of default by deductor: liable as "assessee in default"; interest under § 201(1A): 1% per month for non-deduction, 1.5% per month for non-deposit after deduction

## Key Provisions

### Major TDS Rates at a Glance
| Section | Nature of Payment | Rate | Threshold |
|---------|------------------|------|-----------|
| 192 | Salary | Slab rate | Basic exemption limit |
| 194A | Interest (bank) | 10% | ₹40,000 / ₹50,000 (senior) |
| 194C | Contractor | 1% / 2% | ₹30,000 / ₹1,00,000 |
| 194H | Commission | 5% | ₹15,000 |
| 194I | Rent | 2% / 10% | ₹2,40,000 p.a. |
| 194J | Professional fees | 10% (2% technical) | ₹30,000 |
| 194Q | Purchase of goods | 0.1% | ₹50L per seller |
| 194R | Benefits/perquisites | 10% | ₹20,000 |

### Compliance and Reporting
- **TAN** — must be obtained under § 203A before deducting; quoted on all documents
- **Form 16** — TDS certificate for salary income; issued by employer annually
- **Form 16A** — TDS certificate for non-salary payments; issued quarterly
- **Form 27Q** — quarterly TDS return for payments to non-residents
- **TRACES portal** — TDS Reconciliation Analysis and Correction Enabling System; used for viewing 26AS, downloading certificates, rectification
- **26AS / AIS** — Annual Information Statement; reflects all TDS/TCS credits, high-value transactions, and other financial information linked to PAN
