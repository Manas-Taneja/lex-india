## Definition

Civil suit procedure in India is governed by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC) and the Commercial Courts Act, 2015 for high-value commercial disputes. A civil suit is commenced by filing a plaint and proceeds through written statement, issues, evidence, arguments, and decree. The CPC has not been replaced by a 2023 equivalent (unlike criminal law) and continues as amended.

## Current Law

**Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC)**

- **Order I** — Parties: joinder of plaintiffs and defendants; representative suits
- **Order II** — Frame of suit: every suit must include the whole of the claim; splitting of claims not permitted
- **Order IV** — Institution of suit: suit instituted by plaint filed in court with territorial and pecuniary jurisdiction
- **Order V** — Issue and service of summons: defendant must be served; service by post, electronic means, or personal service
- **Order VI** — Pleadings: plaint and written statement; facts only (not evidence); no departure from pleadings at trial without amendment
- **Order VII** — Plaint: must state cause of action, relief claimed, valuation for court fee
- **Order VIII** — Written statement: defendant's reply; must specifically deny each allegation; defences not raised deemed admitted
- **Order XI** — Discovery and inspection: parties may seek production of documents from each other before trial
- **Order XII** — Admissions: party may call on other to admit facts; admission conclusive
- **Order XIV** — Issues: court frames issues at the first hearing after written statement; issues of law and fact
- **Order XVII** — Adjournments: court may grant adjournment with costs; maximum 3 adjournments to each party *(inserted by 2002 Amendment)*
- **Order XVIII** — Hearing: evidence by affidavit (examination-in-chief); cross-examination oral; right to produce documents
- **Order XX** — Judgment and decree: judgment pronounced; decree follows; decree is the formal expression of the adjudication
- **Order XXI** — Execution of decrees: modes — attachment and sale of property, arrest and detention, garnishee orders, appointment of receiver
- **Order XXIII** — Withdrawal and compromise: suit may be withdrawn with or without liberty to re-file; compromise decree on agreement of parties
- **Order XXXVII** — Summary suits: suits on negotiable instruments and written contracts — defendant must obtain leave to defend; court can pass decree without full trial
- **§ 9** — Jurisdiction: Civil courts have jurisdiction over all civil matters except where expressly barred by statute
- **§ 96** — First appeal from original decree: lies to District Court (against Munsiff/Civil Judge decree) or High Court (against District Court decree)
- **§ 100** — Second appeal to High Court: only on substantial question of law
- **§ 115** — Revision by High Court: against orders of subordinate courts that have caused failure of justice

**Commercial Courts Act, 2015 (for commercial disputes ≥ ₹3 lakh):**
- Mandatory pre-institution mediation under MSMED Act before filing commercial suit
- Strict case management — no adjournments except for exceptional reasons
- 120-day limit for filing written statement (after which right to file forfeited)
- Trial completion target: 1 year

## Key Provisions

**Stages of a civil suit:**

| Stage | Key action |
|-------|-----------|
| Filing plaint | Court fee paid; jurisdiction determined; plaint registered |
| Summons | Defendant served; time to file written statement |
| Written statement | Defendant replies; counter-claim if any |
| Issues framed | Court identifies disputed questions |
| Discovery | Documents exchanged |
| Trial | Evidence by affidavit + cross-examination |
| Arguments | Written/oral submissions |
| Judgment | Pronounced; decree drawn up |
| Execution | Decree enforced |

**Court fee:** Calculated as percentage of claim value; payable on filing. Wrong valuation → plaint rejected.

**Res judicata (§ 11 CPC):** Once a matter is decided between the same parties by a competent court, it cannot be re-litigated. Constructive res judicata: every matter that ought to have been raised but was not raised is also barred.

**Stay of suit (§ 10 CPC):** If a previously instituted suit between the same parties on the same matter is pending, the later suit shall be stayed.

**Limitation (§ 3 Limitation Act + Schedule):** Suits must be filed within the limitation period — typically 3 years for money suits, 12 years for property suits. Court cannot extend limitation except as provided by Limitation Act.

**Temporary injunction (Order XXXIX):** Three conditions — prima facie case, balance of convenience, irreparable harm. Court grants or refuses at interlocutory stage.

**Order XXXVII (Summary suits):** Used for recovery on cheques, promissory notes, written contracts. Defendant must show triable issue to get leave to defend; court can pass judgment on summons.

**Pre-Institution Mediation (Commercial Courts Act, 2015):** Mandatory for commercial disputes before filing suit (except urgent interim relief cases). Failure to attempt mediation is a ground for dismissal at early stage.
