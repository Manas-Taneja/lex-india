## Definition

Theft is the dishonest taking of movable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent, with the intention of permanently depriving them of it. The key mental element is dishonest intent at the moment of taking.

## Current Law

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

- **§ 303** — Theft (punishment: up to 3 years imprisonment + fine)
- **§ 304** — Theft in dwelling house, or of means of conveyance (up to 7 years + fine)
- **§ 305** — Theft by clerk or servant of property in possession of master (up to 7 years + fine)
- **§ 306** — Theft after preparation for causing death/hurt to effect the theft (up to 10 years + fine)
- **§ 307** — Lurking house-trespass or house-breaking to commit theft (up to 14 years + fine)

## Key Provisions

**Elements of theft (§ 303):**
1. Dishonest intention
2. Moving of movable property
3. Out of possession of another person
4. Without consent

**Property must be movable** — land itself cannot be stolen, but crops severed from land can be.

**Consent vitiates theft** — even express or implied consent of the possessor means no theft.

**Aggravated forms:**
- Theft from dwelling: 7 years
- Theft with preparation to cause hurt or death: 10 years
- House-breaking at night to steal: 14 years
