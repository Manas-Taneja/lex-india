## Definition

Foreign exchange management in India is governed by the Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA), which replaced the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 (FERA). FEMA treats foreign exchange violations as civil offences (unlike FERA's criminal approach), with RBI as the primary regulatory authority. FEMA regulates cross-border transactions, foreign investment into India (FDI), Indian investment abroad (ODI), external commercial borrowings, and non-resident accounts. The Enforcement Directorate (ED) enforces FEMA.

## Current Law

**Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999**

- **§ 2(m)** — Foreign exchange: foreign currency; deposits, credits, balances payable in foreign currency; instruments drawn or expressed in foreign currency; drafts/letters of credit in foreign currency
- **§ 3** — Prohibited activities without general/special permission: dealing in foreign exchange except through authorised person; making payment to person outside India; receiving payment from outside India
- **§ 4** — Holding/owning/transferring/importing foreign exchange/securities: Indian resident cannot hold or own foreign exchange except as permitted by RBI
- **§ 5** — Current account transactions: free to transact (subject to RBI restrictions on certain transactions); RBI may restrict in public interest
- **§ 6** — Capital account transactions: regulated by RBI; any person may sell or draw foreign exchange for capital account transactions as permitted
- **§ 7** — Export of goods/services: must be reported to RBI; proceeds must be realised and repatriated within prescribed period (currently 9 months for goods, 15 months for software exports for startups)
- **§ 10** — Authorised persons: only authorised dealers (banks), money changers, and other RBI-authorised persons may deal in foreign exchange
- **§ 11** — RBI directions to authorised persons
- **§ 13** — Civil penalties for contravention: up to 3× the sum involved, or ₹2 lakh where sum not quantifiable; continuing contravention: ₹5,000/day
- **§ 15** — Compounding: person may compound the offence before adjudicating authority; available for most contraventions
- **§ 16** — Adjudicating authority appointed by Central Government
- **§ 17** — Appeals to Special Director (Appeals)
- **§ 19** — Appellate Tribunal for Foreign Exchange (ATFE) — hears further appeals
- **§ 37A** — Attachment and confiscation of assets: where assets are proceeds of foreign exchange contravention, Enforcement Directorate may attach; separate from PMLA proceedings

**Key RBI Regulations under FEMA:**

**FEMA (Transfer or Issue of Security by Person Resident Outside India) Rules, 2019 — FDI:**
- FDI permitted in most sectors automatically (automatic route) or with Government approval (approval route)
- Prohibited sectors: lottery, gambling, chit funds, nidhi companies, real estate business (not construction), tobacco
- Sectoral caps: insurance (74%), banking (74%), defence (74%), telecom (100% subject to conditions)
- FDI in e-commerce: 100% in marketplace model; prohibited in inventory-based model

**FEMA (Overseas Investment) Rules, 2022 — ODI:**
- Indian residents may invest abroad within RBI limits
- Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS): individuals may remit up to USD 250,000 per financial year for permitted capital and current account transactions

**External Commercial Borrowings (ECB):**
- Indian companies may borrow from foreign lenders under RBI's ECB framework
- Minimum average maturity, end-use restrictions (productive sectors; not real estate/capital markets)
- Automatic route (within track/limit) vs approval route

## Key Provisions

**Current vs capital account:**

| Account | Definition | Regulation |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Current account | Transactions not leading to change in assets/liabilities — trade, remittances, invisibles | Generally free; some RBI restrictions |
| Capital account | Transactions changing assets/liabilities — FDI, FPI, ECB, ODI | RBI permission required; sector-specific |

**FDI routes:**

| Route | How | Sectors |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Automatic | No prior approval needed; post-facto reporting to RBI | Most sectors |
| Government (approval) | Prior approval from FIPB/Ministry | Sensitive sectors |
| Prohibited | No FDI permitted | Lottery, weapons without licence, atomic energy |

**Non-resident accounts:**

| Account | Currency | Repatriation | Taxation |
|---------|----------|-------------|----------|
| NRE (Non-Resident External) | INR | Freely repatriable | Interest exempt from India tax |
| NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) | INR | Up to USD 1M/year | Interest taxable in India |
| FCNR(B) (Foreign Currency NR) | Foreign currency | Freely repatriable | Interest exempt from India tax |

**Enforcement:**
- FEMA contravention → ED investigation → adjudication order → civil penalty
- If contravention also involves money laundering → Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) applies separately
- FERA 1973 contraventions were criminal; FEMA 1999 treats as civil (except § 13(1A) — knowing/deliberate contravention → 5 years imprisonment)

**Repatriation requirement:** Export proceeds must be repatriated. Failure to repatriate is a FEMA violation regardless of commercial reasons. Exporter can apply to RBI for write-off of irrecoverable bad debts within prescribed limits.
