# Banking Regulation

## Definition
Banking regulation in India establishes the legal framework for licensing, supervision, and control of banks and financial institutions. The primary statute is the Banking Regulation Act 1949 (BRA), with the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) acting as the apex regulatory authority under the RBI Act 1934.

## Current Law

### Banking Regulation Act 1949 (BRA)

- § 5(b) — "banking": accepting deposits of money from the public for the purpose of lending or investment, repayable on demand
- § 5(c) — "banking company": any company that transacts the business of banking in India
- § 6 — forms of business permissible to banking companies (lending, foreign exchange, safe custody, etc.)
- § 11 — minimum paid-up capital and reserves: RBI may prescribe limits by notification
- § 22 — licensing: no company shall carry on banking business without a licence from RBI; RBI may cancel licence on specified grounds
- § 24 — Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR): every banking company must maintain a minimum proportion of its net demand and time liabilities (NDTL) in gold, cash, or approved securities; RBI prescribes the ratio
- § 35 — inspection of banking companies by RBI; RBI may direct special audit
- § 35A — power of RBI to give directions to banking companies in public interest or in the interest of depositors or banking policy
- § 36 — further powers: RBI may caution or prohibit banking companies; appoint additional directors
- § 45 — power of RBI to apply to Central Government for moratorium and scheme of amalgamation or reconstruction; moratorium suspends all legal proceedings against the bank

### Reserve Bank of India Act 1934

- § 17 — RBI's banking business functions: banker to government, banker to banks, lender of last resort
- § 18 — emergency purchase of bills of exchange in times of crisis
- § 42 — Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR): every scheduled bank must maintain a minimum balance (currently 4%) with RBI on its NDTL; RBI can vary between 3%–15%

### Prudential and Supervisory Frameworks

- Prompt Corrective Action (PCA): RBI's risk-based framework triggered when a bank breaches capital adequacy, asset quality, or leverage thresholds; restrictions imposed include dividend prohibition, branch expansion ban, limits on management compensation
- Basel III norms: implemented via RBI Master Circulars; prescribe Capital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio (CRAR) minimum 9% (Tier 1 + Tier 2)
- Know Your Customer (KYC) / Anti-Money Laundering (AML): Prevention of Money Laundering Act 2002 + RBI KYC Master Direction 2016

### Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation Act 1961 (DICGC)

- DICGC provides insurance on bank deposits up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank (increased from ₹1 lakh effective February 2021)
- Covers savings, fixed, current, and recurring deposits; does not cover deposits of state/central governments or foreign governments
- Insurance premium paid by member banks; depositors do not pay separately
- DICGC Amendment Act 2021: depositors must be paid insured amount within 90 days of moratorium/liquidation commencement

### SARFAESI Act 2002

- § 2(o) — "secured creditor": bank or financial institution holding security interest over assets of the borrower
- § 13 — enforcement of security interest without court intervention: secured creditor issues 60-day notice to borrower after account classified as NPA; if borrower fails to discharge, creditor may take possession or appoint manager
- § 14 — assistance of Chief Metropolitan Magistrate (CMM) or District Magistrate (DM) for taking physical possession
- § 17 — borrower may apply to DRT within 45 days of possession notice; DRT may restore possession if notice was not valid
- § 18 — appeal from DRT order to DRAT; pre-deposit of 50% of debt due required (may be waived up to 25%)

### IBC 2016 Interaction

- For banks as financial creditors: IBC §§ 7–12 — financial creditor may file for corporate insolvency resolution (CIRP) before NCLT when default exceeds ₹1 crore
- IBC takes precedence over SARFAESI once CIRP is admitted; moratorium under § 14 IBC stays all proceedings

## Key Provisions

### Regulatory Ratios

| Ratio | Governing Section | Current Rate (indicative) |
|---|---|---|
| Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR) | RBI Act § 42 | 4% of NDTL |
| Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR) | BRA § 24 | 18% of NDTL |
| Capital Adequacy Ratio (CRAR) | RBI Master Circular (Basel III) | Minimum 9% |
| Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) | RBI Circular | 100% |

### PCA Framework — Trigger Thresholds

| Indicator | Risk Threshold 1 | Risk Threshold 2 |
|---|---|---|
| CRAR | <10.25% | <7.75% |
| Net NPA ratio | >6% | >9% |
| Return on Assets (RoA) | Negative for 2 years | Negative for 3 years |

### Deposit Insurance Coverage (DICGC)

| Deposit Type | Insured | Limit per Bank |
|---|---|---|
| Savings account | Yes | ₹5 lakh |
| Fixed deposit | Yes | ₹5 lakh (aggregate) |
| Current account | Yes | ₹5 lakh (aggregate) |
| Government deposits | No | — |
| Inter-bank deposits | No | — |
