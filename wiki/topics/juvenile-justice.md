# Juvenile Justice

## Definition
The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act 2015 (JJ Act), along with its 2021 amendment, provides a comprehensive framework for the care, protection, treatment, development, and rehabilitation of children in conflict with the law and children in need of care and protection, replacing the Juvenile Justice Act 2000.

## Current Law

### Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act 2015
- § 2(12) — "Child": a person who has not completed eighteen years of age
- § 2(13) — "Child in conflict with law" (CCL): a child alleged or found to have committed an offence
- § 2(14) — "Child in need of care and protection" (CNCP): child who is abandoned, surrendered, found in public place without adult care, victim of abuse or exploitation, child of mentally ill or substance-abusing parent, child engaged in begging, etc.
- § 4 — Juvenile Justice Board (JJB): to be constituted in each district; consists of a Metropolitan Magistrate or Judicial Magistrate of First Class + 2 social workers (at least one woman)
- § 15 — Heinous offence (punishable with imprisonment ≥7 years): JJB to conduct preliminary assessment of a CCL aged 16–18 years to determine whether to try them as an adult before the Children's Court; psychological and social investigation reports mandatory
- § 18 — No child shall be sentenced to death or life imprisonment; maximum period of detention is 3 years in a special home (§ 18(3)); for heinous offences where JJB recommends Children's Court trial, child may be sentenced as adult but cannot receive death or life imprisonment if offence committed as child
- § 19 — Children's Court: designated special court for trial of CCL aged 16–18 referred by JJB for heinous offences
- § 24 — Record of conviction of a child in conflict with law to be expunged after the child attains 18 years and inquiry proceedings are completed
- §§ 27–30 — Child Welfare Committee (CWC): one in each district; jurisdiction over CNCP; 5 members including at least one woman and one child expert
- § 37 — Mandatory reporting: every person who has knowledge of a child being in need of care and protection has a duty to report to CWC or police
- § 44 — Foster care: placement of a CNCP with a family other than biological family; supervised by the State Government
- § 46 — After-care: support for children leaving institutional care after 18 years (up to 21 years) to enable rehabilitation and social integration
- §§ 56–73 — Adoption: only through Child Adoption Resource Agency (CARA) under the Central Adoption Resource Authority; JJ Act prevails over Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act 1956 for children in institutional care

### Juvenile Justice (Amendment) Act 2021
- District Magistrate (DM) / Additional District Magistrate (ADM) empowered to supervise and monitor functioning of JJB, CWC, and all child care institutions in the district
- DM to conduct quarterly visits to all child care institutions
- Adoption orders to be issued by DM (instead of courts) to expedite adoption process
- Strengthened accountability of child care institutions with enhanced inspection mechanisms

## Key Provisions

### Categories of Offences (JJ Act § 2)
| Category | Definition | Procedure |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Petty offence | Imprisonment up to 3 years | JJB; no referral to Children's Court |
| Serious offence | Imprisonment 3–7 years | JJB; no referral to Children's Court |
| Heinous offence | Imprisonment ≥7 years | JJB preliminary assessment; may refer 16–18 age group to Children's Court |

### Child in Need of Care and Protection — Categories (§ 2(14))
| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Abandoned / surrendered | Left by parents or guardians; surrendered owing to socio-economic reasons |
| Orphan | No surviving parent or legal guardian |
| Victim of abuse | Subjected to physical, sexual, mental abuse or exploitation |
| Child labourer / beggar | Employed as child labour or engaged in begging |
| At risk | Lives with persons involved in crime or substance abuse |
| Missing / runaway | Reported missing or run away from home |
| Refugee / disaster | Separated from family during disaster or conflict |

### Institutional Framework
| Institution | Managed By | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Observation Home (§ 47) | State Govt | Temporary reception of CCL pending JJB inquiry |
| Special Home (§ 48) | State Govt | Rehabilitation of CCL ordered by JJB |
| Place of Safety (§ 49) | State Govt | CCL aged 16–18 awaiting Children's Court trial |
| Open Shelter (§ 43) | State Govt / NGO | Day-care and drop-in for CNCP at risk |
| Child Care Institution (§ 50) | State Govt / NGO | Long-term care of CNCP including fit facilities |
| Specialised Adoption Agency | State Govt / NGO | Pre-adoption foster care and adoption placement |

### Interaction with POCSO Act 2012
- Where a CCL is also a victim of sexual offence under POCSO, the child is simultaneously treated as CNCP before CWC and as CCL before JJB
- POCSO Special Court handles the trial of the accused; CWC provides support and rehabilitation measures to the child victim
- Compensation under § 357A CrPC (victim compensation) and POCSO (interim relief) available
