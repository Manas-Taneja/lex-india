# Minimum Wages

## Definition
Minimum wages are the lowest rates of remuneration that employers are legally required to pay workers in scheduled employments, fixed by central or state governments under the Minimum Wages Act 1948 and proposed to be unified under the Code on Wages 2019.

## Current Law

### Minimum Wages Act 1948

- § 2(h) — "scheduled employment": employment listed in the Schedule to the Act; states may add employments
- § 3 — fixing of minimum wages: appropriate government shall fix, review, and revise minimum wages at intervals not exceeding 5 years
- § 4 — components: basic rate of wages + cash value of concessions, or an all-inclusive rate
- § 5 — procedure: committee method or notification method for wage fixation
- § 12 — payment of minimum rates: employer must pay not less than minimum rate; any contract paying less is void
- § 14 — overtime: worker entitled to overtime wages = 2× ordinary rate of wages for hours worked beyond normal working hours
- § 20 — authority for hearing claims: appointed authority may direct payment of difference plus compensation up to 10× the difference
- § 22 — penalties: imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fine up to ₹500 for non-compliance (pre-Code regime)

### Code on Wages 2019

- Consolidates: Minimum Wages Act 1948 + Payment of Wages Act 1936 + Equal Remuneration Act 1976 + Payment of Bonus Act 1965
- § 6 — floor wage: central government to fix a national floor wage; no state may fix minimum wage below the floor wage
- § 8 — advisory board at central level; state advisory boards under § 42
- § 9 — revision every 5 years
- § 43 — overtime = 2× minimum rate
- Notified in 2019; rules drafted; full implementation pending state adoption

## Key Provisions

### Wage Structure Under MWA 1948

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Basic rate | Fixed minimum; varies by scheduled employment and skill level |
| Variable DA | Linked to Consumer Price Index; revised periodically |
| All-inclusive rate | Alternative: single rate subsuming all components |
| Overtime rate | 2× minimum wages for overtime hours |

### Central vs State Sphere

| Sphere | Governs |
|--------|---------|
| Central sphere | Mines, oilfields, railways, air transport, major ports, banking |
| State sphere | All other scheduled employments within the state |

### Floor Wage Concept (Code on Wages 2019)

- National floor wage ensures a minimum nationwide standard below which no state may go
- Geographical regions permitted: different floor rates for different regions
- Existing minimum wages above the floor are unaffected
- Enforces upward harmonisation without overriding state-specific rates
