# India Laws — Topic Index

> Entry point for LLM agents. Read this file first, then fetch the relevant topic page.
> All topic pages are self-contained — no cross-file lookups needed to answer a query.

## Constitutional Law
- [fundamental-rights](topics/fundamental-rights.md) — Fundamental Rights are a set of basic rights guaranteed to all citizens (and in some cases all persons) of India by Part III of the Constitution of India (Articles 12–35).
- [directive-principles](topics/directive-principles.md) — Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) are non-justiciable guidelines in Part IV (Articles 36–51) of the Indian Constitution that direct the State in governance and law-making.
- [constitutional-amendments](topics/constitutional-amendments.md) — Constitutional amendments are formal changes to the text or interpretation of the Indian Constitution, governed by Article 368.
- [emergency-provisions](topics/emergency-provisions.md) — Emergency provisions in Part XVIII of the Indian Constitution (Articles 352–360) allow the Central Government to assume extraordinary powers during threats to national security, constitutional breakdown in a State, or financial instability.
- [panchayati-raj](topics/panchayati-raj.md) — Panchayati Raj is India's system of local self-government in rural areas, given constitutional status by the 73rd Amendment (1992), which inserted Part IX (Articles 243–243O) into the Constitution.

## Criminal Law
- [murder](topics/murder.md) — Murder is the unlawful killing of a person with the intention to cause death, or with knowledge that the act is so imminently dangerous that it will in all probability cause death.
- [theft](topics/theft.md) — Theft is the dishonest taking of movable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent, with the intention of permanently depriving them of it.
- [rape](topics/rape.md) — Rape is a non-consensual sexual act committed by a man upon a woman.
- [cheating](topics/cheating.md) — Cheating is the act of deceiving a person fraudulently or dishonestly, inducing them to deliver property or to do or omit something they would not do if not deceived, causing damage or harm to their body, mind, reputation, or property.
- [defamation](topics/defamation.md) — Defamation is the making or publishing of an imputation about a person, intended to harm that person's reputation, or with knowledge or reason to believe it will harm their reputation.
- [assault](topics/assault.md) — Assault is the act of making a gesture or preparation that causes another person to apprehend that criminal force is about to be used against them.
- [kidnapping](topics/kidnapping.md) — Kidnapping is the taking or enticing away of a person from their lawful guardianship or from India without consent.
- [dacoity](topics/dacoity.md) — Dacoity is the commission of robbery by five or more persons acting conjointly.
- [criminal-conspiracy](topics/criminal-conspiracy.md) — Criminal conspiracy is an agreement between two or more persons to do, or cause to be done, an illegal act or a legal act by illegal means.
- [drug-offences](topics/drug-offences.md) — Drug offences in India are primarily governed by the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 (NDPS Act), which prohibits manufacture, possession, sale, purchase, transport, warehousing, import/export, and use of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances.
- [corruption](topics/corruption.md) — Corruption offences in India are primarily governed by the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 (PC Act), amended significantly in 2018.
- [money-laundering](topics/money-laundering.md) — Money laundering is the process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained proceeds (proceeds of crime) by passing them through a complex sequence of banking transfers, shell companies, or other transactions to make them appear legitimate.
- [child-sexual-abuse](topics/child-sexual-abuse.md) — Child sexual abuse offences in India are primarily governed by the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO Act), which defines and punishes sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography involving children (persons under 18 years).
- [domestic-violence](topics/domestic-violence.md) — Domestic violence under Indian law encompasses physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse inflicted on a woman by her husband, male partner, or male relatives in a shared household.
- [cybercrime](topics/cybercrime.md) — Cybercrime encompasses offences committed using computers, networks, or digital devices — including hacking, data theft, online fraud, identity theft, cyberstalking, publishing obscene material online, and ransomware attacks.
- [atrocities-against-sc-st](topics/atrocities-against-sc-st.md) — Atrocities against Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes are offences committed by non-SC/ST persons against members of SC/ST communities on the basis of their caste identity.
- [terror-offences](topics/terror-offences.md) — Terror offences in India are primarily governed by the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 (UAPA), as amended in 2004, 2008, 2012, and 2019.
- [sexual-harassment-at-work](topics/sexual-harassment-at-work.md) — Sexual harassment at the workplace is any unwelcome act or behaviour — physical contact, demand for sexual favours, sexually coloured remarks, showing pornography, or any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature.

## Procedure
- [bail](topics/bail.md) — Bail is the temporary release of an accused person from custody, upon giving security that they will appear before the court when required.
- [fir](topics/fir.md) — A First Information Report (FIR) is the written document prepared by the police when they receive information about the commission of a cognizable offence.
- [trial-procedure](topics/trial-procedure.md) — Trial procedure in India governs the stages from cognizance of an offence to final judgment in criminal cases.
- [evidence-rules](topics/evidence-rules.md) — Evidence law governs what facts a court may receive, how they are proved, and the weight to be given to different modes of proof.
- [civil-suit-procedure](topics/civil-suit-procedure.md) — Civil suit procedure in India is governed by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC) and the Commercial Courts Act, 2015 for high-value commercial disputes.
- [appeal-and-revision](topics/appeal-and-revision.md) — Appeal and revision are the primary mechanisms for challenging court decisions in India.
- [limitation-period](topics/limitation-period.md) — A limitation period is the maximum time after an event within which a party must file a legal action.
- [arbitration](topics/arbitration.md) — Arbitration is a private, binding dispute resolution mechanism where parties agree to have their dispute decided by one or more arbitrators instead of a court.

## Civil Law
- [specific-performance](topics/specific-performance.md) — Specific performance is an equitable remedy that compels a party to perform a contract as agreed, rather than merely paying damages.
- [injunction](topics/injunction.md) — An injunction is a court order directing a party to do (mandatory injunction) or refrain from doing (prohibitory injunction) a specific act.
- [tort-negligence](topics/tort-negligence.md) — A tort is a civil wrong (other than breach of contract) for which a court provides a remedy in the form of damages.
- [consumer-rights](topics/consumer-rights.md) — Consumer rights in India are governed by the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (CPA 2019), which replaced the Consumer Protection Act, 1986.
- [right-to-information](topics/right-to-information.md) — The Right to Information Act, 2005 (RTI Act) gives every citizen the right to access information held by public authorities.

## Commercial Law
- [contract](topics/contract.md) — A contract is a legally enforceable agreement.
- [company-incorporation](topics/company-incorporation.md) — Company incorporation is the legal process of forming a company as a separate legal entity under the Companies Act, 2013.
- [partnership](topics/partnership.md) — A partnership is a relation between persons who have agreed to share the profits of a business carried on by all or any of them acting for all.
- [sale-of-goods](topics/sale-of-goods.md) — The Sale of Goods Act, 1930 (SGA) governs contracts for the sale of movable goods in India.
- [negotiable-instruments](topics/negotiable-instruments.md) — A negotiable instrument is a written document that entitles its holder to a sum of money, which is transferable by endorsement and delivery (or delivery alone if bearer), so that a bona fide transferee for value without notice of defect (holder in due course) takes free from defects in the transferor's title.
- [insolvency](topics/insolvency.md) — The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 (IBC) is the primary law governing insolvency resolution and bankruptcy in India.
- [competition-law](topics/competition-law.md) — Competition law in India is governed by the Competition Act, 2002 (as amended 2023), administered by the Competition Commission of India (CCI).
- [securities-regulation](topics/securities-regulation.md) — Securities regulation in India is governed by the Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992 (SEBI Act), the Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 (SCRA), and the Depositories Act, 1996.
- [foreign-exchange](topics/foreign-exchange.md) — Foreign exchange management in India is governed by the Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA), which replaced the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 (FERA).
- [insurance](topics/insurance.md) — Insurance law in India is governed primarily by the Insurance Act, 1938 (as substantially amended), the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India Act, 1999 (IRDAI Act), and sector-specific statutes for motor (Motor Vehicles Act, 1988) and life/health insurance.
- [real-estate-rera](topics/real-estate-rera.md) — The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) regulates the real estate sector to protect homebuyers and promote transparency and accountability.

## Labour Law
- [employment-termination](topics/employment-termination.md) — Employment termination under Indian labour law covers the rights and procedures governing retrenchment, layoff, closure, and dismissal of workmen, with the Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (IDA) as the primary statute providing job security protections and mandatory compensatory obligations.
- [minimum-wages](topics/minimum-wages.md) — Minimum wages are the lowest rates of remuneration that employers are legally required to pay workers in scheduled employments, fixed by central or state governments under the Minimum Wages Act 1948 and proposed to be unified under the Code on Wages 2019.
- [provident-fund](topics/provident-fund.md) — The Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) is a mandatory retirement savings scheme administered by the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) under the EPF & MP Act 1952, requiring joint contributions from employees and employers to build a corpus for retirement, disability, and death benefits.
- [maternity-benefit](topics/maternity-benefit.md) — The Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (MBA) protects the employment of women during maternity and entitles them to fully paid leave, nursing breaks, crèche facilities, and protection from dismissal, with the 2017 amendment significantly expanding these rights.
- [factory-safety](topics/factory-safety.md) — The Factories Act 1948 regulates health, safety, and welfare conditions in factories, setting mandatory standards for working hours, leave entitlements, machinery safety, and worker amenities to protect the physical well-being of industrial workers.
- [child-labour](topics/child-labour.md) — Indian law completely prohibits the employment of children below 14 years in any occupation or process, and restricts adolescents (14–18 years) from hazardous occupations and processes, with the Child and Adolescent Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act 1986 as amended in 2016 forming the core framework.
- [contract-labour](topics/contract-labour.md) — Contract labour refers to workmen supplied by a contractor to a principal employer; the Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970 (CLRA) regulates this arrangement by requiring registration and licensing, imposing welfare obligations, and empowering governments to prohibit contract labour in perennial or core activities.
- [gratuity](topics/gratuity.md) — Gratuity is a statutory lump-sum payment made by an employer to an employee as a retirement or terminal benefit in recognition of past service, governed by the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972 (PGA), and payable on superannuation, retirement, resignation, death, or disablement after a minimum qualifying period of continuous service.

## Tax Law
- [income-tax-return](topics/income-tax-return.md) — An Income Tax Return (ITR) is the form filed by a taxpayer with the Income Tax Department declaring total income earned during a financial year, deductions claimed, taxes paid, and taxes owed or refundable.
- [gst](topics/gst.md) — GST is a unified, destination-based indirect tax on the supply of goods and services, replacing a patchwork of central and state levies.
- [customs-duty](topics/customs-duty.md) — Customs duty is a tax levied by the Central Government on goods imported into or exported from India.
- [tds-tcs](topics/tds-tcs.md) — TDS requires specified persons making certain payments to deduct income tax at source and remit it to the government, ensuring tax collection at the point of payment.
- [capital-gains-tax](topics/capital-gains-tax.md) — Capital gains tax is levied under the Income Tax Act 1961 on profits arising from the transfer of a capital asset.

## Family Law
- [divorce](topics/divorce.md) — Divorce is the legal dissolution of a valid marriage by a court.
- [marriage-registration](topics/marriage-registration.md) — Marriage registration is the official recording of a marriage with a government authority, producing a marriage certificate.
- [inheritance-succession](topics/inheritance-succession.md) — Succession is the transmission of property rights from a deceased person (de cujus) to their heirs or legatees, either by operation of law (intestate) or by a valid will (testamentary).
- [adoption](topics/adoption.md) — Adoption is the legal process by which a person takes another person's child as their own, creating the same rights and obligations as a biological parent-child relationship.
- [maintenance-alimony](topics/maintenance-alimony.md) — Maintenance is the legal obligation of one person to provide financial support to another who is unable to support themselves.
- [child-custody](topics/child-custody.md) — Child custody determines who has the legal right and responsibility to care for a minor child.
- [muslim-personal-law](topics/muslim-personal-law.md) — Muslim personal law governs the family and succession affairs of Muslims in India primarily through classical Islamic jurisprudence (fiqh), as applied by the Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act 1937.
- [child-marriage](topics/child-marriage.md) — Child marriage is the marriage of a person below the legally prescribed minimum age: 21 years for males and 18 years for females under the Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2006.

## Property Law
- [property-transfer](topics/property-transfer.md) — Transfer of property is the act by which a living person conveys property, in present or in future, to one or more living persons or to himself and one or more other living persons (§ 5, TPA 1882).
- [property-registration](topics/property-registration.md) — Registration is the process by which documents relating to immovable property are formally recorded with a government officer, conferring notice of the transaction to the world and giving the document legal enforceability.
- [land-acquisition](topics/land-acquisition.md) — Land acquisition is the compulsory acquisition of private land by the state for a public purpose, in exchange for compensation.
- [stamp-duty](topics/stamp-duty.md) — Stamp duty is a tax levied on instruments (written documents) recording transactions.

## Environmental Law
- [environmental-clearance](topics/environmental-clearance.md) — Environmental Clearance (EC) is a mandatory prior approval required before starting any project or activity listed under the EIA Notification 2006, granted by MoEFCC (Category A) or the State-level SEIAA (Category B) after an appraisal process that includes public consultation.
- [forest-rights](topics/forest-rights.md) — The Forest Rights Act 2006 (FRA) recognises and vests forest rights in Scheduled Tribes (STs) and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (OTFDs) who have been residing in forests for generations, correcting the historical injustice of treating them as encroachers on their own ancestral lands.
- [wildlife-protection](topics/wildlife-protection.md) — The Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 (WPA) provides a comprehensive framework for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants by prohibiting hunting, regulating trade, and establishing a network of protected areas including sanctuaries, national parks, and tiger reserves across India.
- [pollution-control](topics/pollution-control.md) — India's pollution control framework rests on three pillar statutes — the Water Act 1974, the Air Act 1981, and the Environment (Protection) Act 1986 — enforced by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) and State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs), supplemented by the NGT's supervisory jurisdiction.

## Intellectual Property
- [copyright](topics/copyright.md) — Copyright is the exclusive legal right granted to the creator of an original work to control its reproduction, distribution, adaptation, and public communication.
- [patents](topics/patents.md) — A patent is a statutory monopoly granted to an inventor for a limited term in exchange for full public disclosure of the invention.
- [trademarks](topics/trademarks.md) — A trademark is a sign capable of distinguishing the goods or services of one undertaking from those of others.

## Banking & Finance
- [banking-regulation](topics/banking-regulation.md) — Banking regulation in India establishes the legal framework for licensing, supervision, and control of banks and financial institutions.
- [loan-recovery](topics/loan-recovery.md) — Loan recovery law governs the mechanisms by which banks and financial institutions can recover outstanding dues from defaulting borrowers.

## Healthcare Law
- [abortion-rights](topics/abortion-rights.md) — The Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act 1971 (MTP Act), as amended in 2021, governs the conditions under which a registered medical practitioner may lawfully terminate a pregnancy in India, balancing a woman's reproductive autonomy with public health safeguards.
- [mental-health-law](topics/mental-health-law.md) — The Mental Healthcare Act 2017 (MHA 2017) replaced the Mental Health Act 1987 and establishes a rights-based framework for the care, treatment, and protection of persons with mental illness, aligning Indian law with the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).
- [disability-rights](topics/disability-rights.md) — The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 (RPwD Act) replaced the Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act 1995 and expanded the list of recognised disabilities from 7 to 21, bringing Indian law into conformity with the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).

## Education Law
- [right-to-education](topics/right-to-education.md) — The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009 (RTE Act) operationalises Article 21A of the Constitution, which was inserted by the 86th Constitutional Amendment in 2002, making free and compulsory education a fundamental right for every child aged 6 to 14 years.

## Social Law
- [juvenile-justice](topics/juvenile-justice.md) — The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act 2015 (JJ Act), along with its 2021 amendment, provides a comprehensive framework for the care, protection, treatment, development, and rehabilitation of children in conflict with the law and children in need of care and protection, replacing the Juvenile Justice Act 2000.
